# **2\. Source Framework: The Law of Resonance**

SYU JIA WUN defines resonance as the amplification or attenuation of systemic responses when external stimuli align with a system’s intrinsic frequencies. The paper identifies four essential components of any resonance event:

1. Intrinsic Property of the System — the system’s resonant signature, its preferred oscillation or configurational bias.

2. External Stimulus or Driver — an input (energy, information, force) applied with periodicity or structure matching the system’s intrinsic property.

3. Coupling Medium or Interface — the transmission channel through which input reaches the system.

4. Time and Accumulation — resonance requires repetition or duration; temporal alignment builds amplitude.

The resonance condition is formalized as:

**f\_in ≈ f\_nat AND C \> C\_min**

Where f\_in is the input frequency, f\_nat is the system’s natural frequency, C is coupling strength, and C\_min is the minimum threshold for energy or information transfer. Resonance is conditional: it requires alignment combined with sufficient connectivity and time.

The paper traces this principle across mechanical systems, acoustic and electromagnetic phenomena, quantum state transitions, orbital mechanics, neural oscillations, circadian biology, emotional contagion, epidemiological dynamics, semantic propagation, and civilizational phase transitions. The central thesis is that resonance is substrate-independent—defined not by what is resonating but by how structure and influence align.

# **3\. The NAC Isomorphism**

The foundational equation of New Age Cybernetics is:

**C \= R × P / M**

Where C is Cybernetics (system output), R is Resource (available energy, material, or information), P is Purpose (intrinsic directional alignment), and M is Method (the coupling mechanism through which resource is applied to purpose).

The structural correspondence to Syu’s resonance condition is direct:

| Syu’s Component | Symbol | NAC Equivalent | Operator Role |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Intrinsic frequency | f\_nat | **Purpose (P)** | What the system is tuned to |
| External stimulus | f\_in | **Resource (R)** | What the system receives |
| Coupling strength | C | **Method (M)** | How transfer occurs |
| Resonance output | — | **Cybernetics (C)** | C \= R × P / M |

The resonance condition f\_in ≈ f\_nat translates to: cybernetic output maximizes when Resource aligns with Purpose. The coupling threshold C \> C\_min translates to: Method must be sufficient but not parasitic. When M grows excessively—through bureaucratic friction, noise, corruption, or semantic parasitism—it overwhelms R × P, and the system transitions from constructive resonance to destructive resonance. This is the Great Filter expressed as a cybernetic inequality.
