## **1.1 Silica Source Materials: Alternative Feedstock Clause**

The canonical GSSG specification identifies sand (SiO₂) as the structural silica input. Sand is named as proof of the Pillar Test abundance criterion: “Sand is abundant everywhere.” This clause extends that criterion to the full family of silica-bearing source materials that may substitute for processed sand at any point in the VLSA scale stack — from THOW fabrication through Earth Flip megastructure construction through generation-ship in-situ resource utilization.

The operative principle: the silica function in GSSG is substrate provision — nucleation sites for graphene lattice formation, structural backbone, adhesion surface. Any material that provides sufficient silica in a form compatible with the pyrolysis pathway qualifies as a GSSG feedstock under this clause. The canonical name Green Solar-Sand Glass preserves ‘sand’ as the primary and most abundant source. It does not exclude equivalent silica-bearing materials where sand is unavailable, depleted, or suboptimal for local conditions.

# **2\. The Sugar-Carbon Reservoir Hypothesis**

The self-healing property of GSSG is explained by the Sugar-Carbon Reservoir Hypothesis, first articulated in ERES TERMS ·47 and ratified under DPR-001. The hypothesis states: when GSSG is manufactured, not all sugar-carbon is fully pyrolyzed into graphene. A fraction remains as distributed, unpyrolyzed sugar-carbon throughout the silica matrix. This reservoir is latent repair substrate.

| Step | Event | Mechanism |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| 1 | Microfracture occurs | Mechanical stress creates crack tip in the graphene-on-silica matrix |
| 2 | Local heating | Friction at crack tip raises local temperature |
| 3 | Reservoir activation | Heat triggers pyrolysis of reserved sugar-carbon at the fracture site |
| 4 | Carbon release | Carbon atoms become mobile at the crack interface |
| 5 | Lattice repair | Mobile carbon reforms graphene bonds across the fracture |
| 6 | Sealing | Crack is closed without external intervention, without replacement material, without energy input beyond the fracture event itself |

Falsifiability (from the canonical GSSG whitepaper): “The hypothesis is falsifiable by demonstrating that GSSG samples with artificially depleted sugar-carbon reservoirs (via extended over-pyrolysis) do not exhibit self-healing under identical fracture conditions.” Test 1 is the first adversarial pass against this hypothesis.

# **5\. Test 1 Specification (Canonical)**

The following is reproduced verbatim from ERES-THREE-FALSIFICATION-TESTS (JAS, 2026, CCAL v2.1):

"One independent laboratory, not affiliated with the ERES Institute, replicates the GSSG pyrolysis protocol end-to-end from published parameters and subjects the resulting specimen to a pre-registered fracture-cycling regimen — minimum fifty controlled microcrack-then-anneal cycles, with load and thermal envelopes fixed in advance. The success criterion is Raman-spectroscopic confirmation of graphene reformation across cycles: the D/G band-intensity ratio must return to within a pre-registered tolerance of the pre-fracture baseline after each anneal, and the 2D-band must remain consistent with multilayer graphene structure. Falsification: if D/G drift exceeds tolerance over three consecutive cycles, or if the 2D-band collapses, the self-healing claim is withdrawn at its current strength and the protocol revised. The cost envelope to reach a definitive yes/no on this test is modest by materials-science standards — a single instrument-week on a calibrated Raman setup plus the cycling rig — and the result is a paper either way."

Source: ERES-THREE-FALSIFICATION-TESTS, JAS, 2026, CCAL v2.1. Nothing in this scope specification enlarges or supersedes the pre-registered criteria set out in that document.
