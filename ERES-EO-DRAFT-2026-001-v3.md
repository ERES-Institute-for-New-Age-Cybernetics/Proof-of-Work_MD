Sign an Executive Order establishing the **Office of Family Amity** within the Executive Office of the President and initiating three immediate actions using existing authority and resources:

1. **Launch a 24/7 National Family Support Hotline** (“1-800-RESONATE”) utilizing existing Federal and State partner capacity, providing anonymous conflict de-escalation and family mediation referral support.  
2. **Authorize a 12-month pilot program** in Northwest Arkansas with measurable outcomes (see Section III).  
3. **Direct the submission of a legislative blueprint** to Congress within 90 days for a Cabinet-level Department of Family Amity.

This is not a permanent new bureaucracy by Executive Order alone.  
It is an operational modernization directive that begins immediately and scales lawfully.

---

## **II. WHAT THIS DOES**

### **Office of Family Amity (OFA)**

Creates a central coordinating office that evaluates federal policy by a single standard:

**The Hundred-Year Question:**  
*Does this strengthen the American family over the next century?*

OFA coordinates fragmented family-related programs across HHS, Education, HUD, VA, and Justice.

---

### **Patriot Dividend Program**

Creates a **temporary emergency economic stabilization mechanism** triggered only during formally declared emergencies.

The Patriot Dividend is structured as:

* time-bound relief,  
* paired with voluntary civic reinvestment incentives,  
* designed to become a long-term infrastructure bridge rather than a permanent dependency program.

---

### **Civic Infrastructure Modernization Initiative**

Directs the federal government to modernize:

* identity verification,  
* voluntary civic participation systems,  
* transparent credential tracking,  
* open-audit public governance recording systems.

This initiative does not mandate a single technology vendor, chain, or token system.  
It establishes national standards and a pilot deployment architecture.

# **TIER 2 — EXECUTIVE ORDER (SIGNABLE VERSION)**

## **EXECUTIVE ORDER**

### **ESTABLISHING THE OFFICE OF FAMILY AMITY, THE PATRIOT DIVIDEND PROGRAM, AND A NATIONAL CIVIC INFRASTRUCTURE MODERNIZATION INITIATIVE**

By the authority vested in me as President by the Constitution and the laws of the United States of America, including Article II of the Constitution, the National Emergencies Act (50 U.S.C. 1601 et seq.), the Economy Act (31 U.S.C. 1535), and Title 5 of the United States Code, it is hereby ordered as follows:

---

## **Section 1\. Purpose and Policy.**

The American family is the foundational unit of national strength. Yet Federal family-related programs remain fragmented across agencies without unified standards of measurement, accountability, or long-term outcomes.

It is the policy of the United States to strengthen family stability, reduce avoidable social harm, improve national resilience, and modernize civic infrastructure through lawful, transparent, voluntary, and measurable initiatives.

---

## **Section 2\. Establishment of the Office of Family Amity.**

(a) There is hereby established within the Executive Office of the President the **Office of Family Amity (OFA)**.

(b) The OFA shall serve as the principal coordinating body for Federal programs affecting family wellbeing, intergenerational continuity, and national social stability.

(c) The President shall appoint a Director of the OFA.

(d) The OFA shall coordinate with relevant Federal departments and agencies, including the Departments of Health and Human Services, Education, Housing and Urban Development, Justice, Veterans Affairs, Labor, and Homeland Security.

---

## **Section 3\. Mission of the Office of Family Amity.**

The OFA shall:

(a) Develop a unified Federal strategy to strengthen family stability and reduce preventable family-related harm;

(b) Evaluate relevant Federal policies using a long-term national interest standard, including the following guiding question:

**Does this policy strengthen the American family over the next century?**

(c) Identify program duplication, service gaps, and inefficiencies across agencies;

(d) Establish a Federal coordination framework to support States, counties, and local communities in voluntary family-strengthening initiatives.

---

## **Section 4\. National Family Support Hotline.**

(a) The OFA shall establish a national family support hotline, which may be publicly branded as **“1-800-RESONATE”**, providing voluntary, anonymous support and referral resources.

(b) Services shall include conflict de-escalation guidance, mediation referrals, crisis support routing, and lawful connections to existing resources.

(c) Hotline operations shall be conducted through existing Federal and State partner infrastructure to the maximum extent feasible.

(d) The hotline shall not be construed as providing medical diagnosis or replacing licensed clinical services, but shall operate as a structured mediation and referral system.

---

## **Section 5\. Pilot Program Authorization.**

(a) The OFA shall coordinate a 12-month pilot program beginning in Northwest Arkansas or another location designated by the Director, with measurable success criteria.

(b) Pilot metrics shall include, to the extent permitted by law:

* reductions in family-related emergency interventions,  
* increased voluntary mediation utilization,  
* improved service coordination outcomes,  
* improved long-term wellbeing indicators.

(c) The Director shall publish quarterly progress summaries.

---

## **Section 6\. Legislative Proposal Requirement.**

Within 90 days of this Order, the Director of the OFA, in coordination with the Office of Management and Budget and the White House Counsel, shall submit to the President a legislative proposal to establish a Cabinet-level Department of Family Amity.

---

## **Section 7\. Establishment of the Patriot Dividend Program (Emergency Stabilization).**

(a) There is hereby established a national policy framework known as the **Patriot Dividend Program**, intended as a temporary emergency stabilization mechanism during declared national emergencies.

(b) The Patriot Dividend Program shall be designed to:

* provide temporary relief during crisis conditions,  
* strengthen household resilience,  
* and accelerate long-term infrastructure modernization.

(c) The Secretary of the Treasury, in coordination with the Director of the OFA, shall develop a program plan including:

* eligibility criteria,  
* distribution mechanisms,  
* fraud prevention standards,  
* and termination/sunset conditions.

(d) The Patriot Dividend Program shall not be implemented except consistent with applicable law and subject to the availability of appropriations.

---

## **Section 8\. Voluntary Civic Reinforcement Mechanism.**

(a) The OFA and the Secretary of the Treasury shall develop a voluntary mechanism by which citizens may elect to return a portion of dividend funds, if received, in exchange for enhanced non-transferable civic credits applicable to lawful public purposes.

(b) Participation shall be strictly voluntary. No citizen shall be penalized for non-participation.

(c) The program shall be structured to encourage reinvestment in education, housing stability, healthcare resilience, skills training, and lawful public benefit contributions.

---

## **Section 9\. National Identity Modernization Initiative.**

(a) The OFA, in coordination with the Department of Homeland Security and the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), shall develop a modernization plan for Federal identity verification systems to reduce fraud, resist advanced spoofing threats, and improve citizen control of identity credentials.

(b) The modernization plan shall include:

* privacy-by-design principles,  
* consent-based access controls,  
* open-audit standards,  
* and robust authentication requirements.

(c) The plan shall provide multiple accessibility pathways to ensure no lawful beneficiary is excluded due to disability, technical limitation, or inability to provide a specific biometric factor.

(d) Within 180 days, NIST shall publish recommended standards or guidance for next-generation authentication integration.

---

## **Section 10\. Civic Credential and Contribution Recording Pilot.**

(a) The OFA shall coordinate with appropriate agencies to establish a pilot system for voluntary credential verification and civic contribution recording, emphasizing transparency, auditability, and privacy protection.

(b) The pilot may include distributed ledger technologies or equivalent tamper-resistant systems, provided that:

* no proprietary black-box governance algorithm is mandated,  
* audit capability is preserved,  
* citizen consent is required.

(c) This section shall not be construed to authorize the creation of a national mandatory social credit system.

---

## **Section 11\. Voluntary Electronic Civic Service (ECS).**

(a) The OFA shall establish a voluntary program known as **Electronic Civic Service (ECS)** enabling Americans to contribute to public benefit projects through digital-first participation.

(b) ECS participation shall not be mandatory, and shall not replace military service or existing civil service programs.

(c) ECS shall provide optional pathways for training, credential recognition, and public contribution certification.

---

## **Section 12\. Development of Family and National Wellbeing Metrics.**

(a) The OFA shall develop a supplementary measurement framework to evaluate the effectiveness of family-strengthening policy.

(b) This framework may incorporate measurable indicators of:

* family stability,  
* community resilience,  
* intergenerational continuity,  
* economic participation integrity,  
* and ecological sustainability.

(c) The OFA shall publish an annual report comparing these metrics against traditional economic indicators.

---

## **Section 13\. Implementation and Timeline.**

(a) Within 60 days:

* the OFA shall be operational,  
* the hotline shall be established,  
* and the pilot plan shall be finalized.

(b) Within 180 days:

* identity modernization recommendations shall be submitted,  
* pilot deployment standards shall be published.

(c) Within 12 months:

* pilot results shall be compiled and submitted to the President with a national scale recommendation.

---

## **Section 14\. Funding.**

(a) Initial OFA operations shall be funded through lawfully available appropriations and interagency transfers consistent with the Economy Act.

(b) No funds shall be expended beyond amounts appropriated by Congress.

(c) Within 120 days, the Director of the OFA and the Director of OMB shall submit a funding and appropriations request for long-term scaling.

---

## **Section 15\. Oversight, Transparency, and Accountability.**

(a) The OFA shall establish internal oversight and audit procedures.

(b) Quarterly progress summaries shall be published in a manner consistent with privacy and national security requirements.

(c) The OFA shall ensure that program development remains transparent, non-coercive, and measurable.

---

## **Section 16\. General Provisions.**

(a) Nothing in this order shall be construed to impair or otherwise affect:  
(i) the authority granted by law to an executive department or agency, or the head thereof; or  
(ii) the functions of the Director of the Office of Management and Budget relating to budgetary, administrative, or legislative proposals.

(b) This order shall be implemented consistent with applicable law and subject to the availability of appropriations.

(c) This order is not intended to, and does not, create any right or benefit, substantive or procedural, enforceable at law or in equity by any party against the United States, its departments, agencies, or entities, its officers, employees, or agents, or any other person.

# **TIER 3 — TECHNICAL APPENDIX (IMPLEMENTERS \+ ARCHITECTS)**

## **Appendix A — Operational Concept**

This Executive Order is designed as a three-part national resilience modernization architecture:

1. **Family stability coordination (OFA \+ hotline \+ pilot)**  
2. **Emergency stabilization capacity (Patriot Dividend framework)**  
3. **Civic modernization infrastructure (identity, auditability, credentials, voluntary service)**

The long-term objective is to transition from reactive crisis response into a measurable, preventive governance infrastructure.

---

## **Appendix B — Key Definitions**

### **Family Amity**

A measurable condition of household stability, intergenerational continuity, and conflict reduction, evaluated through repeatable indicators rather than ideology.

### **Resonance Testing**

A structured conflict assessment and de-escalation methodology used to reduce escalation, improve communication outcomes, and route citizens to appropriate services.  
Not a medical diagnostic term.

### **Voluntary Civic Credits**

A non-transferable benefit credit mechanism designed to incentivize reinvestment into public goods.

## **Appendix D — Identity Modernization Guidance**

Identity modernization under this Order should include:

* quantum-resistant cryptographic transition planning,  
* multi-factor authentication with fallback accessibility options,  
* deepfake and synthetic identity fraud resistance,  
* consent-based data access controls,

* open audit and public standards publication.
