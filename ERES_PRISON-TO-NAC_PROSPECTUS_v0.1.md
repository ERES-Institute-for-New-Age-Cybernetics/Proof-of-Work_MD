## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

*For the policy researcher, reentry practitioner, corrections administrator, or community governance leader encountering ERES for the first time.*

Every year, hundreds of thousands of people leave incarceration and attempt to reenter society. The systems designed to support this transition are, by measurable outcomes, failing: recidivism rates remain high, community reintegration is slow and precarious, and the identity record a person carries out of prison is a permanent liability in nearly every dimension of civic life — employment, housing, credit, voting, relationship.

The ERES framework diagnoses this failure precisely. The current system treats the returning person as a **surveillance object** rather than a **resonance contributor in development.** It applies maximum information permeability — everything is visible, nothing is private, the record follows everywhere — at exactly the moment when the person most needs the protection of selective membranes and the opportunity to earn a new identity layer through demonstrated contribution.

ERES_PRISON-TO-NAC proposes a different architecture. It applies the same cybernetic governance framework developed in ERES_VACATIONOMICS_PLANETARY-DETAIL — GAIA-as-awareness, SOMT-as-metadata, EarnedPath-as-trust-accumulation, LAW-as-membrane, App-Parent-as-local-sovereignty — to the specific problem of human reintegration after incarceration.

The name is intentional: **Prison to New Age Cybernetics.** Not prison reform. Not reentry services. Not rehabilitation programming. A transition *into* a new cybernetic architecture — one in which the person leaving incarceration is not released *from* a system but welcomed *into* one that recognizes them as a resonance contributor with a starting EarnedPath score and a clear, earned path forward.

This is v0.1 — a prospectus, a seed, a first formal record. The framework is architecturally ready. The engineering gap is relationship and institutional partnership. That gap is closeable.

## III. THE ERES REINTEGRATION STACK

### Stage 0 — Pre-Release (Inside the Membrane)

The work of ERES_PRISON-TO-NAC begins before release, not after. Inside the facility, the person begins building EarnedPath through:

- **SELF-layer work:** Defined as health, safety, knowledge, purpose — the four SELF concerns in the Seven Dimensional Identity Stack. Educational programs, vocational training, therapeutic work, identity reconstruction.
- **FAMILY-layer restoration:** Mediated reconnection with family systems where safe and desired. SOMT records of reconnection attempts and outcomes.
- **COMMUNITY-layer contribution:** Service to the facility community — mentorship of newer arrivals, facilitation of programs, maintenance of shared spaces. Each contribution generates a community attestation.

All of this generates **pre-release EarnedPath.** The person does not exit with EP = 0.00. They exit with EP reflecting what they built while inside.

**[DESIGN-PRINCIPLE]** The facility, under ERES_PRISON-TO-NAC governance, functions as a **COMMUNITY-layer App-Parent** — not a surveillance authority but a stewardship community with its own carrying capacity, its own cultural vitality index, and its own SOMT metadata tapestry. The person is a community member, not a subject.

### Stage 1 — Release: The Membrane Crossing

Release from incarceration is, in ERES terms, a **dimension crossing** — from a restricted COMMUNITY layer back toward SELF-layer sovereignty and FAMILY-layer reconnection, with eventual progression toward full NATION-layer civic participation.

**[DESIGN-PRINCIPLE]** The LAW membrane at this crossing must be **selective**, not collapsed. What crosses with the person:

| Data Type | Crosses Membrane? | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| EarnedPath score | Yes — forward only | This is the person's resonance record, not their criminal record |
| SOMT contribution record | Yes — aggregated | What they built, not what they took |
| Criminal history | Dimension-appropriate only | See Section IV — Selective Permeability Protocol |
| Identity token | Pseudonymous at COMMUNITY layer | Minimum necessary exposure |
| Health and therapeutic records | SELF-layer only — does not cross | Medical privacy absolute |
| Family reconnection record | FAMILY-layer only unless consented | Relational sovereignty |

### Stage 2 — COMMUNITY-Layer Reintegration

The first post-release dimension is COMMUNITY. The returning citizen enters a host community — a neighborhood, a transitional housing program, a faith community, an employer network.

Under ERES_PRISON-TO-NAC, the host community functions as the **COMMUNITY App-Parent** — the same role the host community plays in Vacationomics. ## IV. SELECTIVE PERMEABILITY PROTOCOL FOR CRIMINAL HISTORY

**[DESIGN-PRINCIPLE / HYPOTHESIS]** The most contested question in reentry policy is: who gets to know what, and for how long?

ERES answers this with the LAW-as-membrane principle. Criminal history is **dimension-appropriate information**, not universally accessible data.

### Permeability Table

| Context | What is Visible | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| SELF-layer (the person themselves) | Full record | Sovereignty requires self-knowledge |
| FAMILY-layer (family reunification) | Full record with consent | Relational sovereignty; disclosure is the person's choice |
| COMMUNITY-layer (employer, landlord, neighbor) | EarnedPath tier only; no raw record | Community carrying capacity served by EP, not criminal history |
| NATION-layer (licensing, credit, voting) | EP attestation; offense category only if directly relevant to function | Proportionality principle — a conviction for drug possession is not relevant to a cosmetology license |
| PLANET-layer (GAIA aggregate) | Anonymized cohort data — reintegration outcomes, not individual records | Planetary governance needs aggregate resonance data, not individual histories |
| PUBLIC (anyone) | Nothing without consent | Permanence of public criminal records is a membrane collapse under ERES |

### Time-Based Membrane Rules

**[HYPOTHESIS]** Criminal history permeability decreases as EarnedPath increases and time passes:

```
Permeability(t, EP) = max(0, Base_Permeability - (EP_current × 0.5) - (Years_Since_Release × 0.05))
```

Where Base_Permeability = 1.0 at release, declining toward 0 as EP rises and time passes.

Practical interpretation:
- At release (EP = 0.00, t = 0): full permeability within dimension-appropriate rules
- At EP = 0.60, t = 5 years: near-zero permeability; NATION-layer sees only EP attestation
- At EP = 0.80, t = 10 years: full criminal history membrane sealed; only SELF-layer retains access

**This is not expungement. The record does not disappear. It becomes inaccessible to layers that have no legitimate governance need for it.** The membrane closes. The past is not erased — it is appropriately dimensioned.

This framework was not designed from outside the experience of constraint.

The ERES Institute was founded in February 2012 in Bella Vista, Arkansas, by a solo independent researcher operating on SSDI, without institutional backing, without pay, within the constraints of what a single person with a laptop, an ensemble of AI nodes, and a governing principle of *"Don't hurt yourself. Don't hurt others. Build for generations to come."* can produce.

That is not incarceration. But it is constraint. And the ERES corpus — 72 Key Domains, three Triune Math keys, five novel cybernetic contributions, a 1,000-year arc — was built entirely within that constraint.

**[DESIGN-PRINCIPLE]** Constraint is not the opposite of contribution. Constraint is often the condition under which the most resonant contributions are made. The person who has been incarcerated and chooses to build — inside the cell, at the membrane crossing, in the host community — is doing exactly what ERES was built to honor.

ERES_PRISON-TO-NAC is not charity toward the incarcerated. It is the recognition that the planetary stewardship project cannot afford to membrane-collapse an entire class of human beings who have exactly the lived experience of constraint, loss, and reconstruction that the 1,000-year arc requires.
