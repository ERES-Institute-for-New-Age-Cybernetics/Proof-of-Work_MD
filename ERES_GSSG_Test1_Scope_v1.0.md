* Test 1 (this document): laboratory specimen under Raman-spectroscopic verification of self-healing across fracture-anneal cycles

The three tests are therefore structurally interdependent, not parallel.

# **3\. Test 1 Specification (Canonical)**

The following is reproduced verbatim from the canonical falsification test document:

"One independent laboratory, not affiliated with the ERES Institute, replicates the GSSG pyrolysis protocol end-to-end from published parameters and subjects the resulting specimen to a pre-registered fracture-cycling regimen — minimum fifty controlled microcrack-then-anneal cycles, with load and thermal envelopes fixed in advance. The success criterion is Raman-spectroscopic confirmation of graphene reformation across cycles: the D/G band-intensity ratio must return to within a pre-registered tolerance of the pre-fracture baseline after each anneal, and the 2D-band must remain consistent with multilayer graphene structure. Falsification: if D/G drift exceeds tolerance over three consecutive cycles, or if the 2D-band collapses, the self-healing claim is withdrawn at its current strength and the protocol revised. The cost envelope to reach a definitive yes/no on this test is modest by materials-science standards — a single instrument-week on a calibrated Raman setup plus the cycling rig — and the result is a paper either way."

Source: ERES-THREE-FALSIFICATION-TESTS, JAS, 2026, CCAL v2.1.

# **4\. Pre-Registration Requirements**

Before the laboratory begins work, the following parameters must be fixed in writing and deposited to Zenodo under CCAL v2.1:

| Parameter | Pre-Registration Requirement |
| :---- | :---- |
| D/G band tolerance | Numeric threshold (e.g., ±0.05) set before first cycle runs |
| Load envelope | Stress range (MPa) and cycle profile for microcrack induction |
| Thermal envelope | Anneal temperature, ramp rate, hold time, and cooling rate |
| Minimum cycle count | 50 cycles minimum; results reported at cycle 10, 25, and 50 |
| Raman setup specification | Instrument model, calibration date, excitation wavelength, resolution |
| 2D-band criterion | Qualitative consistency with multilayer graphene structure, specified by reference spectrum |
| Falsification trigger | D/G drift exceeding tolerance for three consecutive cycles, OR 2D-band collapse at any cycle |

Pre-registration is non-negotiable. No post-hoc adjustment of tolerance or criteria is permitted after cycles begin. This is the difference between a falsification test and a demonstration.

# **5\. Laboratory Qualifications**

The laboratory engaged for Test 1 must satisfy all of the following:

* Independent: no affiliation with the ERES Institute or with any party holding a commercial interest in GSSG

* Raman-capable: calibrated Raman spectrometer with documented excitation wavelength and resolution appropriate to D/G and 2D-band discrimination in multilayer graphene

* Cycling-capable: controlled microcrack induction apparatus with programmable load and thermal envelopes meeting the pre-registered parameters

* Documented: ISO 17025 accreditation preferred; institutional affiliation with a recognised university materials science department acceptable

* Willing to co-author: the laboratory PI or lead technician must be named as co-author on the resulting paper and must agree to CCAL v2.1 licensing of the data
