# **3\. Key Definitions**

**How:** The method-layer of the cybernetic equation. In land governance, How specifies by what mechanism ownership is established, maintained, transferred, or revoked.

**Tile:** A discrete geospatial unit (latitude/longitude at chosen resolution) that serves as the atomic unit of the property ledger. Every tile is a PlayNAC actor.

**Stewardship Class:** One of three ownership categories—Personal, Public, or Private—each encoding a distinct bundle of rights, duties, and temporal constraints.

**Green Band:** The acceptable range of ecological, social, and justice metrics that a tile must maintain across decades to retain its current stewardship privileges.

**Permanent Layer:** Non-derogable guardrails hard-coded in the PlayNAC KERNEL (human rights, watershed safety, anti-genocide, anti-expulsion).

**Temporary Layer:** Emergency scripts with explicit decay timers, multi-stakeholder re-authorization, and automatic EP/RHC review after expiry.

# **4\. The Ownership Triad**

How resolves all land into three stewardship classes. These are not merely tonal categories; each encodes a distinct legal, ecological, and cybernetic posture within the 1000-Year Future Map.

## **4.1 Personal Ownership**

*Anchored in BORDER\_MM micro-layer*

Personal ownership is the individual or household stewardship of a tile—the most granular unit: a person’s home, garden, workshop. Under BORDER\_MM, the micro-border defines what flows cross that threshold (people, data, goods, water) and under what EarnedPath conditions.

Personal ownership is not absolute dominion. It is a scoped right conditioned on two constraints:

1) The tile’s ecological and social metrics must remain in the green band defined by PROP\_LL.  
2) Micro-rules cannot contradict macro constraints—no personal land use may externalize harm to watersheds, corridors, or future generations beyond ARI thresholds.

Personal ownership is where dignity lives. The permanent layer of WIS\_PT guarantees that no person can be expelled from their tile without due process anchored in the PlayNAC KERNEL’s non-derogable guardrails (anti-genocide, anti-expulsion, human-rights floor).

## **4.2 Public Ownership**

*Anchored in WIS\_PT permanent layer*

Public ownership covers commons: watersheds, water infrastructure, migration corridors, ecological reserves, defense perimeters, and any tile designated as non-alienable for civilizational continuity. These are the permanent-layer assets—their stewardship rules are hard-coded into the KERNEL with non-derogable safety and regeneration thresholds.

Public land is governed by the macro-border scale of BORDER\_MM (city, LOGOS-region, nation, watershed, orbital shell). No single actor—person, corporation, or government—can privatize or personally claim a public tile. Access is universal under EarnedPath conditions; extraction is prohibited or tightly bounded.

The 1000-year viability score of any policy proposal is tested against the integrity of the public layer first.

The WIS\_PT temporary layer allows emergency postures on public land (surge security, rationing, drought response), but these carry explicit decay timers and automatic RHC review—ensuring no “temporary” emergency becomes a permanent seizure of the commons.

## **4.3 Private Ownership**

*Anchored in PROP\_LL conditional stewardship*

Private ownership is the enterprise, organizational, or institutional holding of tiles for productive purpose—farms, factories, campuses, commercial corridors, research facilities. Under PROP\_LL, private ownership is defined as “right plus duty”: access, use, and extraction privileges are conditional on keeping the tile in a green band across ecological, social, and justice metrics over decades.

Private tiles are grouped into LOGOS-aligned domains (districts, corridors, economic zones) so that ownership decisions roll up into governance and societal layers. A private holder’s EarnedPath score reflects not just economic output but long-horizon stewardship.

Tiles that fall below ARI thresholds trigger remediation protocols, not punitive seizure—consistent with the NPR (Non-Punitive Remediation) framework.

Private ownership is the engine of innovation and production, but it operates inside the sandwich: Personal dignity below, Public commons above. It cannot compress either.

The compilation rule is hierarchical and strict. Every proposed land policy must pass validation in this order:

* Layer 1 — Public permanent constraints are tested first. If a policy degrades watershed integrity, violates human-rights floors, or enables expulsion, it fails before any other analysis.  
* Layer 2 — Private conditional duties are validated next. Does the productive use maintain green-band metrics? Does the steward’s EarnedPath score justify continued tenure?  
* Layer 3 — Personal micro-rules are checked last. Are household-level flow rules consistent with macro constraints? Is dignity preserved?

If any layer violates the 1000-year viability map, it fails ARI/RHC validation before deployment. No exceptions.
