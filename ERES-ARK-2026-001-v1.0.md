The question that has not been answered — the question that every ARK proposal quietly avoids because it is harder and less fundable — is this:

 

*What is needed to survive relatively happily on a foreign body?*

Between a biological persistence machine and a civilization. Between humans who survive long enough to reproduce and humans who produce art, science, governance, meaning, and joy.

The history of isolated human communities — Antarctic stations, submarines, space stations, intentional communities, refugee settlements — is a history of the gap between survival and happiness. People who are biologically alive but constitutionally miserable. People who have oxygen but no justice. Calories but no meaning. Safety but no freedom. The gap kills communities as surely as vacuum does, only more slowly and more painfully.

ERES-ARK-2026-001 answers the full question. Both halves. The survival floor and the happiness ceiling. And it demonstrates that the answer has been embedded in the ERES corpus since 2012 — not as a space exploration document, but as an emergency management document. Because the conditions that make a foreign body survivable and the conditions that make an emergency-stricken Earth community survivable are identical. SCALULAR is the ARK spec.
