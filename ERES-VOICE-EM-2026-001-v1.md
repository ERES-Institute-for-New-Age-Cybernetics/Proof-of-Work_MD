# 1. The Signal Path: Voice to Action

## Stage 1 — Entity Utterance

A person speaks. That is all. No keyboard, no screen, no app launch. Hands-free.

The utterance carries three simultaneous signal layers:

**Entity:** Who is speaking. The FAVORS biometric signature — voice-formant pattern (F1–F4), jitter, shimmer, harmonic-to-noise ratio — identifies the entity without requiring them to identify themselves. You don't say your name. Your voice *is* your name.

**Utterance:** What is said. The semantic content — words, syntax, intent.

**Subject:** What it's about. The contextual frame — where you are, what you were doing, what changed.

These three layers fire simultaneously. The system does not process them sequentially. Entity × Utterance × Subject is a single signal event.

## Stage 2 — Semantic + Biometric Split

The signal splits into two parallel channels the instant it enters VERTECA:

**Semantic channel:** The words. What did the person say? Natural language processing parses the utterance into semantic units — keywords, intent markers, urgency indicators, negation patterns.

**Biometric channel:** The body behind the words. BERA captures the physiological state embedded in the voice signal — heart-rate variability encoded in speech cadence, stress markers in formant deviation, respiratory state in pause patterns, electrodermal indicators in vocal tremor.

**Why the split matters:** A person saying "I'm fine" while their biometric channel shows HRV collapse and formant destabilization is *not fine*. The semantic channel says one thing. The biometric channel says another. VERTECA holds both. The system trusts the biometric channel when the two diverge — because the body cannot lie at the formant level.

**Base formulation:**

    Signal_in = Entity(FAVORS) × [Semantic(NLP) + Biometric(BERA)]
