**MAJORITY DEFINITION**

**The Smart-City User-GROUP: Practice and Affiliation**

This section defines the majority case. Not the edge case, not the abstract principle, not the all-things-considered template — the majority case. What the typical national Smart-City looks like in practice, how it forms, how it operates, how its sub-units (cooperatives, municipalities, regions) constitute the national operation, how it affiliates with other national Smart-Cities, what daily life inside it looks like for a Civigen, and what the chief bases the national Smart-City must cover are. The Manual's other sections supply the architectural commitments and the operational schemas; this section supplies the lived form.

The reason the majority definition gets its own section is that no architecture survives contact with deployment unless someone has thought through the typical case. Edge cases are real and important, but they are not where 95 percent of Smart-City life happens. A national Smart-City forming in the early years of Phase 1 needs to know what 95 percent of its operation will look like, not just what to do when an unusual situation arises. This section tells them.

The Nation-as-Smart-City frame is the architecture's reference implementation. In v1.1's reframe, each nation acts as a Smart-City — the deployment unit is national, not sub-national. Cooperatives, municipalities, and regions are sub-Smart-City units that constitute the national Smart-City's internal structure. This is the reference because nations are the existing scale unit at which sovereignty and substrate-stewardship can plausibly coalesce, and because the 1,000-year covenant operates with the three pathways (Contract, Consolidate Size, Consolidate to Another Nation via DOFA-as-Model) that only national-scale entities can meaningfully evaluate.

A sub-national entity (a single cooperative, a single municipality) may deploy UBIMIA in the rare case where the surrounding nation has not yet adopted, but the rare case is treated as a precursor to national adoption rather than as a destination — a sub-national pilot that demonstrates the architecture and prepares the ground for the national Smart-City to follow. The Manual treats sub-national pilots as preparatory rather than terminal because the architecture's commitments (recognition continuity, Civigen migration, the 1,000-year covenant, DOFA-Model consolidation) require national-scale standing to operate fully.

Sub-Smart-City unit formation occurs in parallel with national formation, beginning earlier in some sub-units (those with strong existing cooperative or municipal infrastructure) and following national formation in others. The national Smart-City is operationally complete when a critical mass of sub-units has reached Deployment Day, typically 25-40% of total population in the first cohort, with rolling sub-unit deployment continuing for 5-8 years.

## **M.2.2 What formation must produce, beyond components**

The eight phases produce the operational components, but the majority national Smart-City's formation also produces three intangibles that are constitutionally important and easy to under-supply if formation is rushed:

* **Affiliation expectation.** The nation must enter National Deployment Day expecting to affiliate with other nations through Nation-to-Nation Covenants using DOFA-as-Model within 5 years. * **Constitutional fluency.** The nation's leadership must be able to articulate the four Constitutional Pillars without reading from the Manual, and must be able to articulate the 1,000-year covenant's three pathways. Pillar I, II, III, IV, what each commits to, why each is non-negotiable; Contract, Consolidate Size, Consolidate to Another Nation, what each pathway means and when each is appropriate. A nation whose leadership cannot do this will face national constitutional deliberations in which the Pillars and pathways become peripheral, and operational drift will accumulate. Formation should include constitutional-fluency reviews — internal sessions in which national leaders teach the Pillars and pathways to each other, and to sub-unit leaders.

* **Failure-mode rehearsal.** The nation must rehearse the failure modes — what happens if the algorithmic core produces an output that visibly damages a sub-Smart-City unit, what happens if a Verification Authority is suspected of fraud at national scale, what happens if a National Council member engages in misconduct, what happens if MIEVM ensemble standoff persists, what happens if a sub-unit signals withdrawal, what happens if another nation requests Consolidate-via-DOFA-Model engagement. The rehearsals do not have to produce procedures (the Manual's Part VI supplies those); they produce familiarity, which is what allows the procedures to be executed under stress. Formation should include at least five failure-mode tabletops at national scale.

# **M.4 Governance — How the User-GROUP Decides**

Governance inside the Smart-City User-GROUP runs on three layers, each with distinct authority and procedure. The layers compose so that no single body holds disproportionate power, and so that the Civigen experience of governance is bounded — most Civigens interact with governance only at predictable touchpoints, while the operational discipline runs continuously beneath the surface.

## **M.4.1 The algorithmic core layer**

The deepest governance layer is the algorithmic core. It executes the deterministic rules of UBIMIA — BaseCost calibration, CR setting, 60/40 carryforward, COI amortization, freshness-window enforcement, parameter calibration. The core has no discretion; its outputs are reproducible by any auditor with access to the same input data. The majority Smart-City User-GROUP experiences the algorithmic core as the rhythm of the year — prices change in January, settlement happens in December, and the core's computations are visible in published audit logs.

The Civigen's interaction with the algorithmic core is mostly passive. The core does its work; the Civigen sees the results in their balance, their Bills, their year-end statement. The Civigen does not vote on BaseCost values or on the conversion rate; those are computed, not legislated. This is by design — the algorithmic core is constitutional precisely so that no human authority can manipulate prices to extract or coerce.

## **M.4.2 The Council layer**

Above the algorithmic core sits the GAIA Council — the human supervisory body. The Smart-City Council has 9 members, selected by weighted lottery from Civigens with ΔR\_contributor \> 0.1, serving 2-year terms with half rotating each year. The Council convenes quarterly for regular sessions and extraordinarily as needed for time-sensitive matters.

What the Council can do (within authority): override an algorithmic core output by 2/3 majority and 30-day public notice when override is justified by documented failure of the rule under specific circumstances; resolve dissent in MIEVM ensemble standoffs; adjudicate disputes between Civigens; approve or reject Compassion Override applications; confer or revoke Verification Authority on institutions applying for Type C status; convene constitutional review.

What the Council cannot do: revise the deterministic rules executed by the algorithmic core (rule revision requires constitutional review, not Council action); revoke any Civigen's UBI for any reason (the survival floor is non-revocable); revise the Recognition Rule (that is the Adopting Community's authority, not the Council's); revise the Three Governing Principles or the Eight Immutable Ethical Principles or the Constitutional Pillars (those are non-revisable by any deployment); operate in secret (all Council decisions are public, with attributed votes and published rationales).

The Civigen's interaction with the Council is sporadic. Most Civigens never attend a Council session (sessions are public but most Civigens have other commitments); some Civigens are eligible for selection by lottery and may serve a term; a small number engage with the Council through dispute filings, recall petitions, or Override Proposal sponsorships. The Council's authority is bounded; its visibility is high; its capacity to operate is sustained by the Civigens' attention to its decisions, even when most Civigens do not actively participate.

## **M.4.3 The COI / Submit-to-NPR layer**

The most local governance layer is the User-GROUP COI itself, where Submit-to-NPR happens 24/7. This is the layer most Civigens interact with most frequently, and it is the layer most distinctive to UBIMIA — Council and algorithmic core have analogues in conventional governance, but Submit-to-NPR within COI is the architectural innovation that makes Civigen-level integrity sustainable.

Submit means the Civigen is voluntarily available to be held accountable, by their COI peers, through Non-Punitive Remediation. NPR is the discipline of working through what has happened — what was done, what was its impact, what would repair, what would prevent recurrence — without punishment, blame, or shame as the operative mode. The COI does the work; the Civigen submits to the work; the Instrument of Faith underwrites the binding so that Submit can be voluntary without becoming optional.

In the majority Smart-City User-GROUP, COIs are the size at which trust is operationally possible — typically 50–200 Civigens per COI, with a Smart-City of 10,000 organized into 50–100 COIs. COIs are voluntary affiliations; a Civigen chooses their COI, which means COIs vary in character (residential-block COIs, work-domain COIs, common-interest COIs, kinship-cluster COIs). The Civigen's interaction with their COI is woven through daily life. Casual conversations with COI peers; periodic COI gatherings (typical: monthly); occasional Submit events when accountability matters; participation as a Submit witness for other COI members; coordination on contributions that cross multiple Civigens. The COI is where Famity (Pillar IV) lives most directly — Marriage at Heart at community scale, the WE/Uuu/SOUND-loop felt as a daily field condition, the literal-figurative-subjective binding made operationally tangible.

## **M.4.4 How the layers compose**

The three layers compose by separation of authority:

* Algorithmic core: deterministic; no discretion; constitutional parameter execution. Acts continuously.

* Council: discretionary at the override and adjudication boundary; bounded by what it cannot do. Acts quarterly and as needed.

* COI / Submit-to-NPR: relational; voluntary; underwritten by IOF. Acts continuously through the daily lives of Civigens.

A matter that arises is resolved at the lowest layer that can resolve it. Most matters resolve in COI-level NPR without ever reaching the Council. Some matters escalate to Council adjudication when COI cannot resolve. Very few matters reach the algorithmic core's revision-procedure layer — only constitutional questions about the rules themselves do that. The architecture's stability comes from this layering: when COIs do their NPR work, Councils have less to adjudicate; when Councils adjudicate well, the algorithmic core's parameters need less revision; when parameters are stable, the Civigen experience is predictable, which makes COI work and Council deliberation easier in turn.

# **M.5 Affiliation — How Smart-Cities Connect**

A national Smart-City does not exist in isolation. Affiliation operates at two levels: internal affiliation (sub-Smart-City units within the national Smart-City connecting through Compacts) and external affiliation (national Smart-Cities connecting through Nation-to-Nation Covenants using DOFA-as-Model). Both levels are constitutional and operationally consequential. This section specifies the affiliation patterns the majority national Smart-City uses, the architectural commitments affiliation honors, the three-pathway covenant evaluation, and the practical procedures for forming and maintaining affiliated relationships.

## **M.5.1 Why affiliation is constitutional**

Three reasons make affiliation non-optional:

* **The substrate is one.** Pillar I (Bioenergetic Imperative) commits the architecture to recognizing one substrate of all life. A national Smart-City that operates as if its substrate ends at its border violates this commitment. Watersheds cross borders; air masses cross borders; ecological cycles cross borders; AI.RE breath crosses borders. Affiliation is how Smart-Cities — at sub-unit and national levels — honor the one-substrate commitment operationally.

* **Civigens migrate.** Civigens move between sub-units and between nations over their lifetimes — for work, for family, for education, for ecological reasons. The architecture's commitments to non-revocability of UBI and to recognition continuity require affiliation procedures that allow Civigens to move without losing standing. Within the national Smart-City, sub-unit Compacts handle migration; between national Smart-Cities, Nation-to-Nation Covenants handle it.

* **The KERNEL is singular.** The Global Actuary Investor Authority operates one actuarial table over one substrate. Smart-Cities that affiliate are honoring the singularity by coordinating their operations; Smart-Cities that refuse to affiliate are implicitly forking the KERNEL, which is permitted but is a much larger commitment than maintaining affiliation. The 1,000-year covenant's three-pathway structure (Contract, Consolidate Size, Consolidate to Another Nation) gives nations explicit instruments for honoring KERNEL singularity through affiliation.

## **M.5.2 Sub-Smart-City unit Compacts (within the National Smart-City)**

The basic internal affiliation instrument is the sub-Smart-City unit Compact — a written agreement between two sub-units within the same national Smart-City specifying the cross-sub-unit arrangements. The majority national Smart-City has hundreds-to-thousands of sub-unit Compacts in operation; sub-units sign their first Compacts within 18 months of sub-unit Phase 1 commencement, with affiliation density increasing over the first 5 years.

Sub-Smart-City unit Compacts cover at minimum:

* Civigen migration between sub-units. A Civigen who relocates from sub-unit A to sub-unit B has their record transferred, with prior ΔR\_contributor preserved at standard rates per the national Recognition Rule.

* Cross-sub-unit SCALULAR access. A Civigen of sub-unit A traveling within sub-unit B can access SCALULAR services from B's authorized providers, with reimbursement settled between the COI pools at published rates.

* Verification Authority recognition. Institutions holding Verification Authority in sub-unit A may issue Type C verifications for sub-unit B Civigens visiting or contributing in A's territory.

* MIEVM ensemble coordination. The two sub-units may share MIEVM ensemble attestations for cross-sub-unit Claims, reducing duplicated ensemble work.

* Conflict resolution. Disputes that cross the sub-unit boundary are resolved by joint Council session, with appeal to the National GAIA Council if the joint session deadlocks.

## **M.5.3 Nation-to-Nation Covenants (using DOFA-as-Model)**

The basic external affiliation instrument is the Nation-to-Nation Covenant — a constitutional agreement between two national Smart-Cities specifying the cross-Nation arrangements, modeled on DOFA (Department of Family Amity) as the binding mechanism. Nation-to-Nation Covenants are heavier than sub-unit Compacts because they operate at the constitutional scale where sovereignty meets Famity-binding. The majority national Smart-City signs at least three Nation-to-Nation Covenants within the first 5 years of National Phase 1, with the affiliation network maturing across the first 25 years.

Nation-to-Nation Covenants using DOFA-as-Model cover at minimum:

* Civigen migration between nations. A Civigen who relocates from Nation A to Nation B has their record transferred under the Covenant's terms, with the threefold Marriage at Heart (literal, figurative, subjective) honored across the migration. Recognition continuity is the constitutional default; loss of standing on migration is the failure mode.

* Cross-Nation SCALULAR access. A Civigen of Nation A traveling within Nation B can access SCALULAR services from B's authorized providers under terms that respect the family-amity commitment between the two nations rather than treating cross-border access as mere transactional convenience.

* Substrate-binding coordination. Both nations recognize the shared substrate that crosses their political border — the watershed, the air mass, the ecological corridor, the migratory pathway — and coordinate stewardship of the substrate as one rather than as separate national assets.

* Joint Council operations. Matters that affect both nations are addressed by joint Council sessions with equal-weighted representation, modeled on the way joint sub-unit sessions resolve cross-sub-unit matters within a national Smart-City.

* DOFA-Model alignment. Both nations commit to DOFA-as-Model in their internal Famity arrangements, ensuring that Family Amity at the household, community, and sub-unit levels is consistent with Family Amity at the Nation-to-Nation level. The Covenant is what makes the two Nations' Famity arrangements mutually intelligible.

## **M.5.4 The 1,000-year covenant's three pathways**

Each national Smart-City enters a 1,000-year covenant with the architecture, and the covenant has three pathways the nation may take at decade-mark and century-mark evaluations. The pathways are not exclusive — a nation may take Contract for several centuries and then Consolidate Size in a later century — but at any given evaluation, one of the three is the active pathway.

* **Contract —** the nation commits to operating its Smart-City within the present structure for the covenant period. This is the default pathway and the most common. A nation operating well under Contract continues operating; the structure works; the substrate is honored; the trajectory progresses. Most decade-mark evaluations result in Contract continuation.

* **Consolidate Size —** the nation reduces its operational scope to a smaller domain that better fits substrate-carrying-capacity. This pathway is taken when the population-to-substrate ratio has drifted above carrying capacity and voluntary calibration through Famity-grounded population decisions is insufficient to restore balance within reasonable timescale. Consolidate Size is geographic — the nation may release territory, devolve authority to sub-units, or transition to a more compact operational footprint — and is always voluntary at the constitutional level. Sub-units retain standing through their own sub-unit Compacts during the consolidation transition.

* **Consolidate to Another Nation using DOFA as Model —** the nation merges with another nation into a single combined Smart-City, with DOFA's Family Amity discipline as the model for the merger. This pathway is taken when two nations have reached such depth of Nation-to-Nation Covenant that consolidation is the natural next step, or when one nation's continued independent operation has become unsustainable and another nation's Famity-binding can absorb it without extraction. Consolidate to Another Nation is constitutionally weighty; it requires multi-decade preparation, thorough constitutional review by both nations, and the explicit consent of both Civigen populations through the constitutional process the nations have established. Conquest is not a Consolidate-to-Another-Nation pathway; conquest is the failure mode the architecture refuses to recognize.

The three pathways collectively give the architecture's 1,000-year horizon operational form. A nation that signs the covenant knows what evaluation it faces at decade-marks and century-marks; the evaluation is structured rather than open-ended; the choices are constitutional rather than ad hoc political. This is one of the architectural commitments that distinguishes UBIMIA from inherited governance — the long-arc reorganization of political units is given a constitutional form rather than left to whatever historical contingency happens.

## **M.5.5 Affiliation patterns at scale**

Three patterns of affiliation occur in practice; the majority national Smart-City uses all three over time, at both sub-unit and Nation-to-Nation scales:

* **Adjacent affiliation.** With Smart-Cities (sub-units or nations) in geographically neighboring territory. Most directly serves Civigen migration and shared-substrate concerns. The first sub-unit Compact and the first Nation-to-Nation Covenant a Smart-City typically signs are with adjacent neighbors.

* **Networked affiliation.** With non-adjacent Smart-Cities that share characteristics (similar size, similar constitutional structure, similar SCALULAR provider mix, similar substrate-stewardship priorities). Networked affiliations support practice-sharing, Council-procedure benchmarking, and MIEVM ensemble coordination across distance.

* **Tiered affiliation.** Within the national Smart-City, sub-units affiliate with smaller cooperative units (downward) and with Regional groupings of sub-units (upward). At Nation-to-Nation scale, nations affiliate with smaller national Smart-Cities (typically newer deployments seeking guidance) and with continental or cross-continental groupings (typically older deployments coordinating long-arc trajectory). Tiered affiliations provide the operational pathways through which the architecture scales without requiring centralized coordination.

## **M.5.6 The affiliation network and the broader BEE**

The set of all affiliated Smart-Cities — sub-units within nations and nations across the planet — forms an affiliation network. The network is not centralized: there is no master registry, no head-Smart-City, no governing body for the network as a whole. The network is the emergent structure of bilateral Compacts and Covenants, each Smart-City maintaining the bonds that serve it. ERES Institute publishes a public registry of declared Compacts and Covenants (with the consent of participating Smart-Cities), so prospective affiliates can identify potential partners.

The affiliation network is how the Bio-Ecologic Economy comes into existence at planetary scale. A single national Smart-City operating BEE is a meaningful demonstration; a network of affiliated national Smart-Cities operating BEE across continents is the architecture realized at planetary scale. The path from the first to the second runs through Nation-to-Nation Covenants and the practice-sharing they enable. The majority national Smart-City, by Phase 2 graduation, is part of an affiliation network of at least 10–25 other national Smart-Cities.

Beyond the planetary affiliation network is the architectural commitment to interplanetary deployment, supported through DOFA's broad scope. When Earth-based verification of the architecture's capacity is sufficiently mature, the affiliation network may extend beyond Earth — interplanetary Smart-Cities affiliated with Earth-based national Smart-Cities through Covenants that extend DOFA's Family Amity discipline across planets. ## **M.6.3 The non-punitive discipline**

Across all five levels, the architecture maintains non-punitive discipline. NPR at Level 1 is by definition non-punitive. Cross-COI mediation at Level 2 seeks resolution without blame-allocation. Council adjudication at Level 3 may impose remedial sanctions (Meritcoin clawback for fraud, Verification Authority revocation for misconduct, recall for Council members) but does not impose punitive sanctions disconnected from remedy. External audit at Level 4 produces findings rather than punishments. Constitutional review at Level 5 produces decisions about architecture, not about persons.

Non-punitive does not mean consequence-free. A Civigen who commits fraud loses minting rights for 180 days (consequence), forfeits the fraudulent Meritcoin (remedy), and works through the consequences with their COI through NPR (relational integrity). The combination produces accountability without converting the architecture into a punishment-allocation machine. Civigens can do hard things and not become permanent moral pariahs; institutions can fail and not become permanent enemies. The architecture accepts that real people make real mistakes and structures itself to preserve their standing while addressing the harm.

# **M.7 Growth and Maturation — The User-GROUP Across Years**

A User-GROUP that survives its first year matures through subsequent years. The majority Smart-City User-GROUP follows a recognizable arc; deviations are real but the central tendency is clear enough to specify.

## **M.7.1 Year 1 — Adolescent operation**

Year 1 is the year of learning what the architecture actually does. Year 1 typical experience: high engagement (Civigens are paying attention); high uncertainty (procedures are unfamiliar); high error rate (mistakes are common as practice settles); high learning rate (every error becomes a procedural correction). Year 2 is the year practice settles. Procedures from the Manual are now familiar; calibration values are stable; Civigens know what to expect; the Council's deliberative culture has solidified. Year 2 typical experience: routine engagement (most days are unremarkable); moderate uncertainty (the unusual cases are still unfamiliar); declining error rate (mistakes are rarer as procedures internalize); steady learning rate (each unusual case adds practice depth). Year 2 is when the User-GROUP starts to feel like itself rather than feeling like a deployment.

## **M.7.3 Years 3–5 — Phase 2 candidacy**

Years 3 through 5 are the period during which the User-GROUP becomes a candidate for Phase 2 graduation. The graduation gates from the Manual's Part IV must be satisfied: 24 consecutive months of Council-flagged-failure-free operation, Recognition Rule maturity, SCALULAR provider acceptance above 75%, Council functionality demonstrated, three consecutive clean quarterly audits, constitutional alignment formally affirmed.

Most Smart-City User-GROUPs satisfy the graduation gates by Year 4 or Year 5\. Some do not — Year 5 graduation reviews that fail produce remediation cycles, and the User-GROUP may continue in Phase 1 for additional years before re-candidacy. The architecture does not force graduation; it permits and supports it but does not impose schedules. A User-GROUP that takes 7 years to graduate Phase 1 is still operating UBIMIA correctly, provided the constitutional commitments are honored throughout.

## **M.7.4 Years 6+ — Phase 2 and beyond**

Beyond Phase 2 graduation, the User-GROUP enters integration with existing systems. Welfare programs fold into Gracechain UBI; tax authorities accept Meritcoin (via Credits) for tax payments; SCALULAR providers must accept Credits without fiat-reimbursement scaffolding. Affiliation network membership matures; Dual Recognition Compacts with at least three other User-GROUPs are required for Phase 3 candidacy.

By Year 10–15, the typical User-GROUP is in Phase 3 — full settlement, where Meritcoin and Credits are the only currencies for SCALULAR services within the jurisdiction. The merit layer is still operating but the discount band may already be tightening per the Trajectory Calibration. By Year 25–50, the discount band should have tightened meaningfully; by Year 100, the merit layer's purpose may already be partially fulfilled, with the band continuing to tighten toward the eventual dissolution that the 1,000-year horizon names.

## **III.2 The evaluation formula**

Every merit-bearing flow under UBIMIA is evaluated by:

**Judicial \= SEPLTA-$\_HE × MDCCLXXVI-Action\_Reaction \+ P³\_Cause-Effect**

### **SEPLTA-$\_HE — six-vector financialization assessment**

SEPLTA-$\_HE is the six SEPLTA vectors (Social, Economic, Political, Legal, Technical, Administrative) evaluated against the $\_HE term, the financialization-of-existence operator. The $\_HE term names the pattern by which existence becomes financialized — the slow conversion of substrate-participation into monetary-measurable units, until the units become the measurement and the substrate becomes invisible. SEPLTA-$\_HE asks: across all six vectors, does this flow preserve SELF against $ELF, or does it accelerate $ELF at SELF's expense?

Each vector engages a specific failure mode:

* Social-$\_HE: does the flow strengthen relational substrate (kinship, community, mutual aid) or replace it with transactional substitution (paid services replacing relational care)?

* Economic-$\_HE: does the flow circulate within Bio-Ecologic Economy logic (recycling, substrate-honoring) or extract from substrate into financial pools that do not return to BEE?

MDCCLXXVI is the year 1776, the founding moment of the United States Constitution and the year in which \*all men are created equal\* was committed to a society that had not yet earned the words. Operationally, MDCCLXXVI-Action\_Reaction is checked at the authorization layer. ### **P³\_Cause-Effect — three-horizon discipline**

P³ is the cubic Cause-Effect operator. P³ takes immediate cause-effect, mid-term cause-effect, and long-term cause-effect together; only when all three confirm trajectory-favorable outcomes does the flow authorize. Short-term gain at long-term cost fails P³. Data-Integrity (the Currency volume of the Trilogy) ensures that P³ inputs are verifiable across all three horizons; without Data-Integrity's discipline, P³ would collapse into asserted progress without verifiable substrate.

P³'s three horizons are operationally defined:

* Mid-term horizon: the flow's effect within 1 year. Did the contribution's effect persist? Did the resonance gain hold or decay? Did the verification stand up to dispute?

* Long-term horizon: the flow's effect within the trajectory. Does the contribution support the 1,000-year horizon's destination? Does it accelerate or retard the merit layer's eventual dissolution? Does it deepen substrate-coupling or extract from it?

**Civigens — The Participant**

A Civigen is the participant in UBIMIA whose standing is constituted by Submit-to-Non-Punitive-Remediation 24/7 within their User-GROUP Community of Interest, approved by GAIA, underwritten as Instrument of Faith. Without Submit, there is no Civigen — there is a person receiving services, but not a participant in the architecture.

## **1.1 What Submit means**

Submit is the Civigen's voluntary act of being available, continuously, for Non-Punitive Remediation by their User-GROUP COI. The architectural commitment is 24/7 availability; the practical reality is that NPR events occur infrequently for most Civigens (typical Civigen experiences 0–3 NPR cycles in a typical year), but the availability is constant. A Civigen who is unavailable for Submit (traveling, ill, in crisis) notifies the COI and arrangements are made; sustained unavailability without notice is the failure mode that places Civigen standing in question.

Submit is voluntary. It is not coerced, not legally mandated by external authority, not enforced by punitive sanction. The IOF (Instrument of Faith) is what makes Submit operationally durable without coercion — Civigens enter Submit through faith-binding to the COI and to the architecture's constitutional commitments, and the COI's reciprocal commitment to the Civigen sustains the binding over time. This is a substantial commitment; it is also the architecture's distinctive innovation. Conventional governance manages participation through coercion or through extractive incentive; UBIMIA manages participation through voluntary Submit underwritten by IOF, which produces a different operational reality than either.

## **1.2 What Non-Punitive Remediation means**

NPR is the discipline of working through what has happened — what was done, what was its impact, what would repair, what would prevent recurrence — without punishment, blame, or shame as the operative mode. The COI does the work; the Civigen submits to the work; the result is relational and remedial rather than punitive.

NPR is non-punitive does not mean consequence-free. A Civigen who commits fraud loses minting rights for 180 days (consequence), forfeits the fraudulent Meritcoin (remedy), and works through the consequences with their COI through NPR (relational integrity). The combination produces accountability without converting the architecture into a punishment-allocation machine. Civigens can do hard things and not become permanent moral pariahs; institutions can fail and not become permanent enemies. The architecture accepts that real people make real mistakes and structures itself to preserve their standing while addressing the harm.

## **1.3 What the User-GROUP COI is**

The Community of Interest is the relational scale where Civigens know each other personally and the threefold Marriage at Heart is operationally tangible. Typical COI size: 50–200 Civigens. A national Smart-City of tens-of-millions of Civigens is organized into hundreds-of-thousands of COIs; a sub-Smart-City unit of 5,000–25,000 Civigens is organized into 50–200 COIs.

COIs are voluntary affiliations. A Civigen chooses their COI, which means COIs vary in character (residential-block COIs, work-domain COIs, common-interest COIs, kinship-cluster COIs). GAIA approves each COI structure, ensuring that the COI satisfies basic conditions (size range, governance bylaws, NPR commitment, IOF underwriting) and is not a vehicle for evading accountability.

A Civigen may change COIs with 30-day notice and acceptance by the new COI. COI change is not gated by ΔR\_contributor or by any merit metric — Civigens can leave and join COIs based on relational and life circumstances. What the COI must have is the capacity to host Submit-to-NPR for its Civigens; what the Civigen must have is a COI that hosts them.
