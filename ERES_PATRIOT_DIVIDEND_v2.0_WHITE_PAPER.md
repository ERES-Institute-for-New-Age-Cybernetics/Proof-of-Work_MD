# **EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

The United States faces a compound crisis: geopolitical entanglement generating perpetual economic shock, domestic inflation eroding real income for the working and middle classes, and a fracturing social contract between the governed and the governing. Traditional policy instruments—tax cuts, military spending increases, or austerity—cannot match the velocity of these converging threats.

This paper proposes the **ERES Patriot Dividend**: a strategic, time-bound direct cash transfer to every American adult, funded by windfall profit recapture from crisis-benefiting industries and a new class of Treasury-issued Patriot Bonds. It is not welfare. It is not ideology. It is a real-time reciprocal contract between the state and the citizen—immediate, measurable, and temporary.

| *CORE\_ROOT SolutionProblem: The absence of a reciprocal economic contract between the state and the citizen during extreme systemic stress. The state demands sacrifice but offers no immediate, tangible reciprocity. This gap destroys legitimacy and destabilizes the system from within.* |
| :---- |

The Patriot Dividend closes that gap. It solves simultaneously for legitimacy, demand destruction, social contract fracture, inequality acceleration, and the information deficit required for crisis management—all through a single, administratively lean mechanism.

Within the ERES framework, the Dividend instantiates the foundational governance equation **C \= R × P / M**—Cybernetics equals Resource times Purpose divided by Method. The Resource is windfall-recaptured capital and sovereign credit. The Purpose is national resilience and citizen survival. The Method is direct digital transfer via existing federal infrastructure. When Purpose is clear and Method is efficient, the system corrects. When institutional friction (M) overwhelms available resource (R) and clear purpose (P), systems fail. The Patriot Dividend is engineered to keep M low, R flowing, and P unambiguous.

# **SECTION 2: THE SOLUTION — ERES PATRIOT DIVIDEND**

## **2.1 Definition**

The ERES Patriot Dividend is a monthly, non-means-tested cash payment to all adult U.S. citizens (approximately 277 million individuals), funded by a combination of wartime windfall profit recapture and sovereign debt issuance framed as national investment. It is:

* Universal: Paid to every adult citizen regardless of income, creating political buy-in across all demographics and eliminating administrative overhead of means-testing.

* Immediate: Distributed monthly via existing digital rails (FedNow, Treasury Direct, IRS infrastructure).

* Conditional (Soft): Opt-in grants access; opt-out is permitted but forfeits the dividend. Participation implies acceptance of the reciprocal social contract.

* Temporary: Tied to the duration of the declared national emergency, with objective sunset triggers.

## **2.2 The Mechanism**

**Funding Source.** The Dividend draws from two complementary streams:

1) Windfall Profit Recapture Tax (approximately 40% of total funding): A temporary surcharge on excess profits earned by corporations in energy production, energy trading, and defense contracting during the declared emergency period. “Excess profit” is defined as net income exceeding 120% of the firm’s trailing five-year average. This mechanism targets the crisis-amplified margin—not the baseline business—ensuring the industries benefiting most from the disruption fund the stabilization.

2) Patriot Bond Issuance (approximately 60% of total funding): A new class of Treasury securities marketed directly to American citizens and institutions, bearing a competitive yield and branded explicitly as crisis-response instruments. Patriot Bonds serve dual purpose: they finance the Dividend while channeling domestic savings into sovereign instruments rather than speculative assets, providing a counter-inflationary savings vehicle.

**Distribution.** Monthly deposit via direct deposit, digital wallet, or pre-loaded debit card. The payment amount is indexed to a Crisis Cost Index (CCI)—a composite of fuel, grocery, and housing cost increases relative to the pre-crisis 12-month baseline, targeting 80% offset of the median household’s increased essential burden.

**Administration.** The Dividend leverages existing IRS and Social Security Administration infrastructure for identity verification and payment routing. No new federal agency is required. Smart-city digital payment pilots in 5–10 mid-sized metropolitan areas test efficiency, data collection, and resource allocation optimization in parallel.

## **3.3 Revenue Projections**

**Windfall Profit Recapture (estimated $100–$160 billion/year).** In FY2025, the top five U.S. integrated oil companies (ExxonMobil, Chevron, ConocoPhillips, Phillips 66, Marathon Petroleum) reported combined net earnings exceeding $55 billion under normal market conditions. ExxonMobil alone earned $28.8 billion. A crisis-level energy disruption (Strait of Hormuz-class event) historically doubles or triples energy sector margins. A 50% surcharge on excess profits above the 120%-of-baseline threshold, applied across the top 20 energy producers and top 10 defense contractors (whose combined 2025 net income exceeded $75 billion), conservatively yields $100–$160 billion annually during the crisis window.

**Patriot Bond Issuance (estimated $840–$900 billion/year).** The remaining balance is financed through sovereign debt issuance. For context, the U.S. Treasury issued approximately $5.4 trillion in net new debt between March 2020 and December 2021 to finance pandemic response. An additional $840–$900 billion in purpose-branded, citizen-marketed bonds represents approximately 16–17% of that precedent—significant but fiscally manageable for a crisis of equivalent severity.

## **3.4 The Inflation Question**

The primary economic objection to any direct transfer program is inflationary pressure. The Patriot Dividend addresses this through three integrated mechanisms:

1. Offset, not stimulus. The Dividend is explicitly designed to offset crisis-induced cost increases, not to inject net new purchasing power. At 80% of CCI, the Dividend replaces lost purchasing power rather than expanding demand beyond pre-crisis levels. The economic function is stabilization, not acceleration.

2. Patriot Bond absorption. The bond issuance component actively removes cash from the economy by channeling domestic savings into sovereign instruments. Every dollar of Patriot Bond purchased by an American household is a dollar withdrawn from consumer markets and redirected into government financing—a counter-inflationary mechanism embedded in the funding structure itself.

3. Essential-goods price stabilization. The ARRES Act (see Section 6\) includes authority for temporary price stabilization measures on fuel, groceries, and housing—not Soviet-style price controls, but anti-gouging enforcement with reporting requirements. The Windfall Profit Recapture Tax provides the enforcement incentive: firms that raise prices beyond cost-justified levels face higher effective tax rates.

This three-layer architecture—offset targeting, savings absorption, and price stabilization—ensures the Dividend functions as a shock absorber, not a demand accelerator.

# **SECTION 5: IMPLEMENTATION ROADMAP**

* Executive Framing: Launch the public narrative—“You carry the burden. You deserve the share.” Position the Dividend as patriotic reciprocity, not entitlement.

* Crisis Cost Index (CCI): Treasury and BLS jointly establish the CCI methodology and publish the first baseline measurement.

## **Phase 2: Infrastructure (Months 3–6)**

* Digital Rails: Expand the FedNow instant-payment system for Dividend distribution. Partner with fintech providers to reach unbanked and underbanked populations via pre-loaded debit cards.

* Smart-City Pilots: Select 5–10 mid-sized metropolitan areas to integrate dividend distribution with urban resource management (energy allocation, transport optimization, food-supply logistics).

* Verification: Build citizen registry cross-referenced with IRS, SSA, and Treasury data. Leverage existing Economic Impact Payment infrastructure from 2020–2021.

## **Phase 3: Distribution (Month 6+)**

* First Payment: Initial Patriot Dividend deposited to all verified accounts.

* Monthly Indexing: CCI recalculated monthly using real-time BLS data on fuel, food, and housing. Dividend amount adjusts automatically—up or down—to maintain the 80% offset target.

* Sunset Clause: Dividend terminates automatically 90 days after ALL of the following conditions are met for 90 consecutive calendar days: (a) national emergency declaration is rescinded; (b) WTI crude oil price returns to within 115% of pre-crisis 12-month average; (c) CPI-measured essential-goods inflation falls below 3.0% annualized. Congress may renew in 6-month increments by majority vote.

# **SECTION 7: HISTORICAL AND OPERATIONAL PRECEDENT**

The Patriot Dividend is not without precedent. It synthesizes proven mechanisms from multiple domains:

**The Alaska Permanent Fund Dividend (1982–present).** The longest-running universal cash dividend in American history. In 2025, each eligible Alaskan received $1,000 from the state’s $83 billion sovereign wealth fund, generated by oil revenue. The Alaska model demonstrates that universal, non-means-tested cash transfers are administratively feasible, politically durable across partisan lines, and economically stimulative without generating runaway inflation in a resource-dependent economy. The Patriot Dividend adapts this model from state to federal scale, from permanent to temporary, and from resource-wealth to crisis-response framing.

**COVID-19 Economic Impact Payments (2020–2021).** Three rounds of direct payments totaling approximately $867 billion reached over 160 million American households within weeks of authorization, using IRS and Treasury infrastructure. This demonstrated that the federal government possesses the administrative capacity to execute universal payments at the scale the Patriot Dividend requires. The infrastructure already exists.

**Wartime Economic Stabilization (1942–1947).** The Office of Price Administration managed rationing, price controls, and war bonds simultaneously, maintaining domestic economic stability while the nation prosecuted a global conflict. War bonds alone financed approximately $185 billion (equivalent to \~$3.4 trillion in 2026 dollars) of the war effort while serving as a counter-inflationary savings instrument—precisely the dual function Patriot Bonds are designed to perform.

**Keynesian Wartime Finance.** John Maynard Keynes’s 1940 pamphlet “How to Pay for the War” proposed compulsory savings (deferred pay) combined with direct support for the lowest-income households—a mechanism structurally identical to the Patriot Dividend’s combination of bond issuance and direct transfer. The principle is established: during wartime, the state must simultaneously finance the conflict and protect the domestic base.
