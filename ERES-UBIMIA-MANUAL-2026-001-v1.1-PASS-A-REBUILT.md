**MAJORITY DEFINITION**

**The Smart-City User-GROUP: Practice and Affiliation**

This section defines the majority case. Not the edge case, not the abstract principle, not the all-things-considered template — the majority case. What the typical Smart-City User-GROUP looks like in practice, how it forms, how it operates, how it affiliates with the broader UBIMIA network, what daily life inside it looks like for a Civigen, and what the chief bases the User-GROUP must cover are. The Manual's other sections supply the architectural commitments and the operational schemas; this section supplies the lived form.

The reason the majority definition gets its own section is that no architecture survives contact with deployment unless someone has thought through the typical case. Edge cases are real and important, but they are not where 95 percent of User-GROUP life happens. A User-GROUP forming in Year 1 of Phase 1 needs to know what 95 percent of its days will look like, not just what to do when an unusual situation arises. This section tells them.

The Smart-City frame is deliberate. Not every User-GROUP is in a city, and not every city is smart. The Smart-City User-GROUP is the \*reference implementation\* — the case that exercises every component of the architecture, from THOW shelter at the smallest scale through GSSG infrastructure at the largest, from Civigen Submit-to-NPR through GAIA Council adjudication, from Border concerns through Law concerns through Currency concerns. A cooperative deploying in a rural setting can use the Smart-City description as a baseline and adapt outward; a regional deployment can use it as a foundation and extend upward. The Smart-City User-GROUP is the baseline because it is what the architecture was designed to serve, and what the Trilogy will most directly argue from.

# **M.1 The Majority Smart-City User-GROUP — Profile**

The majority Smart-City User-GROUP, in the architecture's reference case, has the following profile. Numbers are illustrative; jurisdictions deploy at the scales that fit them, with the Cooperative / Municipal / Regional sidebars throughout the Manual marking the variations. * Population: approximately 10,000 Civigens of Record at full enrollment. Phase 1 typically begins with a subset (1,500–3,000 in the first six months) and grows toward full enrollment over the first two years.

* Sites: 5–10 enrollment sites distributed for accessibility (target: every Civigen within 2 km of an enrollment site); 1 Council chamber; 1 sealed-room facility (or shared lease in an existing secure facility); approximately 1,000 THOW units (one per ten Civigens) provisioned for crisis response and migration buffer; HFVN access for every Civigen at no cost.

* Affiliation: at least one Dual Recognition Compact with another User-GROUP within the first 18 months of Phase 1, for the cross-border arrangements that test the architecture's affiliation mechanics.

* SCALULAR providers: 10–30 institutions holding Verification Authority by end of Year 1, covering HEALTH, LAW, PROTECTION, and SKILLS\_TRADE tiers. # **M.3 Daily Operation — The Civigen's Year in the User-GROUP**

This section walks through the typical Civigen's year inside the Smart-City User-GROUP. It is operational rather than aspirational; it describes what daily life looks like, what touchpoints with the architecture occur, what the rhythm of the year is, what the Civigen experiences as discrete events versus what runs continuously in the background.

## **M.3.1 The continuous experience**

Most of the Civigen's life inside the User-GROUP is continuous rather than event-driven. Three things run continuously:

* **UBI flow.** Gracechain UBI deposits arrive on a schedule the User-GROUP sets — daily, weekly, or monthly. The majority Smart-City case is monthly UBI plus weekly Patriot Dividend (where deployed). The Civigen sees the deposit in their account on the published schedule, and the deposit is automatic; no action is required. UBI never depends on prior contribution, scoring, or behavior.

* **Submit-to-NPR availability.** The Civigen is continuously available to Submit — to be held accountable through Non-Punitive Remediation by their User-GROUP COI. The architectural commitment is 24/7 availability; the practical reality is that Submit events occur infrequently for most Civigens (typical Civigen experiences 0–3 NPR cycles in a typical year), but the availability is constant. A Civigen who is unavailable for Submit (traveling, ill, in crisis) notifies the COI and arrangements are made.

* **Resonance contribution.** The Civigen contributes to resonance through their daily activities — caregiving, work, learning, ecological stewardship, civic participation. Most contributions are not separately Claimed; they accumulate as the background substrate of the Civigen's life. # **M.4 Governance — How the User-GROUP Decides**

Governance inside the Smart-City User-GROUP runs on three layers, each with distinct authority and procedure. The layers compose so that no single body holds disproportionate power, and so that the Civigen experience of governance is bounded — most Civigens interact with governance only at predictable touchpoints, while the operational discipline runs continuously beneath the surface.

## **M.4.1 The algorithmic core layer**

The deepest governance layer is the algorithmic core. It executes the deterministic rules of UBIMIA — BaseCost calibration, CR setting, 60/40 carryforward, COI amortization, freshness-window enforcement, parameter calibration. The core has no discretion; its outputs are reproducible by any auditor with access to the same input data. The majority Smart-City User-GROUP experiences the algorithmic core as the rhythm of the year — prices change in January, settlement happens in December, and the core's computations are visible in published audit logs.

The Civigen's interaction with the algorithmic core is mostly passive. The core does its work; the Civigen sees the results in their balance, their Bills, their year-end statement. The Civigen does not vote on BaseCost values or on the conversion rate; those are computed, not legislated. This is by design — the algorithmic core is constitutional precisely so that no human authority can manipulate prices to extract or coerce.

## **M.4.2 The Council layer**

Above the algorithmic core sits the GAIA Council — the human supervisory body. The Smart-City Council has 9 members, selected by weighted lottery from Civigens with ΔR\_contributor \> 0.1, serving 2-year terms with half rotating each year. The Council convenes quarterly for regular sessions and extraordinarily as needed for time-sensitive matters.

What the Council can do (within authority): override an algorithmic core output by 2/3 majority and 30-day public notice when override is justified by documented failure of the rule under specific circumstances; resolve dissent in MIEVM ensemble standoffs; adjudicate disputes between Civigens; approve or reject Compassion Override applications; confer or revoke Verification Authority on institutions applying for Type C status; convene constitutional review.

What the Council cannot do: revise the deterministic rules executed by the algorithmic core (rule revision requires constitutional review, not Council action); revoke any Civigen's UBI for any reason (the survival floor is non-revocable); revise the Recognition Rule (that is the Adopting Community's authority, not the Council's); revise the Three Governing Principles or the Eight Immutable Ethical Principles or the Constitutional Pillars (those are non-revisable by any deployment); operate in secret (all Council decisions are public, with attributed votes and published rationales).

The Civigen's interaction with the Council is sporadic. Most Civigens never attend a Council session (sessions are public but most Civigens have other commitments); some Civigens are eligible for selection by lottery and may serve a term; a small number engage with the Council through dispute filings, recall petitions, or Override Proposal sponsorships. The Council's authority is bounded; its visibility is high; its capacity to operate is sustained by the Civigens' attention to its decisions, even when most Civigens do not actively participate.

## **M.4.3 The COI / Submit-to-NPR layer**

The most local governance layer is the User-GROUP COI itself, where Submit-to-NPR happens 24/7. This is the layer most Civigens interact with most frequently, and it is the layer most distinctive to UBIMIA — Council and algorithmic core have analogues in conventional governance, but Submit-to-NPR within COI is the architectural innovation that makes Civigen-level integrity sustainable.

Submit means the Civigen is voluntarily available to be held accountable, by their COI peers, through Non-Punitive Remediation. NPR is the discipline of working through what has happened — what was done, what was its impact, what would repair, what would prevent recurrence — without punishment, blame, or shame as the operative mode. The COI does the work; the Civigen submits to the work; the Instrument of Faith underwrites the binding so that Submit can be voluntary without becoming optional.

In the majority Smart-City User-GROUP, COIs are the size at which trust is operationally possible — typically 50–200 Civigens per COI, with a Smart-City of 10,000 organized into 50–100 COIs. COIs are voluntary affiliations; a Civigen chooses their COI, which means COIs vary in character (residential-block COIs, work-domain COIs, common-interest COIs, kinship-cluster COIs). GAIA approves each COI structure, ensuring that the COI satisfies basic conditions (size range, governance bylaws, NPR commitment, IOF underwriting) and is not a vehicle for evading accountability.

The Civigen's interaction with their COI is woven through daily life. Casual conversations with COI peers; periodic COI gatherings (typical: monthly); occasional Submit events when accountability matters; participation as a Submit witness for other COI members; coordination on contributions that cross multiple Civigens. The COI is where Famity (Pillar IV) lives most directly — Marriage at Heart at community scale, the WE/Uuu/SOUND-loop felt as a daily field condition, the literal-figurative-subjective binding made operationally tangible.

## **M.4.4 How the layers compose**

The three layers compose by separation of authority:

* Algorithmic core: deterministic; no discretion; constitutional parameter execution. Acts continuously.

* Council: discretionary at the override and adjudication boundary; bounded by what it cannot do. Acts quarterly and as needed.

* COI / Submit-to-NPR: relational; voluntary; underwritten by IOF. Acts continuously through the daily lives of Civigens.

A matter that arises is resolved at the lowest layer that can resolve it. Most matters resolve in COI-level NPR without ever reaching the Council. Some matters escalate to Council adjudication when COI cannot resolve. Very few matters reach the algorithmic core's revision-procedure layer — only constitutional questions about the rules themselves do that. The architecture's stability comes from this layering: when COIs do their NPR work, Councils have less to adjudicate; when Councils adjudicate well, the algorithmic core's parameters need less revision; when parameters are stable, the Civigen experience is predictable, which makes COI work and Council deliberation easier in turn.

# **M.5 Affiliation — How User-GROUPs Connect**

A Smart-City User-GROUP does not exist in isolation. Affiliation with other User-GROUPs is constitutional and operationally consequential. This section specifies the affiliation patterns that the majority User-GROUP uses, the architectural commitments affiliation honors, and the practical procedures for forming and maintaining affiliated relationships.

## **M.5.1 Why affiliation is constitutional**

Three reasons make affiliation non-optional:

* **The substrate is one.** Pillar I (Bioenergetic Imperative) commits the architecture to recognizing one substrate of all life. A User-GROUP that operates as if its substrate ends at its border violates this commitment. Watersheds cross borders; air masses cross borders; ecological cycles cross borders; AI.RE breath crosses borders. Affiliation is how User-GROUPs honor the one-substrate commitment operationally.

* **Civigens migrate.** Civigens move between User-GROUPs over their lifetimes — for work, for family, for education, for ecological reasons. The architecture's commitments to non-revocability of UBI and to recognition continuity require affiliation procedures (Dual Recognition Compacts) that allow Civigens to move without losing standing.

* **The KERNEL is singular.** The Global Actuary Investor Authority operates one actuarial table over one substrate. User-GROUPs that affiliate are honoring the singularity by coordinating their operations; User-GROUPs that refuse to affiliate are implicitly forking the KERNEL, which is permitted but is a much larger commitment than maintaining affiliation.

## **M.5.2 The Dual Recognition Compact**

The basic affiliation instrument is the Dual Recognition Compact — a written agreement between two User-GROUPs specifying the cross-jurisdiction arrangements. The majority Smart-City User-GROUP signs at least one Compact within 18 months of Phase 1 commencement; many sign three or four over the course of Phase 1\.

Dual Recognition Compacts cover at minimum:

* Civigen migration. A Civigen who relocates from User-GROUP A to User-GROUP B has their record transferred, with prior ΔR\_contributor preserved at 50% per the standard re-entry rule (or higher under Compact-specified terms).

* Cross-border SCALULAR access. A Civigen of User-GROUP A traveling within User-GROUP B's jurisdiction can access SCALULAR services from User-GROUP B's authorized providers, with reimbursement settled between the COI pools of the two User-GROUPs at published rates.

* Verification Authority recognition. Institutions holding Verification Authority in User-GROUP A may issue Type C verifications for User-GROUP B Civigens visiting or contributing in User-GROUP A's jurisdiction.

* MIEVM ensemble coordination. The two User-GROUPs may share MIEVM ensemble attestations for cross-jurisdiction Claims, reducing duplicated ensemble work.

* Conflict resolution. Disputes that cross the User-GROUP boundary are resolved by joint Council session (one council from each User-GROUP, equal-weighted), with appeal to a third User-GROUP's Council if joint session deadlocks.

## **M.5.3 Affiliation patterns**

Three patterns of affiliation occur in practice; the majority Smart-City User-GROUP typically uses all three over time:

* **Adjacent affiliation.** With User-GROUPs in geographically neighboring jurisdictions. Most directly serves Civigen migration and shared-substrate concerns (watersheds, regional air quality). The first Compact a Smart-City User-GROUP typically signs is with an adjacent User-GROUP.

* **Networked affiliation.** With non-adjacent User-GROUPs that share characteristics (similar size, similar Pilot Jurisdiction structure, similar SCALULAR provider mix). Networked affiliations support practice-sharing, Council-procedure benchmarking, and MIEVM ensemble coordination across geographic distance.

* **Tiered affiliation.** With User-GROUPs at different scales — a Smart-City User-GROUP affiliates with a smaller Cooperative User-GROUP within or near its jurisdiction (downward) and with a larger Regional User-GROUP that contains it (upward). Tiered affiliations provide the operational pathways through which the architecture scales without requiring centralized coordination.

## **M.5.4 The affiliation network and the broader BEE**

The set of all affiliated User-GROUPs forms an affiliation network. The network is not centralized — there is no master registry, no head-User-GROUP, no governing body for the network as a whole. Third, the timescale of the architecture's self-dissolution: the merit layer must have time to do its work and fade; sub-century timelines are too short for the calibration of the discount band's tightening; multi-millennial timelines are too long for the architecture to commit to specifically.

The 1,000 years is therefore not arbitrary. It is the smallest horizon that gives substrate-process room to work, the largest horizon that institutional continuity can plausibly span, and the right horizon for the merit layer's transitional purpose. Within the 1,000-year horizon, UBIMIA commits to the trajectory toward equality of standing for all human beings. **PILLAR III**

## **III.2 The evaluation formula**

Every merit-bearing flow under UBIMIA is evaluated by:

**Judicial \= SEPLTA-$\_HE × MDCCLXXVI-Action\_Reaction \+ P³\_Cause-Effect**

Three components, each carrying constitutional weight:

### **SEPLTA-$\_HE — six-vector financialization assessment**

SEPLTA-$\_HE is the six SEPLTA vectors (Social, Economic, Political, Legal, Technical, Administrative) evaluated against the $\_HE term, the financialization-of-existence operator. The $\_HE term names the pattern by which existence becomes financialized — the slow conversion of substrate-participation into monetary-measurable units, until the units become the measurement and the substrate becomes invisible. SEPLTA-$\_HE asks: across all six vectors, does this flow preserve SELF against $ELF, or does it accelerate $ELF at SELF's expense?

Each vector engages a specific failure mode:

* Social-$\_HE: does the flow strengthen relational substrate (kinship, community, mutual aid) or replace it with transactional substitution (paid services replacing relational care)?

* Economic-$\_HE: does the flow circulate within Bio-Ecologic Economy logic (recycling, substrate-honoring) or extract from substrate into financial pools that do not return to BEE?

* Political-$\_HE: does the flow strengthen political agency (governance participation, collective decision capacity) or convert political agency into purchasable influence?

* Legal-$\_HE: does the flow operate within legal frames that protect substrate (Common Core access, civil liberties, environmental protections) or undermine them through arbitrage?

* Technical-$\_HE: does the flow use technical capacity in service of substrate (BEE infrastructure, BERA measurement, MIEVM validation) or in service of $ELF maximization (extraction algorithms, attention economies, behavioral modification)?

* Administrative-$\_HE: does the flow strengthen administrative capacity to honor constitutional commitments, or convert administration into bureaucratic extraction (rent-seeking, gatekeeping, opacity)?

### **MDCCLXXVI-Action\_Reaction — paired-move equilibration**

MDCCLXXVI is the year 1776, the founding moment of the United States Constitution and the year in which \*all men are created equal\* was committed to a society that had not yet earned the words. The Action\_Reaction discipline, paired with MDCCLXXVI, names the requirement that every action that pulls toward $ELF must produce a reaction that pulls back toward SELF. The architecture is honest about $ELF's persistent presence — $ELF cannot be banished by fiat — but the architecture commits to equilibration: any flow that increases $ELF must be paired with a flow that increases SELF in equal or greater measure, so that the net trajectory remains toward SELF-preservation across the long arc.

Operationally, MDCCLXXVI-Action\_Reaction is checked at the authorization layer. A merit-bearing flow that scores high on $ELF-acceleration must be accompanied by, or be part of a pattern that includes, SELF-restoring flows of comparable magnitude. A deployment whose merit flows trend systematically toward $ELF without paired SELF-restoring flows is operating against Pillar III, regardless of how its individual flows score.

### **P³\_Cause-Effect — three-horizon discipline**

P³ is the cubic Cause-Effect operator. P³ takes immediate cause-effect, mid-term cause-effect, and long-term cause-effect together; only when all three confirm trajectory-favorable outcomes does the flow authorize. Short-term gain at long-term cost fails P³. Data-Integrity (the Currency volume of the Trilogy) ensures that P³ inputs are verifiable across all three horizons; without Data-Integrity's discipline, P³ would collapse into asserted progress without verifiable substrate.

P³'s three horizons are operationally defined:

* Immediate horizon: the flow's direct effect within 7 days. Did the contribution actually occur? Did the verification arrive? Did the BERA measurement register the change?

* Mid-term horizon: the flow's effect within 1 year. Did the contribution's effect persist? Did the resonance gain hold or decay? Did the verification stand up to dispute?

* Long-term horizon: the flow's effect within the trajectory. Does the contribution support the 1,000-year horizon's destination? Does it accelerate or retard the merit layer's eventual dissolution? Does it deepen substrate-coupling or extract from it?

## **5.2 Why four pillars rather than three or five**

Three pillars (Executive, Legislative, Judicial without Famity) is the inherited structure. Five pillars or more proliferate without structural necessity. The four-branch parallel provides the minimum complete set: action (Executive), rule-setting (Legislative), evaluation (Judicial), binding (Famity). Adding a fifth — say, Education or Defense — would identify a function that one of the four already covers (Education within Famity's binding-across-generations; Defense within Executive's substrate-protection). Four is sufficient and architecturally clean.
