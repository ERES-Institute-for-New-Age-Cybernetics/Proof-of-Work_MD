# **PREAMBLE**

The Resolution Protocol (hereinafter “ResProtocol”) establishes the formal procedural architecture through which the ERES Institute for New Age Cybernetics adjudicates disputes, reconciles systemic tensions, and produces binding governance determinations across all domains of the New Age Cybernetics (NAC) framework. This document specifies the mechanisms by which conflicts—whether interpersonal, institutional, jurisdictional, or algorithmic—are surfaced, triaged, deliberated, and resolved in accordance with the three governing principles of the ERES body of work.

The ResProtocol is designed to operate at every tier of the GSSG planetary architecture (T1 through T8), to interface with the BERA four-index assessment system (ARI, ERI, RHC, RCI), and to respect the economic provisions of UBIMIA, the security architecture of IDIPITIS, the biometric integrity guarantees of FAVORS, and the governance logic of PlayNAC. It is not a standalone instrument but rather a connective procedural layer that binds the entire NAC operational corpus into a coherent adjudicative whole.

The moral vector of this protocol is not neutral. Consistent with the ERES epistemology, ResProtocol is oriented toward BEST, SOUND, and GOOD end-outcomes—meaning that procedural fairness alone is insufficient; outcomes must demonstrably serve the long-term welfare of individuals, communities, and planetary systems alike.

# **1\. SCOPE AND AUTHORITY**

The Resolution Protocol governs all dispute resolution proceedings initiated under, or adjudicated by, any entity operating within the NAC governance framework. Its authority derives from the ERES Triune Cybernetic Framework and is expressed through the following jurisdictional layers:

## **1.1 Jurisdictional Domain**

**Tier 1 (Individual/Citizen).** Disputes between SSSC-credentialed citizens regarding personal rights, SCALULAR certification disagreements, UBIMIA benefit allocation, and EarnedPath milestone adjudication.

**Tier 2 (Community/Municipal).** Conflicts between community governance bodies, local PlayNAC nodes, resource allocation disputes within SECUIR infrastructure zones, and inter-neighborhood SOMT coordination failures.

**Tier 3 (Regional/Provincial).** Cross-jurisdictional tensions between regional governance units, Meritcoin/Gracechain valuation disputes, NBERS eligibility determinations, and CBGMODD data-integrity challenges.

**Tier 4–T8 (National through Planetary).** Macro-scale governance reconciliation including inter-state treaty interpretation under NAC principles, GAIA environmental coordination disputes, VERTECA energy architecture conflicts, and planetary-tier GSSG material allocation adjudication.

## **1.2 Authority Chain**

The ResProtocol derives binding authority from the following hierarchy, in descending precedence:

1. **The Three Governing Principles** (Don’t hurt yourself. Don’t hurt others. Build for generations to come.)

2. **The ERES Triune Math** — serving as the axiomatic constraint set against which all resolutions are validated.

3. **The BERA Index Architecture** — providing quantitative measurement of resolution impact across ARI (Adaptive Resilience Index), ERI (Economic Resilience Index), RHC (Resonant Harmony Cycle), and RCI (Resonant Continuity Index).

4. **PlayNAC Governance Logic** — the procedural substrate through which deliberation occurs.

5. **The applicable GSSG tier-specific regulations** governing the jurisdictional domain in which the dispute originates.

# **2\. CORE PRINCIPLES OF RESOLUTION**

Every proceeding under the ResProtocol shall be governed by the following principles, which are non-negotiable and supersede all procedural technicalities:

## **2.1 The Principle of Non-Harm Primacy**

No resolution may be issued that increases measurable harm to any party, bystander population, or downstream system. Harm is assessed via the BERA indices: a resolution that improves ARI for one party but degrades RHC for the broader community is presumptively invalid and must be remanded for recalibration.

## **2.2 The Principle of Generational Stewardship**

Resolutions must be evaluated not only against their immediate impact but against their projected influence across the 1000-Year Future Map timeline. A resolution that resolves a present conflict but creates structural fragility in Phase 2 (2075–2125) or beyond is deficient and must incorporate mitigating provisions.

## **2.3 The Principle of Epistemic Transparency**

Consistent with the ERES epistemology—which holds that the extraction of private epistemology into public record constitutes the sharing of true wealth—all resolution proceedings shall produce a public reasoning archive. The deliberative logic, evidentiary basis, BERA impact projections, and dissenting positions shall be documented and made accessible through SOMT coordination channels. No resolution may rest on undisclosed reasoning.

## **2.4 The Principle of Cybernetic Feedback**

Every resolution is provisional in the cybernetic sense: it generates systemic feedback that must be monitored and incorporated. The ResProtocol mandates post-resolution review cycles at 90-day, 1-year, and 5-year intervals, during which the BERA indices of all affected parties and systems are reassessed. If a resolution produces unintended negative feedback exceeding defined thresholds, automatic remand procedures are triggered.

# **3\. PROCEDURAL ARCHITECTURE**

The Resolution Protocol operates through a seven-phase procedural architecture. Each phase is mandatory and sequential; no phase may be bypassed except under emergency provisions defined in Section 6\.

| Phase | Designation | Function |
| :---: | ----- | ----- |
| I | **SURFACING** | Formal identification and registration of the dispute through SOMT intake channels. Assignment of dispute classification (interpersonal, institutional, jurisdictional, algorithmic, environmental). |
| II | **TRIAGE** | BERA-indexed assessment of dispute severity, systemic impact scope, and tier classification. Assignment of adjudicative body and procedural track (expedited, standard, or deliberative). |
| III | **MEDIATION** | Structured dialogue facilitated by trained PlayNAC mediators. Parties present positions, evidence, and proposed resolutions. Mediators apply Triune Math validation to all proposals. |
| IV | **DELIBERATION** | If mediation fails to produce consensus, the dispute escalates to a deliberative panel constituted per GSSG tier-appropriate composition rules. Panel conducts independent BERA impact modeling. |
| V | **RESOLUTION** | Issuance of binding determination. Resolution must satisfy all four BERA indices above minimum thresholds and pass Triune Math constraint validation. Dissenting opinions are formally recorded. |
| VI | **IMPLEMENTATION** | Operationalization of the resolution through applicable governance channels: UBIMIA adjustments, SCALULAR re-certification, SECUIR infrastructure modification, Meritcoin/Gracechain ledger entries, or other domain-specific mechanisms. |
| VII | **FEEDBACK** | Post-resolution monitoring and cybernetic feedback integration. BERA indices tracked at 90-day, 1-year, and 5-year intervals. Automatic remand triggered if indices fall below prescribed thresholds. |

# **4\. BERA INDEX INTEGRATION**

The BERA four-index architecture serves as the quantitative backbone of the Resolution Protocol. Every resolution must be evaluated against all four indices, and the following minimum thresholds must be satisfied for a resolution to be considered valid:

## **4.1 Index Thresholds**

| Index | Full Name | Minimum Threshold |
| :---: | ----- | ----- |
| **ARI** | Adaptive Resilience Index | Resolution must not reduce any party’s ARI below their pre-dispute baseline by more than 5%. Net system ARI must be non-negative. |
| **ERI** | Economic Resilience Index | Resolution must demonstrate positive or neutral ERI trajectory for all parties within one UBIMIA distribution cycle. Redistributive provisions must not create new dependency cascades. |
| **RHC** | Resonant Harmony Cycle | Community-level RHC must remain within one standard deviation of pre-dispute mean. Resolutions that produce social fracture exceeding this threshold require mandatory reconciliation addenda. |
| **RCI** | Resonant Continuity Index | Per RCI \= P\_Ω\_norm × ARI\_sys × VibConst, the resolution must preserve or enhance systemic continuity. Any resolution that reduces RCI below 0.7 of the pre-dispute value triggers automatic escalation to the next GSSG tier. |

## **4.2 Triune Math Constraint Validation**

All proposed resolutions are subjected to Triune Math constraint validation. The three canonical formulas serve as boundary conditions:

* C \= R×P/M — Cybernetics \= Resource × Purpose ÷ Method. The resolution must allocate resources proportionate to the stated purpose, and the method of implementation must not exceed the resource-purpose ratio by more than 15%.

* M×E+C \= R — Matter × Energy \+ Constant \= Reason. The material and energetic costs of the resolution, plus systemic constants, must produce a reasonable outcome. Resolutions that fail this test are classified as “unsound” and returned to Phase III.

* REAL \= (E·M·R)/(T·S) — Energy · Matter · Resonance over Time · Space. The resolution’s real-world impact is assessed against the spatiotemporal constraints of its implementation domain. Resolutions with REAL values below the tier-appropriate floor are considered unimplementable.

# **5\. ADJUDICATIVE BODIES**

The Resolution Protocol establishes three tiers of adjudicative authority, each constituted according to the GSSG tier in which the dispute originates:

## **5.1 PlayNAC Mediators (Tiers 1–2)**

Community-level mediators credentialed through SCALULAR’s SSLA (Solid-State Smart-City Law) certification pathway. Mediators must hold a minimum ARI of 0.75 and must have completed the EarnedPath mediation specialization. They operate Phase III proceedings and may issue non-binding recommendations. If mediation produces consensus, the mediator’s recommendation is ratified automatically and proceeds to Phase VI.

## **5.2 Deliberative Panels (Tiers 3–5)**

Multi-member panels convened from regional and national governance pools. Panel composition is algorithmically balanced using FAVORS biometric integrity verification to ensure no conflicts of interest, demographic bias, or jurisdictional capture. Panels have binding authority and issue formal resolutions subject to BERA threshold compliance. Dissenting members produce written opinions that are incorporated into the public reasoning archive.

## **5.3 Planetary Reconciliation Council (Tiers 6–8)**

The highest adjudicative body under the Resolution Protocol, convened only for disputes with planetary-scale systemic implications. The Council operates under enhanced transparency provisions and its proceedings are simultaneously archived across all SOMT/GAIA coordination channels. Council resolutions must satisfy BERA thresholds at the planetary aggregate level and are subject to automatic review under the 1000-Year Future Map projection framework.

# **6\. EMERGENCY PROVISIONS**

The standard seven-phase architecture may be compressed under emergency conditions, defined as circumstances in which delay would produce irreversible harm exceeding defined BERA thresholds. Emergency provisions are subject to the following constraints:

* Phases I–III may be compressed into a single Emergency Surfacing and Triage procedure, but Phase V (Resolution) may not be bypassed under any circumstances.

* Emergency resolutions carry a mandatory 30-day post-resolution review (accelerated from the standard 90-day cycle) and are subject to automatic remand if any BERA index deviates more than 10% from projection.

* The authority to invoke emergency provisions rests with the GSSG tier-appropriate governance body and requires a two-thirds supermajority. Unilateral invocation is prohibited.

* All emergency proceedings must produce a full public reasoning archive within 72 hours of resolution issuance.

# **7\. ALGORITHMIC DISPUTE RESOLUTION**

A distinct class of disputes arises from algorithmic decision-making within the NAC framework—including UBIMIA allocation algorithms, FAVORS biometric matching, Meritcoin/Gracechain smart contracts, and IDIPITIS security clearance determinations. Algorithmic disputes are subject to additional procedural requirements:

## **7.1 Algorithmic Audit**

Any party may request an algorithmic audit of any automated decision that affects their BERA indices. The audit is conducted by CBGMODD-certified data integrity auditors who produce a transparent trace of the decision logic, input data, and output determination. The audit report is entered into the public reasoning archive.

## **7.2 Human Override Provision**

No algorithmic determination within the NAC framework is final until reviewed by a credentialed human adjudicator. This provision is absolute and non-waivable. The human override serves as the epistemic safeguard against systemic algorithmic bias and ensures that the moral vector of the ERES epistemology—oriented toward BEST, SOUND, and GOOD outcomes—is preserved even when automated systems produce technically valid but ethically deficient results.

# **8\. MIEVM VALIDATION INTEGRATION**

The Multi-Instrument Ensemble Validation Method (MIEVM) serves as a meta-procedural layer within the Resolution Protocol. Complex resolutions—particularly those affecting multiple GSSG tiers or involving novel legal or governance questions—are subjected to MIEVM analysis, in which multiple independent analytical instruments (including AI systems operating as parallel validation nodes) assess the resolution’s soundness from diverse epistemic perspectives.

MIEVM validation produces a convergence score indicating the degree of agreement across instruments. Resolutions with convergence scores below 0.65 are flagged for enhanced human deliberation. MIEVM reports are appended to the public reasoning archive and serve as a permanent record of the epistemic rigor applied to the resolution.

# **9\. APPEALS AND REMAND**

Any party to a resolved dispute may file a formal appeal within 60 days of resolution issuance. Appeals are adjudicated by the next-higher GSSG tier body and are limited to the following grounds:

6. **Procedural Deficiency.** A mandatory phase was improperly conducted or bypassed without valid emergency authorization.

7. **BERA Threshold Violation.** The resolution demonstrably fails to meet the minimum index thresholds specified in Section 4.1.

8. **Triune Math Constraint Failure.** The resolution violates one or more of the three canonical formulas as applied in Section 4.2.

9. **New Evidence.** Material evidence not available at the time of original deliberation has emerged that would substantially alter the resolution’s BERA impact profile.

10. **Generational Impact Discovery.** Post-resolution analysis reveals that the determination creates structural fragility in a Future Map phase not previously assessed.

Remanded resolutions return to Phase III (Mediation) unless the appeals body specifies a different re-entry point. The original deliberative panel is replaced to ensure fresh perspective.

# **10\. PUBLIC REASONING ARCHIVE**

The Resolution Protocol mandates the creation and perpetual maintenance of a Public Reasoning Archive for every adjudicated dispute. The Archive constitutes the extracted epistemology of the resolution process—transforming private deliberation into public knowledge in accordance with the ERES principle that the sharing of reasoning is the sharing of true wealth.

Each Archive entry shall contain: the original dispute filing; all evidentiary submissions; BERA impact assessments (pre-dispute, projected, and post-resolution); Triune Math constraint validation reports; mediator or panel deliberative summaries; dissenting opinions; MIEVM validation reports (where applicable); and the full text of the binding resolution. Archives are maintained through SOMT coordination channels and indexed via GAIA cataloguing for permanent accessibility.
