## Part I — TheirWay: Distillation of the Crisis

William Baldwin's Forbes article "They're Coming For Your Social Security" (March 28, 2026) lays bare six proposals for addressing the imminent bankruptcy of the U.S. Social Security system. Each proposal operates within the same scarcity framework. Each violates at least one of the Three Governing Principles. None addresses the root cause.

**The Facts as Stated:**
The Social Security trust fund will be empty by 2033 or earlier. Without legislative correction, all benefits — currently reaching approximately 71 million Americans at an average of $2,071/month — will be cut 23%. Baldwin observes that the system lacks sufficient financial integrity to qualify as a Ponzi scheme, and that what is approaching is better understood as bankruptcy.

**The Six Proposals — Diagnosed Through HPE DESCENT:**

**1. Postpone Retirement (to age 70).** Reduces the long-run deficit by 36–44%. This is the REACTIVE stage of HPE DESCENT — the system responds to insolvency by moving the goalposts. It violates SELF (Don't hurt yourself): forcing bodies to labor beyond their natural rhythm harms health, shortens remaining life quality, and punishes physically demanding occupations disproportionately.

**2. Tax the Rich.** Eliminate the wage cap on the 12.4% payroll tax, or impose a 12.4% surtax on investment income above $500,000. Reduces the deficit by 48–67%. This is the FLAT stage — flattening wealth differences without creating new value. It does not hurt the principle of OTHERS directly, but it treats redistribution as a permanent solution to a structural failure. The $500,000 floor would not be inflation-adjusted, meaning eventually *everyone* pays — a VEILED mechanism.

**3. Tax Everybody.** Raise the payroll tax from 12.4% to 16.4% immediately. Baldwin acknowledges this would accelerate automation-driven job loss and trigger recession. This is the YIELDLESS stage — extracting more from a system already under strain produces negative returns for workers.

**4. Shrink Starting Benefits.** Replace wage-indexed benefit calculations with price-indexed calculations for those born after 1969. Reduces the deficit by 74%. This is the VEILED stage — the cut is hidden inside an indexing formula change that most beneficiaries will never understand until their first check arrives.

**5. Shrink Succeeding Benefits.** Cut 1% from the annual cost-of-living adjustment, meaning purchasing power decays by 1% every year of retirement. Affects *everyone already receiving benefits*. This is the UNTESTED stage — no one has modeled the cascading effects of compounding poverty among the elderly over 20–30 year retirements.

**6. Eliminate Earned Benefits.** Replace all Social Security benefits with a flat payout at 150% of the poverty line. Thirty-five years of contributions vanish. Everyone gets the same amount. This is the terminal stage of DESCENT — the system destroys the relationship between contribution and reward entirely.

**Baldwin's Own Conclusion (paraphrased):** Congress will likely blend tax increases falling mostly on higher earners with benefit cuts mostly affecting future retirees. The dominant legislative strategies are debt (buy votes now, pay later) and redistribution (take from those who worked and saved, give to those who did not).

**The ERES Diagnosis:** All six proposals share a single epistemological failure — they treat the human being as a *cost to be managed* rather than a *resonance to be tuned*. The system's "bankruptcy" is not a financial event. It is a moral event. The system was designed in 1935 when life expectancy was 61 years and the worker-to-retiree ratio was 42:1. It was never built for generations. It was built for budgets. That is why it is failing.
