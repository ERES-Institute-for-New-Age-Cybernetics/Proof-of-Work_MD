# **3\. The Royalty — Core Term**

| Term | Definition |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Royalty Rate** | 1% (one percent) of Gross Revenue |
| **Royalty Base** | Gross Revenue: all revenue received by the commercial deployment vehicle from any deployment, licensing, subscription, transaction fee, or service contract that incorporates ERES-derived protocols — calculated before deduction of any expenses, taxes, or distributions |
| **Payment Frequency** | Monthly — payable within 15 days of month-end close. Negotiable to quarterly if operationally justified, subject to mutual agreement. |
| **Recipient** | The ERES Fiduciary (not Joseph personally). The Fiduciary distributes to beneficiaries according to the terms of the Fiduciary instrument. |
| **Duration** | Perpetual — for as long as any ERES-derived protocol is in commercial use by the Partner or any successor entity. The royalty does not expire, does not have a sunset clause, and does not terminate upon the death of Joseph A. Sprute. |
| **Minimum Floor** | To be negotiated. Suggested: a minimum annual royalty payment during any year in which the license is active, regardless of revenue generated. Protects the Fiduciary during ramp-up periods. |
| **Audit Right** | The ERES Fiduciary retains the right to audit the Partner’s gross revenue records annually, at ERES’s cost, using an independent accountant acceptable to both parties. |

The royalty rate is not a negotiating position. It is the covenant term — the recognition that ERES brings a 14-year, fully specified architecture that the Partner could not have built and cannot build without the Licensor’s ongoing stewardship. What is negotiable is payment frequency, minimum floor, and audit mechanics.

# **5\. Evaporation Clause**

If the royalty stream grows to excess — beyond what the Fiduciary beneficiaries reasonably require — the family definition of beneficiaries expands in proportion to absorb it. Excess does not accumulate indefinitely; it flows outward.

If the royalty stream becomes a burden on the Fiduciary beneficiaries (through administrative complexity, legal exposure, or any other mechanism), the royalty evaporates upon confirmation that its true value has been fully compensated to Dalia and the children.

These are covenant terms, not standard commercial clauses. They are included here so that the Partner understands the nature of the relationship being entered: this is not a financial instrument seeking maximum extraction. It is an arrangement designed to sustain a life and honour a covenant.
