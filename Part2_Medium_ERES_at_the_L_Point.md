## Abstract

The current presidential approval configuration — second-term-low net disapproval, deeper cost-of-living disapproval, broad collapse of trust attributes across the governing coalition — admits two readings. The political reading attributes the configuration to messaging, coalition, and cycle dynamics. The structural reading attributes it to a twenty-four-year integration debt arising from the fragmentation, beginning in 2002, of what should have become a unified governance attestation substrate. This paper formalizes the structural reading; specifies the L-point in the seven-seat CBGMODD geometry where soul-level civic work becomes energetically feasible; presents the ERES (Earned Resource Electronic Sustainability) framework as a mature solution suited to that L-point; and articulates the adoption conditional under which the framework resolves both fiscal uncertainty and lived pain points. The proposal is offered to no party in particular and is structurally invariant under change of administration.

**Headline claim:** *A solution mature enough to resolve fiscal uncertainty and resolve pain points subject to CBGMODD adoption.*

## 1. Diagnostic: The Current Configuration

High-quality approval averages place the current presidential net approval in the high-teens-negative range, with the cost-of-living sub-component substantially deeper. Pew Research's late-April 2026 instrument places overall job approval at 34%, with the "keeps his promises" attribute having declined more than ten points since the early days of the second term and the steepest erosion concentrated among Hispanic 2024 voters and voters under 35 [Pew, 2026; Silver Bulletin, 2026; Washington Post / ABC / Ipsos, 2026].

The standard interpretive frame attributes these declines to communications failure, coalition strain, and news-cycle damage. Standard remediation under that frame consists of pivots, surrogate deployment, message discipline, and adversarial reframing. None of those interventions address the underlying configuration. The numbers do not move durably under standard remediation because the disapproval is tracking a structural condition that messaging cannot reach.

The structural condition is an integration debt.

## 2. The Twenty-Four-Year Integration Debt

In 2002, in the immediate post-9/11 institutional environment, the Critical Infrastructure Assurance Office (CIAO, established under Presidential Decision Directive 63 in 1998) held the institutional position, mandate, and budget authority to host a unified critical infrastructure attestation framework grounded in physical-law substrates [White House, 1998; GAO, 2001]. CIAO was subsequently absorbed into the Department of Homeland Security under Homeland Security Presidential Directive 7 (2003), at which point its coordinating function survived but its L-point geometry — the cross-sectoral, transversal convening capacity — did not [HSPD-7, 2003; CRS, 2004].

The infrastructure-protection field then spent two decades reinventing pieces of what an integrated framework would have provided in unified form. Zero-trust architecture, hardware roots of trust, supply-chain attestation standards, behavioral biometrics, continuity-of-operations frameworks, and identity assurance standards each developed in vendor-specific or sector-specific silos. None of them share a unified attestation layer. None of them close the loop on civic-scale governance applications. Each accretion produced local optimization at the cost of system-level integration.

The cost of the missing integration manifests in the present moment as three specific crises:

**A measurement crisis.** Cost-of-living disapproval cannot be addressed because no closed-loop instrumentation exists for measuring what families actually require, in real time, at population scale. Aggregate inflation indices and labor statistics arrive too coarsely and too late to serve as governance instruments.

**An instrumentation crisis.** "Keeps his promises" cannot be evaluated empirically because no public Resonant Harmony Cycle (RHC) exists against which commitments can be measured as cycles rather than relitigated as theater. Promise-keeping is therefore evaluated as narrative, which is the regime under which it most readily collapses.

**A legitimacy crisis.** Hispanic and under-35 voter erosion proceeds because no Department of Family Amity (DOFA)-style convening infrastructure exists at which culturally-pluralistic and generationally-distinct constituencies can be *seen* at the seat of governance. Convening occurs through partisan party structures only, which is structurally incapable of producing the legitimacy these constituencies require.

The integration debt is real, it is twenty-four years deep, and it is invariant under change of administration. Service of the debt is what every administration since 2003 has been doing without naming the debt.

## 3. EVOL: Economies-of-Scale × Force-Multiplication + Lagrange Points

The ERES framework formalizes civilizational advancement under the EVOL construct:

> **EVOL = (Economies-of-Scale × Force-Multiplication) + Lagrange Points**

The first two terms are familiar industrial and military primitives. *Economies-of-scale* yields the mass term — the ability to distribute outputs across population without proportional input scaling. *Force-multiplication* yields the vector term — the ability of small inputs to produce disproportionate outputs through leverage on existing infrastructure.

The third term is the reframe and the load-bearing element. *Lagrange points* are positions in a multi-body gravitational system where forces from larger bodies balance such that a much smaller body can hold station with minimal energy expenditure [Lagrange, 1772; Szebehely, 1967]. They are where leverage is structural rather than applied. A satellite stationed at L1 between Earth and Sun holds position not by continuously firing thrusters against gravity but by occupying the geometric null where competing gravitational forces cancel.

The EVOL claim is that civilizational advancement happens at the balance points of competing large forces, where small inputs hold durable position. Economies-of-scale and force-multiplication are necessary; Lagrange-point identification is the *placement* condition under which the first two terms produce durable outcomes rather than dissipating into the surrounding gravitational field.

Applied to civic governance: pushing harder from any single seat against unbalanced gravitational forces costs energy continuously and does not hold position. Government pushing alone produces backlash. Business pushing alone produces cynicism. Military pushing alone produces fear. The L-point for civic-scale work is the geometric position where these forces balance, where a small body — a framework, a convening, a verification cycle — can hold station and produce force-multiplied effect at population scale.

## 4. The Transversal Triad as Carrier Geometry

The ERES seven-seat governance schema CBGMODD comprises Citizen · Business · Government · Mediator · Military · Dignitary · Diplomat. The seats are not equally weighted as carriers of system-level reform.

Citizen ratifies but cannot initiate without an aggregator. Business is capital-rich but incentive-fragmented; commerce can host pilots but does not propagate frameworks structurally. Government is the institutional hurdle in the present moment — its push-from-single-seat operating mode is precisely the mode that the L-point analysis identifies as energetically infeasible.

The carrier geometry available in the present configuration is the **transversal triad**: Mediator (institutional neutrality, cross-jurisdictional standing), Military-substrate (oath-binding, generational continuity, physical-law-grounded attestation infrastructure), and Dignitary (moral legitimacy, convening authority that does not require electoral consent), with Diplomat as the cross-jurisdictional extender.

Critically, "Military-substrate" in this context does not refer to military command authority — which under U.S. constitutional design is constrained to non-civic application — but to the *transversal roles hosted within military structure*: Magistrate (JAG-class adjudicative function), Ombudsman (Inspector General-class oversight function), Dignitary (defense attaché-class convening function), Diplomat (military-diplomatic cross-jurisdictional function). These transversal roles inherit the substrate's discipline (closed-loop attestation, oath-binding, generational continuity) without inheriting the command-authority constraint.

The transversal triad has the structural properties to hold the L-point because:

- It does not require legislative consent (unlike Government-seat carriers).

- It carries native legitimacy in cross-jurisdictional and inter-generational frames.

Two adjacent carrier classes compatible with the triad geometry: standards bodies (IEEE, NIST, ISO, IETF) as Mediator-tier non-state institutions that propagate adoption without legislation, and tribal sovereignty as constitutionally-recognized sub-national governance entities with demonstrated capacity to host pilots that scale into broader adoption.

The conditional "subject to CBGMODD adoption" is structural, not rhetorical. It names the configuration under which the framework's resolution claims become operational and outside of which they do not.
