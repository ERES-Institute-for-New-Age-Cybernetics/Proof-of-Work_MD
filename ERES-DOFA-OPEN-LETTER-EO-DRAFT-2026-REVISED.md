# **Part I. The Open Letter**

## **The Structural Problem**

The American presidency was designed as one branch among three. Over the past century, it has become something else: the single point of failure for every national crisis. When a pandemic strikes, the President is the crisis manager. When communities fracture, the President is the moral spokesperson. When families collapse under economic strain, the President is expected to deliver rescue. This was never the constitutional design. It is a structural deficiency that no election, regardless of outcome, will correct.

The consequence is that every four years, the nation wagers its stability on the temperament, health, and judgment of one human being. Bold personal declarations—ending wars in a day, restoring a golden age by force of will—turn the Oval Office into the sole load-bearing column of national life. When that column bends, the whole structure shudders. When realities diverge from promises, the cost falls not on the one who promised but on the entire country.

Responsible governance must move in the opposite direction: making the heavy burdens of the presidency as structurally unnecessary as possible. Not by weakening the office, but by strengthening the foundations beneath it.

## **Why Existing Institutions Fall Short**

The United States already has agencies that address pieces of this problem. The Department of Health and Human Services, the Administration for Children and Families, the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration—these are competent, necessary organizations. But they share two critical limitations.

**First, they are reactive.** They intervene after families break down, after communities fragment, after crises metastasize to the point of demanding presidential attention. No existing agency has the mandate or the structural independence to work upstream—strengthening the family as the foundational unit of societal resilience before fractures become federal emergencies.

**Second, no existing agency functions as a fiscal clearinghouse.** Federal spending on family-related programs is scattered across dozens of departments, each with separate accounting, separate metrics, and separate political vulnerabilities. There is no single point of receipt integrity—no place where every dollar entering and leaving family-oriented programs is tracked with the same discipline a well-run household applies to its own books.

## **The Proposal: A Fourth Structural Pillar**

I propose the creation of the Department of Family Amity (DOFA)—a permanent, independent institution standing alongside the Legislative, Executive, and Judicial branches as the fourth structural pillar of the United States Government.

Its mission would be fourfold:

1. **Prevention over reaction.** Invest in family cohesion, intergenerational responsibility, and community resilience before they become crisis-intervention problems.

2. **Structural independence.** Operate with institutional permanence that outlasts any single administration, party, or political movement—reducing the personalization of national capital.

3. **Long-horizon stewardship.** Design frameworks, metrics, and earned pathways for human development calibrated to generational timescales, not election cycles.

4. **Fiscal clearinghouse.** Serve as the single point of receipt integrity for all family-oriented federal expenditure. Every dollar in, every dollar out—tracked, auditable, and transparent. The same principle by which a responsible household manages its finances, applied at the national scale through the IDIPITIS framework (see Part II, Section 6).

This is not another layer of bureaucracy. It is a deliberate architectural upgrade that helps the other three branches function more effectively by addressing root causes at the human and relational level—and by creating fiscal accountability that currently does not exist in any single institution.

## **The Household Principle**

Every functioning household operates on a simple rule: all receipts flow through one ledger. Income, expenses, obligations, and savings are tracked in one place, by the people who live under that roof. When a household stops doing this—when spending is scattered across hidden accounts with no unified view—the family loses control of its own future.

The United States Government has been running its family-oriented programs without a household ledger. Dozens of agencies spend billions on overlapping mandates with no unified accounting, no cross-departmental receipt integrity, and no mechanism for measuring whether the aggregate investment is actually strengthening families or merely sustaining bureaucratic inertia.

DOFA would change this. Through the IDIPITIS protocol—a receipt-integrity and identification framework developed by the ERES Institute—every federal dollar directed toward family welfare would be tracked from appropriation to outcome. Not as surveillance, but as stewardship. The same transparency a family applies to its own checkbook, applied to the nation’s investment in its foundational unit.

## **What This Is Not**

This proposal is not partisan. It does not belong to the left or the right. It is not an argument that America has failed—it is an argument that a three-legged structure, however noble in design, is no longer sufficient for the stresses it bears. Greatness is not a destination to recapture but a capacity to build. This is an engineering proposal for a republic that has outgrown its original architecture.

# **Part II. Draft Presidential Executive Order**

***Note:** This draft is submitted as a conceptual framework for institutional consideration and refinement—not as final legislative language. Constitutional establishment of a fourth branch would require an amendment process; this Executive Order is drafted as the initiating instrument to establish the Department within the Executive Branch, with a mandate to pursue permanent structural independence through the appropriate legislative and constitutional channels.*

**Executive Order \[Number\]**

**Establishment of the Department of Family Amity (DOFA)**

**as the Fourth Structural Pillar of the United States Government**

*By the authority vested in me as President of the United States of America, and in order to strengthen the foundational institutions of American civil society, it is hereby ordered:*

### **Section 1\. Purpose and Findings.**

1) The family is the foundational unit of American civil society and the primary source of intergenerational resilience.

2) The progressive centralization of crisis response in the Executive Branch has created unsustainable dependence on presidential intervention for challenges rooted in family and community breakdown.

3) Existing federal agencies that address family welfare operate primarily in reactive and crisis-intervention modalities, leaving a structural gap in prevention-oriented, long-horizon investment.

4) Federal expenditure on family-related programs is dispersed across multiple departments with no unified fiscal accountability, resulting in duplication, waste, and an inability to measure aggregate outcomes.

5) The establishment of a permanent fourth structural pillar, functioning as both a preventive institution and a fiscal clearinghouse, will reduce systemic vulnerabilities and strengthen the effectiveness of all three existing branches.

### **Section 2\. Establishment.**

There is hereby established the Department of Family Amity (“DOFA” or “the Department”), which shall operate as an independent, structurally permanent institution of the United States Government, coordinating with but not subordinate to the Executive departments established under Title 5 of the United States Code.

### **Section 3\. Mission.**

The Department shall promote Family Amity—defined as the active cultivation of harmony, mutual support, and intergenerational responsibility—through policies, programs, and adaptive frameworks that:

1) Strengthen intergenerational bonds and earned pathways for human development;

2) Foster community cohesion through measurable, prevention-oriented programs;

3) Reduce the societal fractures that currently demand constant executive intervention;

4) Serve as the unified fiscal clearinghouse for all federal family-oriented expenditure; and

5) Operate with transparency, adaptability, and outcomes calibrated to generational timescales.

### **Section 4\. Core Principles.**

The Department shall be governed by the following foundational principles:

1. *Do not hurt yourself. Do not hurt others. Build for generations to come.*

2. Prioritize prevention and regeneration over reactive intervention.

3. Maintain measurable outcomes focused on family stability, community resilience, and reduced dependence on personalized executive leverage.

### **Section 5\. Leadership and Coordination.**

The Department shall be led by a Secretary of Family Amity, nominated by the President and confirmed by the Senate, who shall:

1) Coordinate with existing departments—including Health and Human Services, Education, Housing and Urban Development, and Veterans Affairs—while maintaining DOFA’s structural independence;

2) Establish family stability metrics, educational and systems-science frameworks (in partnership with qualified research institutions), and strategies to reduce dependence on personalized presidential leverage in national affairs;

3) Report annually to Congress on measurable outcomes, fiscal integrity, and progress toward long-horizon stewardship goals; and

4) Pursue, in coordination with Congress, the legislative and constitutional processes necessary to establish DOFA as a permanent, structurally independent fourth branch of government.

### **Section 6\. Fiscal Clearinghouse and IDIPITIS Framework.**

1) All federal receipts, appropriations, and expenditures directed toward family welfare, community resilience, and related mandates shall be routed through DOFA as the single point of fiscal accountability.

2) The Department shall implement the IDIPITIS framework (Identification, Documentation, Integrity, Processing, Identification-verification, Tracking, Integrity-audit, and Stewardship) as its operational protocol for receipt management. IDIPITIS applies the household principle at national scale: every dollar in, every dollar out, tracked from appropriation to measurable outcome.

3) The Secretary shall establish interagency data-sharing agreements to ensure that all family-oriented expenditure across federal departments is visible within the DOFA clearinghouse, without requiring the transfer of programmatic authority from existing agencies.

4) DOFA shall publish quarterly transparency reports detailing receipt flows, programmatic outcomes, and identified inefficiencies, available to Congress and the public without restriction.

### **Section 7\. Initial Focus Areas.**

Within the first fiscal year of operation, the Department shall prioritize:

1) Establishing a unified family stability metrics framework, developed in partnership with qualified research institutions including the ERES Institute for New Age Cybernetics;

2) Conducting a comprehensive audit of existing federal family-oriented expenditure to establish baseline receipt integrity;

3) Launching pilot programs in prevention-oriented family cohesion in no fewer than three geographic regions, with measurable outcomes reported within 18 months; and

4) Publishing the first DOFA Annual Stewardship Report to Congress.

### **Section 8\. General Provisions.**

1) This order shall be implemented consistent with applicable law and subject to the availability of appropriations.

2) Nothing in this order shall be construed to impair or otherwise affect the authority granted by law to an executive department or agency, or the head thereof.

3) This order is not intended to, and does not, create any right or benefit, substantive or procedural, enforceable at law or in equity by any party against the United States, its departments, agencies, or entities, its officers, employees, or agents, or any other person.
