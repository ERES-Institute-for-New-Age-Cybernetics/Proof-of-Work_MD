# **8\. MIEVM Epistemological Note**

The Multi-Instrument Ensemble Validation Method deployed four AI systems as parallel critique nodes, each bringing different architectural biases. The ensemble was culled from a larger original set due to solo-researcher bandwidth constraints—the same constraint-based epistemology that produced the 72 Key Domains in CyberRAVE (culled from the Register of Collective Indices based on TLD renewal capacity).

**MIEVM Disclosure:** All four instruments are large language models with overlapping training corpora and no independent clinical, financial, or regulatory standing. MIEVM functions as a coherence check and structured critique methodology—not external validation. Independent clinical review by qualified professionals is required before any investment or clinical decision.

**The MIEVM’s value is not in averaging scores but in identifying the lowest-scoring dimensions and resolving them specifically.** Grok’s 5/10 investability and DeepSeek’s 5.0/10 clinical feasibility in Round 1 identified the two most consequential gaps. Both were resolved by Round 2 (Grok → 8.5 investability; DeepSeek → 8.8 clinical realism). The remaining Round 2 gaps were executional, not structural.
