**PART I — THE DEPARTMENT OF FAMILY AMITY**

**Section 1\. *Establishment.***

**(a)** There is hereby established within the Executive Office of the President the **Office of Family Amity** (hereinafter “DOFA”), which shall serve as the principal coordinating body for all Federal programs, policies, and initiatives affecting the American family.

**(b)** DOFA shall operate as a permanent office with the status, authority, and resources necessary to coordinate across all departments and agencies of the Federal Government on matters of family wellbeing, intergenerational equity, and social harmony.

**(c)** The President shall, within 90 days of this Order, submit to Congress proposed legislation to establish DOFA as a Cabinet-level Department, with the Director serving as Secretary of Family Amity.

**Section 2\. *Mission and Mandate.***

**(a)** DOFA shall evaluate all Federal policies, proposed and existing, by the **Hundred-Year Question**: Does this policy strengthen or weaken the American family over the next century?

**(b)** DOFA shall develop and maintain the **National Family Wellbeing Index** (NFWI), a composite metric replacing GDP as the primary measure of national progress for family-related policy evaluation. The NFWI shall incorporate the four BERA Resonance Metrics: Aura Resonance Index (ARI), Emission Resonance Index (ERI), Resonant Harmony Cycle (RHC), and Resonant Continuity Index (RCI).

**(c)** DOFA shall coordinate with existing departments to eliminate duplication, close service gaps, and ensure that no American family falls between bureaucratic jurisdictions.

**(d)** DOFA shall establish a **Family Amity Corps** of trained mediators, counselors, and community coordinators deployed to every Congressional district, providing direct family support services free of charge at point of access.

**Section 3\. *DOFA Governance Structure.***

**(a)** DOFA shall be governed in accordance with the **CBGMODD Seven-Stakeholder Model**, ensuring that all policy decisions are evaluated from the perspectives of: (1) Citizens, (2) Businesses, (3) Government, (4) Military, (5) Ombudsmen, (6) Dignitaries, and (7) Diplomats.

**(b)** No single stakeholder category shall hold veto authority over DOFA policy. Significant policy changes shall require coalition formation across a minimum of four stakeholder categories.

**(c)** An independent **Ombudsman Division** within DOFA shall have full access to all operational metadata, investigation authority, and whistleblower protection power, ensuring continuous accountability.

**(d)** All DOFA governance decisions shall be recorded on the **Sociocratic Overlay Metadata Tapestry** (SOMT), providing cryptographic data provenance and a tamper-proof audit trail accessible to the public.

**PART II — THE PATRIOT DIVIDEND PROGRAM**

**Section 4\. *Establishment of the Patriot Dividend.***

**(a)** There is hereby established the **Patriot Dividend Program**, a strategic, time-bound direct economic transfer to every verified American adult during periods of declared national economic emergency, funded by windfall profit recapture, Treasury-issued Patriot Bonds, and voluntary return recirculation.

**(b)** The Patriot Dividend is not welfare. It is a reciprocal contract between the Federal Government and the citizen—immediate, measurable, and temporary—recognizing that citizenship during systemic crisis carries inherent value deserving direct acknowledgment.

**(c)** The Secretary of the Treasury, in coordination with the Director of DOFA, shall determine the monthly Dividend amount using the **Consumer Crisis Index** (CCI), targeting an 80 percent offset of essential-goods inflation for the median American household.

**Section 5\. *Patriot Credit and Voluntary Return Mechanism.***

**(a)** Citizens who voluntarily return part or all of their Patriot Dividend shall receive **Patriot Credits** at a 1.25x multiplier for the first 100 percent of the monthly Dividend returned, and 1.5x for sustained participation of six or more consecutive months.

**(b)** Patriot Credits shall be non-transferable, non-cashable, and applicable across five channels: (1) Tax Credit against Federal income tax liability; (2) Education Credit at any accredited institution or SCALULAR-certified training module; (3) Housing Credit toward down payment, rent, or mortgage; (4) Healthcare Credit for qualified medical expenses; and (5) Merit Badge recognition in the EarnedPath civic ledger.

**(c)** Citizens who opt into the Patriot Credit system shall receive the **GAIA Starter Pack**: a personal digital node comprising verified identity (IDIPITIS-secured), a merit-tracking ledger, a community investment portfolio, and a SCALULAR credential pathway. The Starter Pack constitutes the citizen’s first node in the national civic infrastructure architecture.

**(d)** No citizen shall be penalized, disadvantaged, or subjected to reduced services for choosing not to participate in the Voluntary Return Mechanism. The system grows through attraction, not compulsion.

**Section 6\. *Sunset and Transition Provisions.***

**(a)** The Patriot Dividend shall terminate 90 days after all of the following conditions have been sustained for 90 consecutive days: (1) the triggering national economic emergency has been formally rescinded; (2) the relevant commodity index is within 115 percent of the pre-crisis average; and (3) essential-goods CPI is below 3.0 percent annualized.

**(b)** Patriot Credits shall remain valid for five years following program sunset. GAIA Starter Packs are permanent. SCALULAR credential pathways remain open. EarnedPath merit ledgers persist.

**(c)** The emergency infrastructure shall be explicitly designed to become the peacetime foundation for the **Universal Basic Infrastructure for Meaningful Individual Autonomy** (UBIMIA) transition.

**PART III — NATIONAL IDENTITY AND SECURITY ARCHITECTURE**

**Section 7\. *IDIPITIS Identity Architecture.***

**(a)** The Director of DOFA, in coordination with the Secretary of Homeland Security and the Director of the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), shall develop and deploy the **IDIPITIS** four-layer quantum-resistant identity architecture as the national standard for citizen identity verification across all Federal programs established by this Order.

**(b)** IDIPITIS shall be designed on the principle of **citizen sovereignty over personal data**: no government agency, private entity, or third party shall access an individual’s identity data without explicit, revocable, informed consent. Citizens shall retain the right to exit the system and delete all personal data at any time.

**(c)** The four layers of IDIPITIS shall provide defense-in-depth against synthetic identity fraud, deepfake spoofing, quantum computing attacks, and centralized database exploitation, achieving a target computed attack probability of no greater than 10 to the negative 50th power.

**(d)** All IDIPITIS algorithms and architectural specifications shall be published as open-source under the CARE Commons Attribution License to permit independent security audit by the public, the academic community, and international partners.

**Section 8\. *FAVORS Biometric Verification.***

**(a)** The IDIPITIS architecture shall incorporate the **FAVORS** six-factor biometric verification system, including aura liveness detection, as the authentication standard for Patriot Dividend distribution, GAIA Starter Pack issuance, Meritcoin transactions, and all DOFA civic services.

**(b)** FAVORS implementation shall comply with all applicable Federal privacy laws, the Americans with Disabilities Act, and the principles of universal accessibility. No individual shall be excluded from civic services due to inability to provide any single biometric factor; alternative verification pathways shall be maintained.

**(c)** NIST shall, within 180 days of this Order, publish a Federal Information Processing Standard (FIPS) for FAVORS biometric integration, incorporating the ERES Institute’s six-factor architecture and aura liveness detection methodology.

**PART IV — MERITCOIN-GRACECHAIN CIVIC INFRASTRUCTURE**

**Section 9\. *Establishment of Meritcoin and Gracechain.***

**(a)** There is hereby established the **Meritcoin-Gracechain** civic infrastructure as the economic backbone of DOFA operations, Patriot Credit tracking, EarnedPath merit ledgers, and CBGMODD governance recording.

**(b)** Meritcoin shall operate on **Proof-of-Resonance** (not Proof-of-Work): the four BERA Resonance Metrics (ARI, ERI, RHC, RCI) shall feed directly into Meritcoin value accrual. Ethical and ecological harmony in, economic value out. It is not mining—it is tuning.

**(d)** Meritcoin shall not be classified as a security, commodity, or currency under existing Federal law. The Director of DOFA, in coordination with the Secretary of the Treasury, the Chair of the Securities and Exchange Commission, and the Chair of the Commodity Futures Trading Commission, shall within 120 days of this Order submit to the President a proposed regulatory classification for Proof-of-Resonance civic tokens that reflects their unique character as measures of verified contribution to the common good.

**Section 10\. *SCALULAR Certification Pathway.***

**(a)** The **Scalable Certification Architecture for Lifelong Universal Learning and Adaptive Resilience** (SCALULAR) shall provide the credentialing infrastructure for all DOFA programs, operating across four pillars: HEALTH (Solid-State Smart-City Health Protocol, SSHP), LAW (Solid-State Legal Architecture, SSLA), PROTECTION (Solid-State Public Safety, SSPS), and SKILLS/TRADE (Solid-State Skills and Trade, SSST).

**(c)** SCALULAR credentials shall be recorded on Gracechain, providing permanent, tamper-proof verification of skills, training, and civic contribution regardless of employer, institution, or geographic relocation.

**PART V — CBGMODD GOVERNANCE INTEGRATION**

**Section 11\. *Seven-Stakeholder Governance Standard.***

**(a)** All programs, offices, and initiatives established by this Order shall operate under the **CBGMODD Seven-Stakeholder Governance Standard**, ensuring that Citizens, Businesses, Government, Military, Ombudsmen, Dignitaries, and Diplomats each hold defined rights, responsibilities, and participatory authority in governance decisions.

**(b)** The CBGMODD governance architecture shall operate across four decision-making levels: (1) Constitutional Level—requiring 75 percent supermajority of all seven stakeholder groups; (2) Legislative Level—requiring simple majority of four or more stakeholder groups; (3) Executive Level—government implementation with Ombudsman oversight; and (4) Judicial Level—Ombudsman panels with citizen juries for dispute resolution.

**(c)** Structural safeguards shall include: five-year sunset clauses on all policies requiring affirmative renewal; 60 percent citizen direct-vote override authority at any level below constitutional; clearance decay requiring continuous merit maintenance with no permanent authority; data sovereignty ensuring the right to exit and delete; and open-source publication of all governance algorithms.

**Section 12\. *NBERS as National Progress Metric.***

**(a)** The Director of DOFA, in coordination with the Secretary of Commerce and the Chair of the Council of Economic Advisers, shall develop and publish the **National Bio-Energetic Resonance Standard** (NBERS) as a measurable replacement for GDP in evaluating the success of all programs established by this Order.

**(b)** NBERS shall incorporate the four BERA Resonance Metrics and shall measure national progress in terms of human flourishing, ecological sustainability, family stability, and intergenerational equity rather than aggregate economic output alone.

**(c)** Within two years of this Order, the Director of DOFA shall submit to the President and to Congress a report comparing NBERS outcomes against GDP for all programs established hereunder, with a recommendation on whether NBERS should be adopted as a supplementary national progress indicator.

**PART VI — IMPLEMENTATION, FUNDING, AND OVERSIGHT**

**(c) Phase 3 (Days 181–365):** Deploy GAIA Starter Packs in pilot districts. First SCALULAR Tier 1 (SSSC) certifications issued. Begin NBERS metric collection. Submit DOFA Cabinet-level legislation to Congress. Publish first quarterly Family Wellbeing Report.

**(d) Phase 4 (Year 2+):** National rollout of all programs. Full NBERS reporting. Meritcoin-Gracechain operational deployment. DOFA Family Amity Corps deployed to all 435 Congressional districts. First Hundred-Year Question policy review cycle initiated.

**Section 14\. *Funding.***

**(a)** Initial funding for the Office of Family Amity shall be drawn from existing Executive Office of the President appropriations and interagency transfers authorized under the Economy Act (31 U.S.C. 1535).

**(b)** The President shall submit to Congress a supplemental appropriations request for full DOFA operations, Patriot Dividend Program capitalization, and Meritcoin-Gracechain infrastructure development within 120 days of this Order.

**(c)** No funds shall be expended under this Order in excess of amounts appropriated by Congress, except as authorized by existing law for the immediate establishment of the coordinating office.

**Section 15\. *Savings Clauses and Legal Compliance.***

**(a)** Nothing in this Order shall be construed to impair or otherwise affect the authority granted by law to any executive department, agency, or the head thereof.

**(b)** This Order shall be implemented consistent with applicable law and subject to the availability of appropriations.

**(c)** This Order is not intended to, and does not, create any right or benefit, substantive or procedural, enforceable at law or in equity by any party against the United States, its departments, agencies, or entities, its officers, employees, or agents, or any other person.

**(d)** All programs established under this Order shall comply with the Privacy Act of 1974, the Administrative Procedure Act, the Paperwork Reduction Act, the Federal Information Security Modernization Act, and all other applicable Federal statutes and regulations.

**APPENDIX C — CONSTITUTIONAL AND LEGAL FOUNDATION**

## **C.1 — Youngstown Framework Analysis**

This Executive Order is designed to operate within **Zone One** of the Jackson concurrence framework from Youngstown Sheet & Tube Co. v. Sawyer, 343 U.S. 579 (1952)—the zone of maximum Presidential authority where the President acts with express or implied authorization from Congress.

The Order cites existing statutory authority (Economy Act, National Emergencies Act, Title 5 organizational provisions) and explicitly defers to Congress on all matters requiring appropriations, regulatory classification, and departmental establishment. No provision of this Order purports to legislate, appropriate funds beyond existing authority, create enforceable private rights, or override existing statutory mandates.

## **C.2 — Appropriations Clause Compliance**

Section 14 expressly limits expenditures to amounts appropriated by Congress, with initial operations funded through existing Executive Office appropriations and Economy Act interagency transfers. The Patriot Dividend Program requires supplemental appropriations, and the Order directs the President to submit such a request within 120 days. No expenditure is authorized in excess of congressional appropriation.

## **C.3 — APA and Privacy Compliance**

Section 15 mandates compliance with the Administrative Procedure Act, Privacy Act, FISMA, and Paperwork Reduction Act. IDIPITIS and FAVORS deployments are subject to NIST FIPS publication processes, which include public comment periods. CBGMODD governance transparency through SOMT exceeds current Federal transparency requirements.

## **C.4 — Nondelegation and Separation of Powers**

This Order directs the executive branch to organize its internal operations and submit legislative proposals to Congress. It does not delegate legislative authority, create courts, or assume judicial functions. The CBGMODD governance model supplements—but does not replace—existing constitutional structures. DOFA operates within the executive branch under Presidential authority to organize executive offices.
