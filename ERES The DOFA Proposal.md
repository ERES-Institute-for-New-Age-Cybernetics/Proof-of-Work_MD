# **Part I. The Open Letter (Revised)**

## **The Structural Problem**

The American presidency was designed as one branch among three. Over the past century, it has become something else: the single point of failure for every national crisis. When a pandemic strikes, the President is the crisis manager. When communities fracture, the President is the moral spokesperson. When families collapse under economic strain, the President is expected to deliver rescue.

This was never the constitutional design. It is a structural deficiency that no election will correct.

The consequence is that every four years, the nation wagers its stability on the temperament, health, and judgment of one human being. When that person bends, the whole structure shudders.

Responsible governance must move in the opposite direction: making the heavy burdens of the presidency as structurally unnecessary as possible. Not by weakening the office, but by strengthening the foundations beneath it.

## **The Missing Link: Family Fragmentation as a Driver of Presidential Overreach**

Decades of social science show a consistent pattern: family instability—fragmentation, intergenerational conflict, economic precarity at the household level—produces downstream crises that cascade into schools, local law enforcement, mental health systems, and eventually federal emergency response. When families break down, communities absorb the stress. When communities break down, states turn to Washington. And when Washington responds, it lands on the desk of one person: the President.

This is not a partisan claim. It is a systems observation.

Existing federal agencies (HHS, ACF, SAMHSA) are reactive—they intervene after fractures have already metastasized into emergencies. No existing agency has the mandate to work upstream with preventive, long-horizon investment. And no existing agency functions as a unified fiscal clearinghouse for family-oriented spending, which is currently scattered across dozens of departments with separate accounting, separate metrics, and no aggregate accountability.

## **The Proposal: A Permanent Independent Agency**

I propose the creation of the Department of Family Amity (DOFA) —not as a literal fourth constitutional branch (which would require an amendment), but as a statutorily created independent agency modeled on the Federal Reserve or the Government Accountability Office. DOFA would sit within the Executive Branch by law but operate with structural independence: a single, long-term director (e.g., 12-year non-renewable term), a fixed appropriation formula, and a mandate that prioritizes generational timescales over election cycles.

Its mission would be fourfold:

1. Prevention over reaction – Invest in family cohesion and intergenerational responsibility before crises emerge.  
2. Structural independence – Outlast any single administration, reducing the personalization of national stability.  
3. Long-horizon stewardship – Use metrics and earned pathways calibrated to generational timescales.  
4. Fiscal clearinghouse – Serve as the single point of receipt integrity for all federal family-oriented expenditure, using the IDIPITIS framework (see Part II, Section 6).

## **The Household Principle**

Every functioning household operates on a simple rule: all receipts flow through one ledger. The United States government has been running its family-oriented programs without a household ledger. DOFA would change this—not as surveillance, but as stewardship.

## **What This Is Not**

This proposal is not partisan. It is not a call for a constitutional amendment tomorrow. It is a practical engineering proposal for a republic that has outgrown its original reactive architecture.

# **Part II. Draft Presidential Executive Order (Revised)**

Executive Order \[Number\]

Establishment of the Department of Family Amity (DOFA)  
as a Prevention-Oriented Independent Agency and Fiscal Clearinghouse

*By the authority vested in me as President of the United States of America, and in order to strengthen the foundational institutions of American civil society, it is hereby ordered:*

### **Section 1\. Purpose and Findings.**

(a) The family is the foundational unit of American civil society and a primary source of intergenerational resilience.  
(b) The progressive centralization of crisis response in the Executive Branch has created unsustainable dependence on presidential intervention for challenges rooted in family and community breakdown.  
(c) Existing federal agencies operate primarily in reactive and crisis-intervention modalities, leaving a structural gap in prevention-oriented, long-horizon investment.  
(d) Federal expenditure on family-related programs is dispersed across multiple departments with no unified fiscal accountability, resulting in duplication, waste, and an inability to measure aggregate outcomes.  
(e) Establishing a permanent, prevention-oriented agency with fiscal clearinghouse authority will reduce systemic vulnerabilities and strengthen the effectiveness of existing departments.

### **Section 2\. Establishment.**

There is hereby established the Department of Family Amity (DOFA) as an independent agency within the Executive Branch, organized under Title 5 of the United States Code, with the structural independence, long-term leadership tenure, and fiscal clearinghouse authorities described herein. The Secretary of Family Amity shall be nominated by the President and confirmed by the Senate for a single, non-renewable 12-year term.

### **Section 3\. Mission.**

The Department shall promote Family Amity—defined as the active cultivation of harmony, mutual support, and intergenerational responsibility—through:

(a) Prevention-oriented programs that strengthen families before crises emerge;  
(b) A unified fiscal clearinghouse for all federal family-oriented expenditure;  
(c) Long-horizon metrics and earned pathways for human development;  
(d) Annual reporting to Congress on fiscal integrity and measurable outcomes.

### **Section 4\. Core Principles.**

1. *Do not hurt yourself. Do not hurt others. Build for generations to come.*  
2. Prioritize prevention and regeneration over reactive intervention.  
3. Maintain measurable outcomes focused on family stability and community resilience.

### **Section 5\. Leadership and Structural Independence.**

(a) The Secretary of Family Amity shall serve a single 12-year term, removable only for cause.  
(b) DOFA shall receive a fixed appropriation formula (e.g., 0.5% of annual federal outlays, adjusted for inflation) to insulate it from annual budget volatility.  
(c) The Secretary shall pursue, in coordination with Congress, statutory codification of DOFA’s independent agency status within 24 months of this order.

### **Section 6\. Fiscal Clearinghouse and IDIPITIS Framework.**

(a) All federal receipts, appropriations, and expenditures directed toward family welfare, community resilience, and related mandates shall be visible within DOFA’s clearinghouse via interagency data-sharing agreements. Programmatic authority remains with existing agencies.  
(b) The Department shall implement the IDIPITIS framework (Identification, Documentation, Integrity, Processing, Identification-verification, Tracking, Integrity-audit, and Stewardship) as its operational protocol for receipt management.  
(c) DOFA shall publish quarterly transparency reports detailing receipt flows, programmatic outcomes, and identified inefficiencies.

### **Section 7\. Transition and Sequencing Plan.**

Within the first 24 months of operation:

(a) Months 1–6: Establish DOFA as a coordination office within the Executive Office of the President, pending Senate confirmation of the Secretary.  
(b) Months 7–18: Conduct a comprehensive audit of existing federal family-oriented expenditure across HHS, Education, HUD, VA, and other relevant agencies to establish baseline receipt integrity.  
(c) Months 19–24: Launch pilot programs in prevention-oriented family cohesion in no fewer than three geographic regions, with measurable outcomes reported within 18 months of pilot start.  
(d) Month 24 onward: Pursue statutory codification as a permanent independent agency with fixed appropriation and 12-year secretarial term.

### **Section 8\. Initial Focus Areas.**

(a) Unified family stability metrics framework, developed in partnership with qualified research institutions including the ERES Institute for New Age Cybernetics;  
(b) Baseline audit of existing family-oriented expenditure;  
(c) Pilot programs in prevention-oriented family cohesion;  
(d) First DOFA Annual Stewardship Report to Congress within 18 months.

### **Section 9\. General Provisions.**

(a) This order shall be implemented consistent with applicable law and subject to the availability of appropriations.  
(b) Nothing in this order shall impair existing statutory authorities of executive departments.  
(c) This order does not create any enforceable right or benefit against the United States.
