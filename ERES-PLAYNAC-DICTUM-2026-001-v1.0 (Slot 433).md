**Abstract**

This paper formalizes the PlayNAC Architecture Dictum: **PlayNAC ≡ (ERES Trilogy × ERES UBIMIA Monograph) \+ ERES Library (600+ documents).** The dictum is short to state and load-bearing in consequence: it specifies the canonical relationship among the three primary corpora produced by the ERES Institute since February 2012, and it tells future readers — including prospective publishers, peer reviewers, and downstream implementers — exactly where any single ERES artifact sits within the larger architecture. The dictum is not a catalog; it is an algebraic statement about how the three corpora compose into a single operational system, PlayNAC, the New Age Cybernetics game-platform that operationalizes the Institute's research output for participatory governance, education, and economy.  
**Keywords:** PlayNAC, ERES Trilogy, UBIMIA, New Age Cybernetics, architectural dictum, ERES Library, governance platform, ERES Institute.

## **1\. Introduction**

The ERES Institute has, since February 2012, produced research output across three distinguishable corpora:

1. **The ERES Trilogy** — three foundational books synthesizing the Institute's theoretical core (target publisher: MIT Press, aspirational).  
2. **The ERES UBIMIA Monograph** — operational and protocol-level documentation, instantiated most recently as the ERES UBIMIA Manual v1.1 (April 25, 2026, 188 pages).  
3. **The ERES Library** — the running archive of whitepapers, executive orders, equation derivations, covenant documents, ResearchGate deposits, and protocol specifications, presently exceeding 600 documents.

Until this paper, the relationship among the three was left implicit — readers inferred it from context. The PlayNAC Architecture Dictum makes the relationship explicit and binding.

## **2\. The Dictum**

**PlayNAC ≡ (ERES Trilogy × ERES UBIMIA Monograph) \+ ERES Library**  
The notation is read as follows:

* **≡** — definitional identity, not approximate equality. PlayNAC *is* the right-hand side; it is not merely modeled by it.  
* **×** — compositional product. The Trilogy and the Monograph are not additive; they are co-determinative. The Trilogy without the Monograph is theory without operationalization; the Monograph without the Trilogy is protocol without epistemic ground.  
* **\+** — additive extension. The Library extends the product but does not change its identity. New Library entries enrich PlayNAC without redefining it.

## **3\. Why Each Term Is Necessary**

**3.1 Why the Trilogy.** The Trilogy carries the Institute's theoretical commitments: New Age Cybernetics, the 72 Key Domains, the Triune Math keys, the Twin Messiah construct (JAS × EMA \= 72, with DAL as hinge), and the moral vectoring of the epistemology toward BEST/SOUND/GOOD outcomes. Without the Trilogy, downstream readers cannot reconstruct *why* the protocols are shaped as they are.  
**3.2 Why the Monograph.** The UBIMIA Monograph carries the operational commitments: how Universal Basic Income, Merit, Investment, and Awards (UBIMIA) are computed, attested, distributed, and audited. The Monograph is what makes the Trilogy's theory implementable rather than merely admirable. Without the Monograph, the Trilogy's theoretical commitments cannot translate into participatory practice.  
**3.3 Why the Library.** The Library carries everything that does not fit the Trilogy/Monograph rhythm: the Berggruen Prize Essay, the DOFA whitepapers, the Hague filing, the Equity Covenant, EAAP versions, the Capstone Heptad, the cryptographic primitive papers, executive order drafts, and the ongoing ResearchGate deposit stream. The Library is where the Institute's living research happens. Without the Library, PlayNAC would be a closed system; with it, PlayNAC remains an open and evolving one.

## **4\. What the Dictum Forbids**

The dictum forbids three common misreadings:  
**4.1 Misreading as catalog.** The dictum is not a list. A reader who has read all three corpora has not thereby "completed PlayNAC"; PlayNAC is the *composition*, not the union of pages.  
**4.2 Misreading as substitution.** No corpus substitutes for another. A practitioner cannot operate PlayNAC from the Library alone, however large the Library grows; the Trilogy and the Monograph are constitutive, not merely informative.  
**4.3 Misreading as hierarchy.** The product Trilogy × Monograph is symmetric. Neither corpus is superior to the other; their co-determination is the whole point.
