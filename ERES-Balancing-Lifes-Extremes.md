## Abstract

This public document presents a long-form framework for understanding how family-oriented investment and economic effort interact to shape overall comfort and well-being. Its central claim is that neither extreme produces the most sustainable life on its own. When one side expands without limit, hidden costs rise. When both are developed in a coordinated way, they can reinforce each other. The framework expresses that idea through explicit mathematics, including a simple quadratic model and a more realistic bounded nonlinear model with saturation, synergy, thresholds, and imbalance penalties.
