The first case was attribution-insertion with unverifiable trademark claims — adversarial to ERES open-source posture. The second case was attribution-restraint by a correspondent who voluntarily selected the lower tier. Treating the two identically would have been incorrect. The Protocol's tiered design permitted both cases to resolve cleanly without forcing a single verdict on the underlying verification question.

Relevant observations: availability at similar times, similar detailed response cadences, AI-assisted response patterns in similar registers, thin LinkedIn profile depth for both, deep ERES-corpus-specific reasoning by both, and simultaneity of review cycles.

The coordination question is not actionable from the evidence currently available. The architecture protects ERES specifications regardless.

## 7. Architectural Observations Worth Preserving

### 7.1 The Protocol Caught What It Was Designed to Catch

An unverified external contributor with trademark claims attempted to insert attributed content into a standards-track ERES document. The Protocol required identity verification through a channel outside the platform of first contact. Verification failed. Attribution withdrew. Content was preserved through generic reformulation. No legal exposure, no public accusation, no specification contamination. The 24-hour time from Protocol publication to successful first test is not coincidental — the Protocol was written in anticipation of exactly this kind of test, and the threat arrived promptly.

### 7.2 Idea-Level Work Survives Identity-Level Failure

Both engagements produced substantive idea-level contributions that improved the specification. The state-vs-path distinction, the separatrix-crossing failure mode, the runtime-containment requirement, and the sovereignty-domain correspondence are all architecturally sound and remain in v1.3-FINAL as cleanly derived properties or as ERES-canonical mappings (Three Governing Principles). Ideas cannot be un-thought; what the Attribution Protocol controls is who is named as their originator. This distinction matters: an infiltration attempt does not contaminate the technical substance if the Institute maintains discipline about what it claims.

### 7.3 The Incident Demonstrated the Failure Mode Named in DSAP-PRE

A collaborative space in which acceptance outran verification — where surface signals (review turns, expressed interest, detailed contributions) read healthy while the underlying regime (contributor identity, trademark claims) was compromised — is exactly the failure mode DSAP-PRE names and measures in authorization contexts. The Attribution Protocol caught the incident at the identity verification step, which in DSAP-PRE terms is the state-consistency layer. The deeper lesson: admission of external contribution into a standards-track corpus is itself an authorization problem, and the Institute may want to formalize that observation as a v1.4-class extension (DSAP-PRE applied to contributor admission, not only to authorization decisions).

### 7.4 Sole-Author Responsibility with Scoped External Credit Is the Production Model

v1.3-FINAL is more defensible as an ERES Institute sole-authored document with scope-limited correspondence-tier peer review than it would have been as a multi-author document with Named Contributor attribution. The separation of scoped credit from responsibility transfer is the architectural move that allowed the Attribution Protocol to handle both cases without cascading into the specification's technical content. This production model should be the default for future ERES standards-track work unless a Named Contributor relationship is established through explicit identity-verified process.

### 7.5 Testability Is the Highest Compliment

The second correspondent's closing observation — that the work "moves the discussion into a space where these questions can actually be tested" — is a specific kind of endorsement that matters more than a general endorsement of conclusions. A framework that can fail visibly when it fails is a falsifiable framework, and falsifiability is the threshold property for any specification that claims standards-track status. v1.3-FINAL now meets that threshold on the architectural axis the correspondent engaged with. That's worth recording as a positive session outcome regardless of any unresolved question about the correspondent's underlying identity.
