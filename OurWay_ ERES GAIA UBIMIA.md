## **What Still Doesn't Work (But Is Bounded)**

### **1\. Criminal justice and dispute resolution (outside scope)**

The proposal should explicitly state: *"This plan covers benefit distribution and resource allocation only. Criminal justice, contract enforcement, and tort law remain separate systems (though they may eventually be reformed along similar principles)."* Without this disclaimer, the proposal overclaims.

### **2\. Global supply chain for replacement parts**

Solar panels come from China. Batteries come from lithium mined in Australia or Chile. Sensors come from Taiwan. A solid-state smart city in Arkansas cannot manufacture these locally. The proposal's "local material sourcing" assumption is false for high-tech components.

Possible fix: Acknowledge that planetary supply chains remain, but argue that their cost is amortized over 30-year replacement cycles and is negligible compared to current monthly bills. Add a Phase 0 line item: "Replacement parts budget \= 0.5% of initial capital per year."

### **3\. Political opposition from incumbent industries**

Utilities, banks, insurers, landlords, and automakers have trillions in sunk capital. They will not surrender without a fight. The proposal does not address this.

Possible fix: Acknowledge that Phase 0 must be built in a jurisdiction with minimal incumbent opposition (e.g., a greenfield site in a Global South country with no existing utility monopoly). Bella Vista, Arkansas is not that jurisdiction — it's inside the U.S. regulatory system.

### **4\. AI alignment**

The MIEVM ensemble does not agree on basic facts, let alone values. Asking Claude, Grok, DeepSeek, and ChatGPT to validate the same resonance claim will produce four different answers.

Possible fix: Replace "parallel validation" with "majority vote among AI instruments" or "human-in-the-loop for disputes." Or drop the claim and use a single, purpose-built AI for VERTECA verification.
