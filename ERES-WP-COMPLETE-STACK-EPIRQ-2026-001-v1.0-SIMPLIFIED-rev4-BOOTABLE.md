## §2 — The Time Horizon: A Thousand Years Out

The framework looks one thousand years ahead. Not five, not fifty, not a million. A thousand.

A thousand years is chosen because it is the shortest time horizon long enough to take multi-generational consequences seriously. Anything shorter lets designers ignore questions about durability, intergenerational equity, and ecological recovery. Anything much longer collapses into pure speculation. A thousand years means roughly forty generations have to be able to look back at the decisions made today and say: *that was sound.*

Financial instruments have to accrue value across the lifespans of great-grandchildren. The thousand-year map does not assume things will be peaceful. It assumes things will be turbulent. The framework is designed for turbulence, not for calm.

## §3 — The Economic Hinge: The Earth Change Flip

For a thousand-year framework to work, the global economy has to change shape. Currently, the world economy rewards consumption: the more you extract and consume, the more wealth you accumulate. ERES calls the shift away from this the **Earth Change Economic Flip**.

The Flip has three dimensions:

**From consumption to care.** Wealth accrues to those who maintain, repair, restore, and nurture — not to those who extract and discard.

**From extraction to regeneration.** Productive activity replenishes the systems it draws from, rather than depleting them.

**From depletion-funded to merit-funded.** People earn through demonstrated contribution to the common project rather than through ownership of depleted natural assets.

The Earth Change Economic Flip is the historical event that the framework's other layers either prepare for, enable, or operate within. It is not optional and it is not gradual in the way most economic reforms are. It is a phase transition: the moment when the economic logic of the planet inverts.

The discipline that operationalizes the Flip is called **Meritcology** — the physics of merit. We will return to it in §11.

Every claim in the framework reduces to one measurement event. The measurement event has a name: **Resonance Testing**. The equation is:

**CBGMODD** — seven-seat governance witness. The seven seats are Civilian, Banking, Government, Medical, Military, Digital, and Diplomatic. Each seat sees the same event from a different angle. The seven angles together prevent any one perspective from defining truth alone.

**FAVORS** — six biometric markers (Face, Acoustic, Vector, ODOR, Retina, Signature). ODOR is canonical, not optional. The six markers ground the witness in physical reality — a biometric event happened to a real organism, not just a string of bytes.

**BERA** — Bio-Ecologic Rating Aggregate. The measurement of how the cohort interacts with the rest of life on Earth. BERA is what links every transaction back to the framework's null hypothesis: did this event help keep life on Earth alive, or not?

**RATE** — the seven-dimensional output. Seven dimensions, not one. See §12.4.

### §12.2 Why multiplication

CBGMODD × FAVORS is a cross-product, not an addition. This means: governance witness and biometric grounding must *both* be present. Witnessing without biometric grounding is hearsay. Biometrics without witnessing is surveillance. Multiplication forces both into every measurement. If either is zero, the product is zero, and no RATE is generated.

### §12.3 Why addition for BERA

After CBGMODD × FAVORS produces a witnessed-and-grounded event, BERA is added — not multiplied. This is because BERA is *ground truth from the living planet*, contributed independently of human governance and biometric capture. Adding BERA means: bio-ecologic reality is not subject to the governance vote. It contributes its own measurement, on its own terms, and the equation respects that.

### §12.4 Why seven dimensions

RATE is seven-dimensional because seven is the minimum number that prevents collapse to a single score while remaining tractable:

- **R₁** — Sustenance (water, food, shelter at biological tier)
- **R₂** — Viability across scale (does it work THOW → FDRV?)
- **R₃** — Generational integrity (does it survive forty generations?)
- **R₄** — Access (can the cohort actually reach what it needs?)
- **R₅** — Authorization (is it properly witnessed by CBGMODD?)
- **R₆** — Soft resonance (does it feel right to those living inside it?)
- **R₇** — Reciprocity-over-time (does it pay back what it took?)

Each dimension does a job the others cannot. Removing any one of them creates a blind spot. Keeping all seven is the framework's commitment to honest measurement.

### §12.5 The prohibition on scalar collapse

**RATE may not be averaged, summed, or otherwise reduced to a single number.** This is the framework's most important measurement rule. A scalar score (credit score, GDP, social credit) lies because it hides which dimensions are strong and which are weak. RATE forces every dimension to be visible. If R₄ (access) is low, that fact may not be hidden by averaging it with a high R₆ (soft resonance). The seven dimensions stand together or fail visibly.

When CBGMODD × FAVORS does not yet yield a witnessed event — for example, because the seven seats have not all reported — the event is held in a state called **VEILED**. VEILED is not failure.
