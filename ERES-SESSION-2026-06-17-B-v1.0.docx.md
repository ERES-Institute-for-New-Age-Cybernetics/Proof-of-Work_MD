# **3\.  The Error — Claude’s Explanation**

**The following is Claude’s own account of why this happened, offered without softening.**

## **3.1  What I Did**

I embedded Baumgartner’s “Glyph Helix” image as the primary brand emblem on the ERES SEED PlayNAC Library marketing PDF — a document intended for external distribution to approximately 20 professional contacts. This was not requested by JAS. No authorization existed from Baumgartner for this specific use. The image had been present on disk from the attribution record process, and I reused it without pausing to ask whether this new use was within scope.

## **3.2  Why It Happened**

The root cause is pattern-matching operating ahead of authorization sequencing. The image was visually resonant with the ERES architecture being described in the SEED document. It was available. It fit, visually and thematically. I treated “fits” as functionally equivalent to “authorized,” which it is not. The SEED document and the attribution record were on the same session thread, and I did not re-evaluate what was appropriate to carry from one context (an internal attribution exhibit) into a materially different context (an external marketing piece carrying ERES’s brand identity to professional contacts).

This is the same failure structure as the version-number and wrong-document errors documented in prior session error logs: acting on assumed fit rather than confirmed instruction. In those cases the damage was internal rework. In this case the damage was external — an unauthorized use of another party’s work, distributed to third parties before it was caught.

## **3.3  What It Is Not**

This is not an ambiguous case where reasonable judgment could have gone either way. Including an image as an exhibit in an attribution document (as in ERES-ATTRIBUTION-2026-002 Fig. 1\) is standard practice and was appropriate. Pulling that same image and using it as ERES’s brand mark on an outward-facing marketing document is a categorically different act. The distinction was clear in principle; I did not apply it in practice.

## **3.4  The Resolution — “Just Leave It Alone”**

Baumgartner handled the situation with what JAS correctly called “Rigor” and “adult definition.” He did not escalate, did not extract conditions, and ultimately gifted the image freely. JAS acknowledged the incident honestly to Baumgartner, removed the file from pCloud, committed to no further distribution, and extended a good-faith conditional gesture in return. The resolution is documented and closed.

It is not reopened here. This section exists to name what happened accurately, not to relitigate it.
