# **Section 1: The Day-1 Conversion Mechanism**

## **1.1 Conversion Architecture**

The ERES 401(k) Soft Landing operates through a four-stage conversion process that transforms a legacy accumulation-based retirement asset into a resonance-weighted, merit-sustained lifetime income stream. The conversion is voluntary, IRS-compliant via trustee-to-trustee rollover, and reversible during a transition window.

### **Stage 1: Legacy Asset Intake**

The participant’s existing 401(k) balance is rolled over via standard IRS trustee-to-trustee transfer into an ERES-compatible trust structure (a Qualified Retirement Trust with GraceChain ledger integration). No taxable event occurs. The balance is recorded on GraceChain as the participant’s Initial Legacy Value (ILV).

### **Stage 2: Resonance Assessment & Conversion Index**

The participant receives a Conversion Index (CI) score derived from three ERES metrics:

* NBERS Score: Bio-ecologic rating measuring the participant’s current alignment with ecological and civic contribution standards.

* ARI (Aura Resonance Index): Systemic resonance alignment within the broader ERES network.

* EarnedPath Position (EP): The participant’s cumulative contribution trajectory, calculated as EP \= CPM × WBS \+ PERT.

The Conversion Index formula:

*CI \= (NBERS\_norm × 0.40) \+ (ARI\_sys × 0.35) \+ (EP\_norm × 0.25)*

Where each component is normalized to a 0–1.0 scale. A new participant with no prior ERES engagement receives a baseline CI of 0.60 (the “grace floor”), ensuring no one enters the system penalized. The CI determines the allocation weighting across the three output streams.

### **Stage 3: Tri-Stream Allocation**

The Initial Legacy Value is allocated into three streams, weighted by the Conversion Index:

| Stream | Allocation | Function | ERES Component |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| UBIMIA Base Floor | 40% of ILV | Guaranteed lifetime income floor, independent of market conditions | UBIMIA (Universal Basic Income \+ Merit \+ Incentives \+ Awards) |
| GERP Productive Assets | 35% of ILV | Investment in ecological/civic productive assets via the Giant Earth Resource Planner | GERP \+ SROC (Societal Return on Capital) |
| EarnedPath Merit Stream | 25% of ILV | Ongoing merit-based dividends from continued contribution, even post-traditional retirement age | EarnedPath \+ Meritcoin/GraceChain |

These percentages represent the default allocation for a CI of 0.60. Higher CI scores shift weight toward the GERP and EarnedPath streams (higher upside potential), while the UBIMIA floor remains protected at a minimum of 35% regardless of CI.

### **Stage 4: Lifetime Income Generation**

The participant’s monthly income is calculated as:

*Monthly Income \= UBIMIA\_floor \+ GERP\_yield \+ EP\_merit\_dividends*

Where:

* UBIMIA\_floor \= (ILV × 0.40) / Longevity\_Factor, adjusted annually for inflation via BERC ecological productivity index.

# **Section 4: Legal & Regulatory Pathway**

The ERES 401(k) Soft Landing operates within existing U.S. retirement law and does not require new legislation to initiate. The following legal mechanisms enable the transition:

## **4.1 IRS-Compliant Rollover**

The initial asset transfer uses standard trustee-to-trustee rollover under IRC Section 401(a)(31) and Section 402(c). The ERES trust qualifies as a receiving custodian under existing IRS rules for self-directed retirement trusts. No taxable event is triggered. The participant retains all tax-deferred status on transferred balances.

## **4.2 Trust Structure**

The ERES Qualified Retirement Trust operates under ERISA fiduciary standards, with GraceChain providing the transparency and audit trail that exceeds current 401(k) reporting requirements. PlayNAC governance functions as the fiduciary oversight mechanism, with consensus-based decision-making replacing single-sponsor control.

## **4.3 SECURE 2.0 Alignment**

The SECURE 2.0 Act (2022–2025 provisions) creates several pathways that align with ERES mechanisms: in-plan annuity options (supporting the UBIMIA floor), alternative asset access in defined contribution plans (supporting GERP ecological allocation), auto-enrollment and auto-escalation features (compatible with PlayNAC governance defaults), and expanded catch-up contributions for older workers (complementing EarnedPath merit streams).
