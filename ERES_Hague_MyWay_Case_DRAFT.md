## SECTION III — THE PRIVATE/PUBLIC DISGUISE AS STRUCTURAL VIOLATION

### III.1 — The Mechanism

ERES epistemology identifies a universal structural pattern: every institution that accumulates knowledge faces a dilemma — the knowledge that gives the institution its power is the same knowledge that, if shared freely, would dissolve the institution's competitive advantage. This is not a moral failing. It is an architectural constraint.

The gap between what an institution knows privately and what it shares publicly is the SPRT (Spiritual-Public-Record-Trust) disguise. The width of this gap determines the degree of wealth hoarding. Banks hoard financial knowledge. Governments hoard security knowledge. Technology companies hoard algorithmic knowledge. The pattern is universal.

**The ERES Position:** This disguise is the mechanism by which genuine wealth — epistemological wealth, not merely financial wealth — concentrates inward rather than radiating outward. An idea hoarded is an idea whose generative potential decays. An idea published is an idea whose generative potential compounds across generations.

### III.2 — The Legal Argument

The MyWay Case argues that the systematic operation of the private/public disguise — when applied to knowledge necessary for human survival, dignity, and intergenerational continuity — constitutes a structural violation of the following instruments:

| Instrument | Article(s) | Violation Identified |
|------------|-----------|---------------------|
| **UDHR** | Article 19 (information freedom); Article 27 (share in scientific advancement) | Institutional gatekeeping of epistemological wealth restricts participation in scientific and cultural life |
| **ICESCR** | Article 15 (right to enjoy benefits of scientific progress) | Proprietary enclosure of knowledge systems prevents equitable benefit-sharing |
| **Berne Convention** | Article 6bis (moral rights of authors) | Institutional assimilation of individual epistemological contributions without attribution violates moral rights |
| **TRIPS Agreement** | Article 7 (objectives: mutual advantage; social/economic welfare) | IP regimes that concentrate knowledge contradict TRIPS' own stated objective of mutual advantage |
| **Paris Agreement** | Article 2 (long-term temperature goal); Technology Framework | Hoarding of sustainability knowledge delays planetary boundary compliance across generations |

**Core Claim:** The private/public disguise, when applied to knowledge required for human dignity (UDHR), scientific participation (ICESCR), attribution integrity (Berne), mutual advantage (TRIPS), and planetary survival (Paris), operates as a structural human rights violation — not through individual malice, but through architectural design.
