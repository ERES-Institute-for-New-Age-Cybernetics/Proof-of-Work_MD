## §0.1 What the Infomediary Is (and Is Not)

The Infomediary is the **Instrument-of-Faith** through which any Civigen — any participant qualified under the broader CBGMODD architecture — submits a query and receives an answer that has been **qualified, quantified, rationalized, filtered, shaped, and morally vectored** before it is returned. It is not a search engine. It is not a chatbot. It is not a content management system. It is not an oracle. It is the named operational intermediary between a User-GROUP Query and the ERES DATABANK, with the ERES Trilogy serving as its noise-filter and intelligence-shaper, and with BEST / SOUND / GOOD as the moral-vectoring layer that determines the tier at which DETAIL is returned.

The four negations matter, because each names a category the Infomediary has been mistaken for in earlier drafts.

It is not a *search engine* because search engines return ranked-by-relevance lists with no commitment to admissibility, measurement, framing, or moral vectoring. The Infomediary's three intake operations — qualify, quantify, rationalize — make admissibility, measurement, and framing first-class operations rather than emergent accidents of relevance scoring.

It is not a *chatbot* because chatbots are conversational instruments that compose plausible text. The Infomediary does not compose. It mediates. AD\_ON-AI is the downstream composer the Infomediary feeds. That separation is canonical: the Instrument that mediates is structurally distinct from the Instrument that composes, and both are downstream of the human Wielder.

It is not a *content management system* because CMSs index and serve content. The Infomediary does query the ERES DATABANK — which is itself a content corpus — but the DATABANK is *one of three things* the Infomediary holds in tension. The Trilogy is another. The Wielder's INTENT tier is the third. None of these is content in the CMS sense.

It is not an *oracle* because oracles return answers under the pretension of certainty. The Infomediary returns DETAIL under the explicit semantics of the VEILED state when attestation is pending, BEST / SOUND / GOOD when moral vectoring is required, and Sim OUTPUT when the answer is provisional pending real-world validation. No pretense of certainty appears anywhere in the round-trip.

What the Infomediary *is*, then, is the named, specifiable, attestable, revocable, federally-mirrorable Instrument through which a 1000-Year Future Map can actually be queried by the people who live under it. The Instrument is what makes the Map governable.

## §0.3 The Three Intake Operations: Qualify · Quantify · Rationalize

When a Civigen submits a query to the Infomediary, the query enters the Instrument through three intake operations, performed in sequence and with explicit return values at each stage. The three operations are not abstract. They have specific semantics, specific outputs, and specific failure modes.

### Qualify — Admissibility Gating

The qualify stage answers the question: *is this query admissible?* It is the SEPLTA-style gate. SEPLTA (Social-Ecological-Political-Legal-Technical-Aesthetic) is the six-domain admissibility frame the ERES corpus has used since the early CyberRAVE era to determine whether a query fits any of the corpus's recognized purpose-states. A query that fits none of the six domains is not admissible — not because it is wrong, but because it is *not-yet-positioned*. The Infomediary returns the query to the Civigen with a VEILED tag and a suggestion for re-positioning.

A query that fits one or more domains passes the qualify gate with a domain tag. The tag is consequential: it determines which BERA measurements the quantify stage will require, which Pillar(s) the rationalize stage will route to, and which Trilogy volume the noise-filter pass will run through.

The qualify stage is conservative. When in doubt, it tags VEILED rather than passing through. This is by design. A query that the Instrument is uncertain how to admit is better held at the gate than passed through with false admission.

### Quantify — BERA Measurement Mapping

The quantify stage answers the question: *what BERA measurements does this query require?* BERA — the seven-dimensional rating system at the heart of the ERES measurement architecture — produces RATE outputs across R₁ through R₇. Different queries require different subsets of the seven dimensions. A query about energy infrastructure may require R₁ (resource availability) and R₅ (resonance with the surrounding ecology) but not R₃ (relational quality). A query about a child's educational progress may require R₃ and R₇ (reciprocity over time) but not R₁.

The quantify stage produces a **BERA mask** — a specification of which RATE dimensions the round-trip will compute and return. The mask is consequential downstream: it determines which fields of the DATABANK the Infomediary will query, which Trilogy volume's measurement layer the noise-filter pass will run through, and which Sim OUTPUT fields will populate.

The quantify stage is also conservative. A query whose BERA mask cannot be determined within reasonable bounds is returned VEILED with a suggestion that the Civigen specify scope. The Instrument does not guess.

### Rationalize — Pillar / Volume Routing

The rationalize stage answers the question: *which Pillar(s) and which Trilogy volume(s) frame the answer?* The four Constitutional Pillars under the GAIA canopy — HELP, USE, ENERGY, LAW — are the framing-tier categories. The three Trilogy volumes — *One-Good*, *Security-Clearance*, *Data-Integrity* — are the routing-tier categories. The rationalize stage assigns the query to one or more Pillars and one or more volumes.

A query that is purely energetic (e.g., a Civigen asking about household photovoltaic capacity under current SECUIR conditions) routes to ENERGY × *Data-Integrity* — the Pillar provides the constitutional commitment, the Trilogy volume provides the noise-filter and intelligence-shaper appropriate to the data class.

A query that is multi-Pillar (e.g., a Civigen asking what their CBGMODD-C station owes the next seven generations under current BERA readings, and what EarnedPath actions close the gap) routes to all four Pillars and at least two Trilogy volumes — *One-Good* for the moral-vectoring layer, *Data-Integrity* for the BERA layer.

The rationalize stage is the most consequential of the three intake operations because it determines the *shape* of the answer the Civigen will receive. A query that is rationalized to *One-Good* will receive an answer whose primary axis is moral-vectoring. A query that is rationalized to *Data-Integrity* will receive an answer whose primary axis is measurement. The Wielder's INTENT tier (Implicit, Explicit, Definitive) is consulted at this stage; Definitive INTENT overrides any default routing the Instrument would otherwise apply.

The rationalize stage is *not* conservative. It will route. A query that has passed qualify and quantify will be rationalized, even if the routing produces a multi-Pillar / multi-volume composite that is downstream-expensive to compose. The Trilogy's job is to absorb that downstream cost; the Infomediary's job is to route honestly.

## §1.2 Qualify Stage — Admissibility Gating

The query enters the Infomediary at the qualify stage. The qualify stage runs the SEPLTA admissibility frame:

- **Social** — Yes. The query concerns intergenerational obligation, which is a social-domain question.
- **Ecological** — Yes. The "next seven generations" framing engages ecological-time horizon, which routes through the GAIA canopy.
- **Political** — Partial. The query does not name a polity, but the CBGMODD-C station is a political position by structure.

- **Technical** — Yes. EarnedPath actions are technical-domain operators (the EarnedPath system is specified in the Manual).
- **Aesthetic** — Partial. The seven-generations framing has aesthetic weight (it is an inherited form), but the query does not engage aesthetic content directly.

Six domains, four full hits and two partial. The query is **admissible** under SEPLTA. The qualify stage tags the query with the four full domains (Social, Ecological, Legal, Technical) and notes the two partials (Political, Aesthetic) for downstream awareness.

The qualify stage's return: `QUALIFIED { domains: [S, E, L, T], partial: [P, A], VEILED: false }`.

## §1.3 Quantify Stage — BERA Measurement Mapping

The query proceeds to the quantify stage. The quantify stage produces the BERA mask by mapping the query's measurement requirements across the seven RATE dimensions (R₁ through R₇).

- **R₁ (resource availability)** — Required. "Owe" implies a resource accounting; what is owed must be measured against what is available.
- **R₂ (relational quality, narrowed sense)** — Required. Intergenerational obligation is a relational measure.
- **R₃ (resonance with ecological surround)** — Required. The seven-generations framing makes ecological resonance a primary axis.
- **R₄ (recurrence / cyclicality)** — Required. Generations are cyclic by structure.
- **R₅ (reciprocity)** — Required. Owing implies reciprocity.
- **R₆ (resilience under stress)** — Optional. The query does not specify a stress condition, but resilience under stress is implicit in any seven-generations framing.
- **R₇ (reciprocity over time)** — Required. Time-extended reciprocity is the core of the query.

Six dimensions required, one optional. The BERA mask is `[R₁, R₂, R₃, R₄, R₅, R₇] + R₆?` — read as: six dimensions hard, one dimension soft. The query is high-dimensional, which is consistent with its multi-Pillar character.

The quantify stage's return: `QUANTIFIED { mask: [R₁, R₂, R₃, R₄, R₅, R₇], soft: [R₆], VEILED: false }`.

## §1.4 Rationalize Stage — Pillar / Volume Routing

The query proceeds to the rationalize stage. The rationalize stage assigns the query to Pillars and Trilogy volumes.

**Pillar routing.** The query touches all four Constitutional Pillars:
- **HELP** — "Owes" is a HELP-tier obligation operator.
- **USE** — EarnedPath actions are USE-tier operators (the actions consume and direct resources).
- **ENERGY** — The seven-generations horizon is an ENERGY-tier question (the energetic ledger across that horizon).
- **LAW** — The owe-relation is LAW-tier (obligation under the constitutional commitments).

All four Pillars are engaged. The Pillar routing is **full canopy** — the query touches the full GAIA architecture rather than any single Pillar.

## §1.6 Trilogy Noise-Filter Pass

The Trilogy receives DETAIL from the DATABANK and runs the noise-filter pass across the three engaged volumes.

***One-Good* noise-filter.** Removes DETAIL fragments that fail BEST / SOUND / GOOD calibration. For the representative query, this removes content that frames intergenerational obligation in extractive or zero-sum terms — content that would advise the C station to externalize cost rather than carry it. The remaining fragments are morally vectored toward BEST / SOUND / GOOD.

***Security-Clearance* noise-filter.** Removes DETAIL fragments that the Wielder is not authorized to see at the current SCALULAR tier. For Class B Implicit-INTENT Wielders at SSSC baseline, this removes Definitive-INTENT content that requires Class A attestation, and removes Pillar-LAW content that requires SSLA tier authorization. ***Data-Integrity* noise-filter.** Removes DETAIL fragments that fail BERA RATE thresholds for the requested mask. For the representative query, this removes fragments whose RATE on R₃ (ecological resonance) falls below the threshold for ecological-time-horizon queries — i.e., fragments that frame the question without ecological grounding. The remaining fragments are measurement-validated.

The three noise-filter passes are run in parallel; their results are intersected. The intersection is the noise-filtered DETAIL.

## §1.7 BEST / SOUND / GOOD Moral-Vectoring Layer

The noise-filtered DETAIL passes through the BEST / SOUND / GOOD moral-vectoring layer. The vectoring layer is *not* a filter; it is a *tier assignment*. Each remaining DETAIL fragment is assigned to one of three tiers:

- **BEST** — fragments that exemplify BEST-tier outcomes (the strongest moral vectoring). Returned to the Civigen with BEST-tier prominence.
- **SOUND** — fragments that exemplify SOUND-tier outcomes (the structurally-sound middle, where most operational guidance lives). Returned to the Civigen with SOUND-tier prominence.
- **GOOD** — fragments that exemplify GOOD-tier outcomes (the achievable baseline). Returned to the Civigen with GOOD-tier prominence.

For the representative query, the BEST tier surfaces fragments on full-stewardship action — what the C station *should* do under the strongest reading of intergenerational obligation. The SOUND tier surfaces fragments on structurally-sound action — what the C station *can sustainably* do under current resource constraints. The GOOD tier surfaces fragments on baseline action — what the C station *must at minimum* do to satisfy the constitutional commitment.

The three tiers are returned together. The Civigen receives all three — not the BEST tier alone, not the GOOD tier alone — because moral-vectoring honesty requires that the achievable baseline, the sustainable middle, and the strongest reading all be visible. A round-trip that returns only BEST is utopian; a round-trip that returns only GOOD is defeatist; a round-trip that returns only SOUND is technocratic. The three together are honest.
