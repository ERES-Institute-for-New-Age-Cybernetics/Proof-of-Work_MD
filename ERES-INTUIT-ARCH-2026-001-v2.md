**Full designation:** Silent Energy Circular Universe Infinite Rotation.

**Definition:** SECUIR is the security and qualification layer. It determines which users, groups, and entities are cleared to interact with which parts of the ERES system at which depth.

**Base formulation:** Security is not access denial—it is access precision. SECUIR does not ask "should this person be kept out?" It asks "what is the right depth of engagement for this person given their qualifications?" The metaphor is clearance, not exclusion.

### EPIR-Q — Instrument Axis

**Definition:** EPIR-Q is the qualification filter. It is not a meter—it is a passband. Inputs that meet the qualification criteria pass through; inputs that do not are routed to remediation (NPR), not rejection.

**Input:** User identity and context from SECUIR.

**Output:** Qualified / remediation-routed / escalated. The Tri-Codex parameters define the passband width.

**Base formulation:** Qualification is a filter, not a judgment. The distinction matters: a judgment implies that unqualified persons are lesser. A filter implies that unqualified persons need a different pathway—NPR (Non-Punitive Remediation)—to reach qualification. No one is permanently disqualified.

### EarnedPath — Governance Axis

**Formula:** EP = CPM × WBS + PERT

**Definition:** EarnedPath is the trajectory-governance instrument. Where EPIR-Q asks "do you qualify *now*?", EarnedPath asks "how did you get here and where are you going?"

**Input:** User-group qualification status from EPIR-Q + historical trajectory data from GraceChain.

**Output:** Verified trajectory position — a proof-of-progress that maps the user's path through the qualification structure over time.

**Three components:**

- **CPM (Critical Path Method):** The shortest verified path from current state to goal state. Not the fastest path — the shortest path that satisfies all upstream governance rules.
- **WBS (Work Breakdown Structure):** The component tasks that compose the trajectory. Each task is a verifiable state transition logged on GraceChain.
- **PERT (Program Evaluation and Review Technique):** The probabilistic time estimate for trajectory completion. PERT acknowledges uncertainty — it does not pretend to predict exact completion dates, but provides optimistic, most likely, and pessimistic bounds.

**Base formulation:** EPIR-Q qualifies the person for this moment. EarnedPath qualifies the *trajectory* — proving that the person's path to qualification was genuine, not gamed or fabricated. In Paper 1's clinical context, Maria's BERA ARI moving from 42 to 58 over 14 months is an EarnedPath: CPM (the clinical pathway from diagnosis to weaning threshold), WBS (each treatment stage as a component task), PERT (probabilistic estimate of time-to-threshold based on population data from L3).

SECUIR × EPIR-Q / EarnedPath narrows scope from "what are the rules?" to "who, specifically, qualifies under those rules — and can they prove how they got here?" The instrument axis (EPIR-Q) handles the present-tense qualification. The governance axis (EarnedPath) handles the historical verification.
