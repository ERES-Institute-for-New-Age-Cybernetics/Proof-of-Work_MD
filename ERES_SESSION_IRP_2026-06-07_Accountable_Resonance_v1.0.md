## **Abstract**

This session documents the derivation of the Inverse Resonance Pyramid, a calibration framework mapping resonance scores (1–10) to descriptive word count, where higher resonance compresses into fewer words and lower resonance decompresses into more words. The pyramid reveals a collapse point at 0/10 where description ceases to refer and instead *performs* the failure it attempts to name. Beyond ten words, language generates unanchored noise rather than measurable signal. The session establishes that the limit is not word count but referential integrity — the capacity of language to map to structured states rather than embodying their absence. This finding has direct implications for ERES-MIEVM validation, BEST/SOUND/GOOD calibration, and the epistemology of AI-generated description.
