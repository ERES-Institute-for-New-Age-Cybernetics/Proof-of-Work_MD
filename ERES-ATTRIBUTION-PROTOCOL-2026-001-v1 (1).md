## 1. Purpose

This protocol specifies how external contributions to ERES specifications are credited. It exists to reconcile two requirements that otherwise conflict:

1. **Intellectual honesty.** Substantive contributions to ERES specifications — including peer review that changes the specification text — must be credited in the document record. Unacknowledged contribution is not acceptable.

2. **Verification discipline.** ERES specifications are published under CCAL v2.1 and are intended for standards-track use. Naming individuals as contributors creates claims about those individuals (role, affiliation, endorsement) that the ERES Institute cannot audit from correspondence alone.

The protocol resolves this by separating **contribution recording** (always required) from **named attribution** (contingent on reviewer consent and ERES Institute confirmation).

---

## 2. Contribution Recording

Every ERES specification that incorporates external contribution MUST include a Review and Contribution section (or equivalent) that records:

- A generic reviewer identifier (Reviewer A, Reviewer B, etc., scoped to the document).
- The correspondence channel and date range (for example: "LinkedIn correspondence, April 22–23, 2026").
- A specific, citable description of what the reviewer contributed, with section references in the document where the contribution appears.

Contribution recording does not require reviewer consent. It documents the historical fact that a contribution was made, without making identity or affiliation claims.

---

## 3. Named Attribution

Named attribution — replacing "Reviewer A" with an individual's name, affiliation, or role — requires **both** of the following:

1. **Reviewer request or consent.** The reviewer has affirmatively asked to be named, or has confirmed consent when asked.

2. **ERES Institute confirmation.** The ERES Institute has taken reasonable steps to confirm the named identity. Acceptable confirmation includes:
   - Direct correspondence from a verified email address.
   - A verifiable professional identifier (ORCID, institutional affiliation with a contact path).
   - A reviewer-provided identity document or credential that the ERES Institute can audit.
   - For LinkedIn correspondents specifically: confirmation via a channel outside the LinkedIn platform, because LinkedIn profile claims cannot be independently audited.

A reviewer's LinkedIn profile, LinkedIn-stated role, or LinkedIn-stated affiliation is **not** sufficient for named attribution on its own. LinkedIn provides channel-of-correspondence provenance only.

If both conditions are met, the specification may be revised to replace the generic reviewer reference with the named attribution, with a change note in the revision log.

---

## 4. Declined Attribution

A reviewer may at any time:

- Request that their contribution remain generic (no named attribution at any revision).
- Request that their contribution be removed entirely from the contribution record, if the contribution has not yet been incorporated into specification text.

Once a contribution has been incorporated into specification text, removing the contribution record is not generally possible without withdrawing the specification revision that depended on it. Generic reference remains available in all cases.

---

## 5. Collaboration vs. Review

This protocol distinguishes two roles:

- **Reviewer** — a contributor who raised questions, identified gaps, or provided critique that the ERES Institute incorporated into specification text. Reviewer contributions are recorded per Section 2 and named per Section 3.

- **Collaborator** — a contributor who co-authored specification text, performed validation work, or took on a named responsibility within ERES Institute operations. Collaborator attribution requires a written collaboration agreement beyond the scope of this protocol.

A reviewer may become a collaborator with mutual consent and a written agreement. Reviewer contribution alone does not establish collaborator status.

---

## 6. Peer Review Record

For each specification revision, the ERES Institute maintains a peer review record that includes:

- The generic reviewer identifier used in the document.
- The correspondence channel and date range.
- A copy or excerpt of the correspondence establishing the contribution.
- The specific section(s) of the specification where the contribution was incorporated.

This record is internal to the ERES Institute and is not published with the specification, but is retained so that any subsequent named attribution request can be matched to the specific contribution it concerns.

---

## 7. MIEVM Ensemble Contributions

Contributions from MIEVM ensemble nodes (Claude, Grok, DeepSeek, ChatGPT, or others) are a separate category. These are credited as:

- **Node-level attribution**: the model name and operator (for example: "Claude, Anthropic").
- **Session provenance**: the date and session context in which the contribution occurred.
- **Contribution description**: the specific input to the specification.

MIEVM node contributions do not require consent in the same way human-reviewer contributions do, because they are attributable to the model operator as a matter of API provenance rather than to an individual. However, the ERES Institute takes care not to misrepresent MIEVM contributions as endorsements by the operating companies.

---

## 8. Application

This protocol applies to all ERES specifications and working notes published on or after April 23, 2026. Prior specifications may be retroactively updated to align with this protocol at the discretion of the ERES Institute, preserving the substance of any existing attribution.
