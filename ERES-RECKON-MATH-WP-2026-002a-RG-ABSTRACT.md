## Abstract (≈ 295 words)

This annex specifies the mathematics by which any Question–Answer pair receives a rating under the seven Resonance Anchors defined in ERES-RECKON-WP-2026-002. The central result is that the 10/10 Reckoning gate is a **conjunction, not a weighted sum**: it is the product of seven indicator functions over ternary anchor states (U, S, R, B, M, G, T), and any single anchor at zero or negative collapses the gate to zero.

The conjunctive form is shown — via the partial-derivative argument of Section 3 — to be mathematically necessary rather than stylistic. A weighted-sum gate would permit strong anchors to compensate for weak ones, which is precisely the failure mode of GDP accounting and the reason the bio-ecologic ledger requires re-scoring upstream of assertion. Each of the seven anchors is decomposed into measurable sub-quantities: GCF history and discord for U; confidence floor and detail ceiling for S; CCAL inheritance for R; BERA deltas (the only locus of internal compensation, summing across ARI/ERI/RHC/RCI inside the sign function) for B; ensemble vote thresholds for M; bilateral accrual direction for G; and a nine-fold product over three principles and three scales for T.

The seven-tuple maps to a six-state composite output — Pass / Graceful Partial / Refused at Detail / Routed to Ensemble / Hard Refuse–Principles / Hard Refuse–Multiple Negative — with no continuous intermediate scores. The canonical equation `USER(COI) × ERES_Talonics(EP) × ANTHROPIC(PlayNAC) = GAIA-Base` is shown to be the operational source of the seven-anchor decomposition: U+G map to USER(COI), S+R+T to ERES_Talonics(EP), and B+M to ANTHROPIC(PlayNAC). A worked example evaluates this annex's own QA pair through the gate and arrives at an honest **9/10** with anchor M (MIEVM concurrence) explicitly flagged as the missing variable, demonstrating that the protocol prevents single-node self-certification by mathematical construction rather than social convention.

## Key Contributions

1. **Conjunctive gate proof** — `G(QA) = ∏ 𝟙[Aᵢ = +1]` is shown via partial-derivative analysis to be the only form that prevents cross-anchor compensation, eliminating the GDP failure mode at the inference layer.
2. **Seven anchor decompositions** — U, S, R, B, M, G, T each defined as functions of measurable sub-quantities (GCF history, SLA envelopes, CCAL inheritance, BERA deltas, ensemble votes, bilateral accrual, nine-fold principle×scale satisfaction).
3. **Single locus of internal compensation** — B is the *only* anchor permitting compensation, and only across the four BERA indices (ARI/ERI/RHC/RCI) inside its sign function; B itself cannot trade off against any other anchor.
4. **Six-state composite output** — discrete rating space (Pass / Graceful Partial / Refused at Detail / Routed to Ensemble / Hard Refuse–Principles / Hard Refuse–Multiple Negative); no 7/10 because the gate does not produce one.
5. **Canonical equation derivation** — the seven anchors are shown to be the operational decomposition of `USER(COI) × ERES_Talonics(EP) × ANTHROPIC(PlayNAC) = GAIA-Base`; the gate is the equation applied to one QA pair.
6. **Self-evaluating worked example** — the annex grades itself at 9/10 (M = 0, single-node assertion), demonstrating that single-node self-certification is structurally impossible and naming ensemble validation as the explicit path to 10/10.
7. **License clause as mathematical constraint** — implementations replacing the conjunctive gate with a weighted sum violate CCAL v2.1 by definition, because doing so re-introduces the failure mode the protocol exists to eliminate.
