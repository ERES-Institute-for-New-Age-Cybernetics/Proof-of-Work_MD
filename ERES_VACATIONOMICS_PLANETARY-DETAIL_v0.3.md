## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

*For the hospitality operator, technology partner, or governance researcher encountering ERES_VACATIONOMICS for the first time.*

Hospitality — the act of providing rest, shelter, and welcome to a traveler — is one of the oldest human institutions. It is also one of the most ecologically and culturally stressed. Mass tourism extracts from communities and ecosystems faster than they can regenerate. The systems built to manage bookings optimize for transaction volume, not planetary health.

ERES_VACATIONOMICS_PLANETARY-DETAIL proposes a different architecture. It treats rest and renewal as **cybernetic functions** — inputs to a Resonant Harmony Cycle that either increases or decreases the capacity of people, communities, and ecosystems to thrive. Every hospitality interaction either adds resonance to the system or subtracts it. The goal of governance is to make the net direction reliably positive, across a 1,000-year arc.

The framework rests on four operating principles: (1) **GAIA** — a planet-level awareness intelligence that observes consequences without surveilling individuals; (2) **SOMT** — a metadata tapestry that records what happened and what it cost, without recording what was said or felt; (3) **EarnedPath** — an access-weighting system where the right to visit ecologically sensitive places is earned through demonstrated stewardship, not purchased; and (4) **LAW as membrane** — information boundaries that let the right data flow at the right scale while keeping private journeys private.

The Agentic Hospitality Interface (AHI) is the practical entry point: a natural-language booking layer that handles the transaction at Step 5 agentic threshold, emits SOMT metadata automatically, applies EarnedPath gates invisibly, and leaves the guest with zero friction and zero surveillance.

This document is at v0.3 — a draft standard, pilot-ready, not yet SOUND-tier certified. The gap between here and SOUND-tier is engineering, not philosophy. The three remaining items are: a running SOMT prototype, one completed VLSA Test 92 (real-world booking), and a live EarnedPath scoring run. Everything else is specified.

## XVII. EARNEDPATH v0.1 FORMULA

**[HYPOTHESIS — functional form proposed; pending first scoring run for SOUND-tier validation]**

### Scoring Formula

```
EP_new = α · EP_old + β · outcome_signal + γ · community_attestation - δ · time_decay
```

Where:

| Parameter | Symbol | Default Value | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| Continuity weight | α | 0.70 | Weight of prior EarnedPath score in new score |
| Outcome weight | β | 0.20 | Weight of post-stay outcome_signal |
| Community weight | γ | 0.10 | Weight of community attestation (host community rating of guest stewardship) |
| Decay rate | δ | 0.02 per year of inactivity | Annual decay applied when no SOMT records emitted |

**Score range:** EP ∈ [0.00, 1.00]
**Initial score:** EP_new entrant = 0.30 (standard access; Tier 1 only)
**Floor:** EP cannot drop below 0.00
**Ceiling:** EP cannot exceed 1.00 through self-report alone; community_attestation required for scores above 0.60

### Tier Access Thresholds

| Tier | EP Threshold | Destination Type |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | EP ≥ 0.00 | Standard hospitality; no ecological sensitivity flag |
| Tier 2 | EP ≥ 0.40 | Sensitive ecosystem; UNESCO-class heritage; limited carrying capacity |
| Tier 3 | EP ≥ 0.70 | High ecological sensitivity; community-restricted access; irreplaceable biomes |

### Decay Function

If no SOMT records emitted for N years:
```
EP_decayed = EP_current · (1 - δ)^N
```

Example: EP = 0.65, inactive 3 years → EP_decayed = 0.65 × (0.98)³ = 0.65 × 0.941 = 0.612

Decay reflects that stewardship is a living practice, not a permanent credential.

- Host receives standard payment via existing rails (credit card / OTA flow unchanged)

- Escrow releases to host as community benefit contribution upon outcome_signal receipt
