## **Introduction**

### **The Problem That GSSG Solves**

Extraction economies depend on material scarcity. A substance is valuable because it is rare, difficult to refine, or controlled by a monopoly. That logic extends from fossil fuels to rare earth metals to construction materials. The result: wealth accumulates at extraction bottlenecks, and the biosphere pays the cost.

The Earth Change Economic Flip names the transition from extraction-funded to merit-funded economy, from consumption-rewarding to care-rewarding, from depletion-based wealth to regeneration-based wealth. For that transition to be real — not rhetorical — it requires at least one material substrate that cannot be extracted into scarcity.

GSSG is that substrate.

### **What GSSG Is**

Green Solar-Sand Glass is a composite material produced by pyrolysis of sand (SiO₂) and sugar (C₆H₁₂O₆). The process creates graphene-on-silica structures where:

* Sand provides the structural silica backbone  
* Sugar provides the carbon source for graphene lattice formation  
* Pyrolysis (controlled heating in oxygen-limited environment) drives carbonization

The resulting material is electrically conductive (via graphene), structurally robust (via silica), and self-healing (via the Sugar-Carbon Reservoir Hypothesis).
