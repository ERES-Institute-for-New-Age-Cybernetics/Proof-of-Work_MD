## **5.2 Pillar-Specific Curriculum Architecture**

The SSHP curriculum is organized into five competency domains that span the full lifecycle:

* Biological Literacy: How the human body functions, from cellular to systemic. Includes genetics, immunology, endocrinology, neuroscience at appropriate depth for each Horizon.

* Reproductive and Developmental Health: Conception through end-of-life. Age-appropriate modules on puberty, sexual health, pregnancy, childbirth, pediatric care, geriatric care, death and dying.

* Public Health Systems: How healthcare systems function, how to navigate them, how to advocate for system improvement. Includes epidemiology basics, health policy literacy, and community health organization.

### **5.2.2 LAW (SSLA) Curriculum**

The SSLA curriculum builds legal literacy from personal rights outward to international governance:

* Personal Rights and Responsibilities: The irreducible rights that attach to every human being. Consent mechanics, bodily autonomy, property rights, intellectual property basics, contract literacy.

* Dispute Resolution: Negotiation, mediation, arbitration, adjudication. The learner understands the full spectrum from informal conflict resolution to formal legal proceedings.

* Jurisdictional Awareness: How legal systems vary across jurisdictions. The learner can identify which legal framework governs any given situation and knows how to seek appropriate legal guidance.

* Civic Participation: How governance systems function at local, national, and international levels. Voting, advocacy, petition, legislative process, judicial review.

* Digital Rights and Data Sovereignty: Specific to the information age but designed for generational durability. The right to data ownership, algorithmic transparency, and consent-based identity (COI integration).

The SSPS curriculum addresses threat awareness and resilience across all scales:

* Cybersecurity and Digital Hygiene: Password management, encryption awareness, social engineering recognition, device security, network safety. Updated on the 3-year renewal cycle to reflect evolving threat landscapes.

* Environmental Hazard Awareness: Natural disaster preparedness (region-specific), climate adaptation, hazardous material recognition, food and water safety assessment.

* Community Resilience: Neighborhood-level emergency planning, mutual aid organization, infrastructure vulnerability assessment, communication during disruption.

* Civilizational Protection: Large-scale threat awareness including pandemic preparedness, supply chain resilience, energy infrastructure, and existential risk literacy. Designed for Horizon 2 and 3 learners.

### **5.2.4 SKILLS/TRADE (SSST) Curriculum**

The SSST curriculum follows the HPE ASCENT progression from the SPT Papers: Validation → Coordination → Transparency → Circularity → Dimensionality → Sentience:

* Foundational Making: Basic construction, basic electronics, basic programming, basic agriculture, basic food preparation. Every SSST-certified individual can build, grow, cook, and repair at a subsistence level.

* Digital Competency: From basic device operation through data literacy, software use, and introductory coding. Designed for technology-independence: the concepts survive specific platform obsolescence.

This eliminates the arbitrary deadline—the learner advances when ready, but the system predicts when “ready” is likely to occur.

The multiplication and addition in EP \= CPM × WBS \+ PERT is not merely mathematical notation—it encodes operational logic: the Critical Path multiplied across the Work Breakdown Structure generates the learning map; PERT is added as the temporal uncertainty envelope that makes the map realistic.
