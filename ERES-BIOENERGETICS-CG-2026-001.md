**Learning Objective:** The student articulates what bioenergetics is, distinguishes it from pseudoscience, and locates it within the ERES cybernetic framework C=R×P/M.

* Definition: Bioenergetics is the study of energy transformations and energy exchanges within and between living organisms and their environment. It encompasses biochemical thermodynamics (ATP synthesis, metabolic pathways), bioelectromagnetics (neural signaling, cardiac electrophysiology), and bioresonance (the measurable coherence patterns that emerge from healthy biological systems).

This demarcation separates ERES bioenergetics from unfalsifiable energy-healing claims while preserving space for traditional knowledge systems (TCM, Ayurveda) whose empirical observations predate their mechanistic explanations.

**Learning Objective:** The student names, defines, and distinguishes the four BERA Resonance Indices.

**RHC (Resonant Harmony Cycle):** Measures the temporal coherence of biological rhythms—circadian, ultradian, and infradian cycles. Grounded in chronobiology, HRV analysis, and sleep architecture studies. RHC captures whether the organism’s rhythms are harmonized or dysregulated.

**RCI (Resonant Continuity Index):** Co-developed with Jimmy D. Butzbach’s Continuity Theory. Measures the persistence and stability of resonant states over time.
