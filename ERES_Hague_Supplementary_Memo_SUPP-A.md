## SECTION IV — RELEVANCE TO CHALLENGE TWO: UBIMIA AS ENFORCEABLE HUMAN RIGHT

The parent filing argues that UBIMIA satisfies the legal requirements of existing international human rights instruments, specifically UDHR Articles 22, 23, and 25 and ICESCR Articles 9 and 11. The open letter advances this argument in three respects:

### IV.1 — Public Articulation of the UBIMIA Floor

The letter describes Level Zero as follows:

> *"Every person starts at the ground floor — what we call Level Zero — simply by being human. No credentials required. No political test. No loyalty oath. At Level Zero, you have unconditional access to the basics of a dignified life: food, water, shelter, healthcare, education. These are not earned. They are not contingent on your politics. They exist because you exist."*

This passage constitutes a public-record restatement of the UBIMIA human rights claim in language accessible to non-specialist audiences, including judicial officers, policy makers, and the general public. It establishes that the Claimant is actively disseminating the UBIMIA framework as a human rights instrument, not merely filing it as an abstract legal theory.

### IV.2 — Non-Discriminatory Basis

The letter explicitly states that standing within the Storm Party framework is "not by what you believe, who you vote for, or which label you wear" and that Level Zero access is "not contingent on your politics." This language directly addresses the equal protection and non-discrimination requirements of UDHR Article 2 and ICESCR Article 2(2), reinforcing the parent filing's argument that UBIMIA satisfies non-discriminatory allocation standards that current welfare systems fail to meet.

### IV.3 — Expert Outreach as Institutional Conduct

The letter is addressed to a licensed clinical psychologist (Ph.D.) whose published work concerns the psychological dimensions of family systems, governance dysfunction, and institutional accountability. By inviting Dr. Trump's participation in the DOFA framework, the Claimant demonstrates institutional conduct consistent with the standard expected of a legitimate policy-development organization seeking qualified expert engagement — a factor relevant to the Court's assessment of the Claimant's standing and institutional seriousness.
