**Status key:**
- 🟢 **CONFIRMED** — independently verified against real, citable evidence
- 🟡 **PARTIAL** — real evidence exists but doesn't cover the full claim as stated
- ⚪ **HYPOTHESIS** — plausible, internally consistent, not yet tested either way
- 🔴 **REJECTED (strong form)** — the claim as originally stated is contradicted by evidence; a narrower version may survive
- ⏳ **PENDING** — a specific test exists/is proposed but has not been run

## 1. GSSG (Green Solar-Sand Glass) — Material Claims

| # | Claim | Status | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1.1 | Sand (SiO₂) + sugar (C₆H₁₂O₆), pyrolyzed, yields a graphene-on-silica composite | 🟢 CONFIRMED | Published, peer-reviewed (2025): sucrose-coated silica/carbonate sand, heat-treated, forms layered graphene on the sand surface. Demonstrated application: heavy-metal adsorption (82.4–99% copper removal), **not** structural/PV/self-healing use. |
| 1.2 | Graphene-silicon material can generate usable photovoltaic power | 🟢 CONFIRMED | Real, established research area. Published efficiencies 13.7–18.8% via graphene-Si Schottky heterojunctions. Different fabrication route (CVD-grown/transferred graphene on crystalline Si wafers) than 1.1. |
| 1.3 | Graphene-Si is more radiation-tolerant than silicon alone | 🟢 CONFIRMED | 84% efficiency retention under heavy radiation fluence vs. 55% for plain silicon. Genuinely relevant to off-world/Earth-Change survival framing. |
| 1.4 | Self-healing via the Sugar-Carbon Reservoir Hypothesis | ⏳ PENDING | No literature precedent found either way. ERES-original mechanism. Falsification protocol exists (Test 1: Raman-spectroscopic D/G band tracking across 50+ fracture-anneal cycles) but has **not been run by an independent lab.** |
| 1.5 | A single GSSG batch (one pyrolysis process) is simultaneously structural + self-healing + photovoltaic | ⚪ HYPOTHESIS | The individual properties (1.1–1.3) are each real, achieved via *different* material architectures. No literature shows them unified in one composite. This is the actual novel ERES claim — open question, not yet falsified or confirmed. |

## 2. Data Center Thermal Management

| # | Claim | Status | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2.1 | "Enough energy lets us control temperature anywhere, heating or cooling" | 🔴 REJECTED (strong form) | True for heating (energy→heat is unconstrained). False for cooling — Second Law requires a heat sink regardless of energy availability; more energy without a sink increases net heat load. |
| 2.2 | Heat/capture/convert/store/cool/clean/repeat cycle reduces residual heat needing rejection | 🟡 PARTIAL | Capture, store, clean steps are real. "Convert" (ORC→electricity) is negligible at real DC waste-heat temperatures (~38°C, barely above ambient) — corrected out of the model. Real lever is direct reuse, not conversion. |
| 2.3 | Direct heat reuse fraction achievable at scale | 🟡 PARTIAL | Sourced range: 0–20% (current/regulatory baseline, Germany) up to 65–80% (Google Hamina, Cyberjaya, Costa Rica — all cases with **year-round, non-seasonal** thermal demand). Bottleneck is matched local demand, not technology. |
| 2.4 | S³C co-location (agriculture/desalination bundled with housing+data infra) reaches the 65-80% upper range | ⚪ HYPOTHESIS | Plausible mechanism (continuous demand vs. seasonal) is real and citable; no actual S³C site exists to measure against. |
