# **3\. Upstream Protocol — IDIPITIS**

## **3.1 What IDIPITIS Is**

IDIPITIS is the ERES intent-declaration protocol. Before any ERES action occurs — a governance decision, an information exchange, an industry rating, or a service fulfillment — IDIPITIS requires that the intent driving the action be explicitly declared, attested, and recorded. This converts what would otherwise be implicit motivation into explicit, auditable input.

## **3.2 Why It Sits Upstream**

IDIPITIS is not a licensed offering. It is a protocol-tier discipline that operates above all four offerings and constrains how they may be invoked. Every PlayNAC decision begins with IDIPITIS-declared intent. Every Infomediary exchange begins with IDIPITIS-declared intent. Every HowWay rating engagement begins with IDIPITIS-declared intent. Every SLA-Fulfillment bundle is assembled in response to IDIPITIS-declared intent.

This upstream position means IDIPITIS does not require separate licensing. Customers engaging any ERES offering operate under IDIPITIS by default, as a precondition of engagement.

## **3.3 Operational Benefit to Customers**

The customer-visible benefit of IDIPITIS is the audit-trail property it produces. Every ERES exchange carries a declared-intent record at its head, signed under EAAP, recoverable under EAAP-CRL revocation. This means a customer asked years later “why did your board decide X” or “why did you exchange this data with that counterparty” has a cryptographically signed answer rooted in the declared intent that initiated the exchange. This is a regulatory-defensibility property of growing market value.
