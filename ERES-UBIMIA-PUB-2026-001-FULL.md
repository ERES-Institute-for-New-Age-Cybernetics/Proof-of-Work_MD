# **2\. UBIMIA — The Two-Layer Economic Floor**

UBIMIA stands for Universal Basic Income \+ Meritocratic Income Augmentation. It is a single, integrated entitlement architecture with two functionally distinct layers.

## **2.1 Layer A — UBI (the Floor)**

Layer A is an unconditional, lifelong allowance that secures food, shelter, healthcare, and learning access for every recognized participant. Distribution occurs on Gracechain and is treated in ERES as a human right, not a transfer payment. The floor is non-conditional: receipt does not require prior contribution, productivity scoring, or behavioral compliance. The floor is non-revocable for core survival categories, even where new Meritcoin minting may be suspended for verified harm.

## **2.2 Layer B — MIA (the Augmentation)**

Layer B is a merit-tracked supplement earned through verified contribution to SELF, OTHERS, and FUTURE — the three Governing Principles of ERES (Don't hurt yourself; Don't hurt others; Build for generations to come). MIA accrues in Meritcoin, is computed from BERA — the four-index Bio-Ecological Resonance Architecture — and converts into purchasing power, governance weight, and access tier under SCALULAR (the universal service-tier pricing layer).

## **2.3 Why both layers must coexist**

A floor without augmentation collapses incentive: there is no recognition for excellence, no signal for contribution, and over time the floor itself becomes politically vulnerable because no one defends it as more than a transfer. An augmentation without a floor collapses dignity: those who cannot contribute — through age, illness, or circumstance — fall through. UBIMIA refuses the trade-off by routing each function through a ledger purpose-built for it. The same person, two ledgers, one account.

## **2.4 Defining the participant**

UBIMIA inherits a Participant Recognition Rule from the adopting jurisdiction (residency, citizenship, or community-sponsored enrollment for stateless persons). Children receive UBI from birth via guardian; adults retain UBI on cross-border migration for a defined transition period; emergency SCALULAR access is available to all human beings present in the jurisdiction under a compassion allowance, regardless of formal recognition. Recognition is the only P0 gap that genuinely blocks a pilot — and it is intentionally deferred to the adopting community, because the alternative is for ERES to dictate citizenship rules globally, which is precisely the centralized authority the architecture is designed to refuse.

# **3\. Gracechain — The Unconditional Ledger**

Gracechain is the settlement layer for grace: gifts, baseline entitlements, restorative transfers, and intergenerational endowments. It carries the UBI portion of UBIMIA and any value movement that should occur regardless of merit recognition.

## **3.1 Design properties**

* Non-conditional issuance. Receipt does not require prior contribution, productivity scoring, or behavioral compliance.

* Recipient-anchored identity. Identity is bound to the person rather than to wallet history; loss of credentials does not strand the floor. Recovery occurs via independent trustees (typically family, community organization, and legal aid) with a time-locked review window — never via state-issued ID alone.

* Restorative settlement. Gracechain is the channel through which Patriot Dividend distributions, FAVORS rebalancing flows, and IDIPITIS adjustments propagate.

* Gift-economy semantics. Transactions are recorded but not collateralized; Gracechain does not extend interest-bearing debt.

* Public, auditable aggregate flows; private contribution-level data. The ledger is verifiable in aggregate without exposing the specific intimate contributions (caregiving, mental health work, learning) that produced the resonance gain.

## **3.2 The coercion-resistance argument**

Every UBI program eventually faces the same pressure: how do you guarantee the floor without making the floor itself a coercion lever? Conditional UBI fails by design — once the floor can be revoked for non-compliance, it ceases to be a floor and becomes a behavior-control instrument. UBIMIA's answer is structural rather than rhetorical: by isolating unconditional issuance on its own ledger, the merit ledger cannot be weaponized to revoke survival. Restriction-without-revocation mechanisms exist for verified harm (the Meritcoin minting right can be paused; non-essential SCALULAR access can be judicially restricted), but the core survival floor — food, shelter, basic healthcare — is held outside the reach of those mechanisms.

# **4\. Meritcoin — Proof-of-Resonance**

Meritcoin is the contribution-recognized currency of MIA. It is mined by tuning, not by hashing. The minting condition is a measurable improvement in resonance, gated by the EAAP authorization stack.

## **4.1 The BERA indices**

BERA is the four-index Bio-Ecological Resonance Architecture against which contributions are measured:

| Index | Name | What it measures |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| ARI | Adaptive Resonance Index | System fit to changing conditions |
| ERI | Ethical Resonance Index | Alignment with the Eight Immutable Ethical Principles |
| RHC | Resonant Harmony Cycle | Sustained periodic coherence over time |
| RCI | Resonance Continuity Index | P\_Ω\_norm × ARI\_sys × VibConst (Butzbach Continuity Theory) |

## **4.2 What the slogan means**

The operative slogan is deliberate: ***“It's not mining — it's tuning.”*** Where Bitcoin externalizes cost as heat, Meritcoin internalizes value as harmony. A unit is minted when a contribution demonstrably moves a measured resonance vector forward without authorization-blocking degradation in the others. The condition cannot be faked by burning energy because it is not a computational puzzle — it is a measurement of a real-world system before and after a contribution.

## **4.3 What prevents fraud and gaming**

Five mechanisms compose to make Meritcoin manipulation-resistant:

* EAAP authorization: a conjunctive product across four independent factors (Anchor, RHC amplitude, EarnedPath viability, Future-Map convergence) — any single weak factor refuses authorization, so single-metric optimization fails.

* MIEVM concurrence: the Anchor factor cannot be self-certified. Multi-Instrument Ensemble Validation requires concurrence from at least three independent model lineages (e.g., Claude, ChatGPT, Grok, DeepSeek), with documented Erasure Completeness Ratio.

* Oracle-measured system change: contributions are validated by independent before/after measurement of the affected system, not by self-attestation alone. Sensor data, institutional records, peer attestation, and counter-attestation by recipients each carry differentiated quality weights.

* Diminishing returns and category balance: low-effort repeated contributions yield diminishing Meritcoin; a participant cannot reach the maximum SCALULAR discount from a single domain — contribution must be diversified across at least two domains.

* No fiat exchange: Meritcoin cannot be bought, sold, or swapped for fiat or for any other cryptocurrency. It is earned only through verified contribution and spent only on SCALULAR services and governance participation. There is no order book, no liquidity pool, no swap contract. This single rule eliminates the leak through which extractive capital re-enters the system.

# **5\. Conversion: Resonance-Priced Access**

The conversion of Meritcoin into SCALULAR service access is the most dangerous design surface in the architecture. If conversion is auctioned, wealthier participants outbid poorer ones and the system reverts to extraction. If conversion is rationed centrally, the rationing authority becomes a political target. Resonance-Priced Access (RPA) is the formal answer.

## **5.1 The formula**

**Cost(S) \= BaseCost(Tier) × Complexity(S) × 1 / (1 \+ ΔR\_contributor)**

Three terms, each constrained:

* BaseCost(Tier) is fixed annually by a deterministic formula (last year's minted Meritcoin divided by last year's SCALULAR demand, with a slight deflationary bias). No human sets prices; volatility is capped at 25 percent per year.

* Complexity(S) is a published, fixed multiplier for each service (e.g., 1.0 for a primary care visit, 4.0 for major surgery).

* ΔR\_contributor is the participant's normalized 12-month resonance contribution history — flow, not stock. Higher contribution lowers the price, with a floor at 50 percent of base.

## **5.2 What the formula prevents**

Meritcoin balance never appears in the cost formula. Hoarding does not lower prices. Spending does not raise them. Contribution history dictates discount; the rate at which contributions decay (12-month rolling window) means that last year's heroism does not permanently entitle this year's access. Cross-domain subsidy is a feature: an exceptional ecological contributor pays less for healthcare, which is exactly the inter-domain solidarity the framework is designed to encode.

## **5.3 A worked example**

Assume BaseCost(HEALTH) \= 10 Meritcoin per hour, Complexity(primary care) \= 1.0, Complexity(heart surgery) \= 4.0:

| Participant | ΔR\_contributor | Primary care | Heart surgery |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| New participant (no history) | 0.0 | 10.00 Mc | 40.00 Mc |
| Active contributor (top 20%) | 0.4 | 7.14 Mc | 28.57 Mc |
| Exceptional (top 2%) | 0.8 | 5.56 Mc | 22.22 Mc |
| Maximum (top 1%) | 1.0 | 5.00 Mc | 20.00 Mc |

No participant pays more than the new-participant rate, and no participant — however exceptional — receives free access. The 50 percent floor preserves the link between contribution and access that defines a meritocratic supplement; the cap on the new-participant rate ensures nobody is priced out of survival.

## **5.4 Governance weight**

Meritcoin also carries governance weight inside ERES-aligned institutions. Weight is computed as min(1.0, ΔR\_contributor × 2 \+ balance\_normalized × 0.5) — capped at 1.0, and dominated by contribution rather than balance. A pure hoarder with no contribution receives at most half a vote; an active contributor reaches the cap. This prevents Meritcoin accumulation from translating into political control, which is the failure mode of every wealth-weighted democracy.

# **6\. The Authorization Stack — EAAP, DSAP-PRE, RCR**

UBIMIA's economic mechanics — minting, conversion, settlement — sit on top of an authorization stack specified separately and treated as normative dependencies. This section summarizes the stack at a level adequate for publication; the authoritative specifications are EAAP v1.3-FINAL (ERES-EAAP-STD-2026-001-v1.3-FINAL) and Separatrix Math v2 (ERES-SEPARATRIX-MATH-2026-001-v2).

## **6.1 EAAP — Four-Factor Proof-of-Resonance**

The ERES Attestation and Authorization Protocol (EAAP) defines Proof-of-Resonance as the conjunctive product of four factors, each evaluated on the unit interval:

**PoR(t) \= A(t) × R(t) × P(t) × F(t)**

| Factor | Name | Measurement |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| A | Anchor Product | Product across seven Resonance Anchors. Factor M (MIEVM concurrence) cannot be self-certified. |
| R | RHC Amplitude | Normalized amplitude of the Resonant Harmony Cycle over the previous two cycles. Flatlined R returns zero. |
| P | EarnedPath Viability | Path accessibility: P \= 1 / (1 \+ viable-alternative-cost-penalty). |
| F | Future-Map Convergence | Divergence from the ERES 1,000-Year Future Map across five dimensions (ecological, social, technical, intergenerational, resilience). |

Conjunctive evaluation is normative. Weighted-sum compensation across factors is prohibited for authorization. A weak factor cannot be rescued by a strong one. This is the structural answer to the gaming attack: a contributor who games one metric still fails authorization on the others.

## **6.2 DSAP-PRE — Pre-Threshold Regime Detection**

The Decision Space Accessibility Protocol — Pre-Threshold (DSAP-PRE), specified in Separatrix Math v2, addresses the failure mode in which a system has crossed into irreversible path dependency while all four PoR factors still evaluate above threshold. Three proxies fire on regime transition:

* ρ\_RHC (self-integrity proxy) — second-derivative analysis of RHC amplitude; fires when acceleration toward flatline exceeds a sustained threshold.

* ρ\_PERT (relational integrity proxy) — PERT distribution topology; fires on modality collapse, variance collapse, or viable-alternative collapse.

* ρ\_hyst (temporal integrity proxy) — hysteresis counterfactual; fires when reversal perturbation fails to produce recovery within a defined recovery threshold.

If DSAP-PRE returns zero, authorization is refused with REFUSAL\_REGIME\_TRANSITION before PoR is even evaluated. A system in regime transition cannot be rescued by tighter qualification downstream — by definition, the alternatives have foreclosed. Each proxy maps to one of the three Governing Principles, which is not coincidence: the structure of the integrity check encodes the structure of the ethics.

## **6.3 RCR — Runtime Containment Requirement**

RCR is the non-bypassability property of the full stack. Every UBIMIA operation that consumes or transfers value — Meritcoin minting, Meritcoin-to-Credit conversion, Credit payment of Bills, Community Opportunity Investment flows, carryforward allocations — must produce a fresh authorization record bound to it. No execution path may complete without a fresh authorization check; no component may rely solely on previously issued or transported authorization state. Failure to validate fails closed at the execution boundary.

In pilot deployments (under 100 participants, under 10,000 transactions per year), RCR may be implemented as an audited log rather than on-chain records. Production deployments must use Gracechain-anchored RCR records.

# **7\. Annual Settlement: Credits, Bills, COI, GAIA Amortization**

Daily life inside UBIMIA does not require constant Meritcoin micro-transactions. Settlement occurs on an annual cycle managed by GAIA (the governance and amortization body), with rules deterministic enough to be auditable and discretionary enough to absorb shocks.

## **7.1 The cycle**

* Conversion. Meritcoin earned during the year converts to Credits at a GAIA-set rate (CR), published in advance and adjusted by formula, not discretion.

* Bills. Credits pay annual Bills across the four SCALULAR tiers — HEALTH (SSHP), LAW (SSLA), PROTECTION (SSPS), SKILLS\_TRADE (SSST).

* Carryforward. Of unused Credits, 60 percent carries forward to the same participant in the following year. The remaining 40 percent flows to the Community Opportunity Investment (COI) pool.

* Amortization. The COI pool is amortized across the whole GAIA Body as a reduction in BaseCost(T) for all participants in the following year. Today's surplus becomes tomorrow's price relief — for everyone, not just the contributor.

## **7.2 Why annual rather than continuous**

Annual settlement is the political concession that makes the system tractable. Continuous reconciliation imposes cognitive load on every participant and creates surface area for manipulation at every transaction. Annual settlement bounds the audit window, gives GAIA time to detect anomalies, and aligns naturally with most existing tax and benefit systems — easing the transition mechanics described in Section 10\.

# **8\. Fraud Resistance & Civil Liberties Safeguards**

The single most predictable attack against UBIMIA is the social-credit framing: that any merit-tracking system inevitably becomes a coercive scoring regime. The architecture answers the attack structurally — not rhetorically — through a constitutional constraint.

| The Constitutional Constraint No rating system is valid under UBIMIA unless it protects the individual from exploitation and protects the commons from deception. The system measures externalities and institutional performance, not private life. |
| :---- |

## **8.1 Fraud resistance and verification integrity**

Merit issuance and institutional ratings are supported by verifiable proof channels: third-party attestations, audited reporting, cryptographic receipts, and where appropriate, sensor-oracle or transactional evidence. Fraud prevention is reinforced by randomized audits, anomaly detection, and reputation-weighted validation, ensuring that repeated false claims reduce a participant's scoring authority rather than increasing it. Earned credit remains scarce, meaningful, and behaviorally grounded; Meritcoin does not become inflationary 'points.'

## **8.2 Anti-gaming incentive design**

UBIMIA applies diminishing-return curves, cross-category balance requirements, and long-term consistency weighting. Sustainability and contribution cannot be gamed through repetitive low-effort actions. High-impact work retains proportional recognition. UBIMIA is not a virtue-token generator; it is a systemic feedback mechanism for aligning incentives with measurable outcomes.

## **8.3 Civil liberties, privacy, and voluntary participation**

Participation in UBIMIA is voluntary and consent-based. Strict privacy protections apply: participants own their personal data, public auditability is bounded to aggregate resonance flows, and zero-knowledge proofs are the default for sensitive contribution categories (mental health, caregiving for ill family, intimate learning). A participant can prove a valid contribution without revealing what the contribution was. Selective disclosure is opt-in only, with optional weight bonuses for verification — never penalties for privacy.

## **8.4 Governance safeguards against political capture**

The UBIMIA standard is open, forkable, and publicly auditable. Governance is distributed across multiple independent institutions rather than centralized under any single authority. The definition of merit is constitutionally constrained — sustainability, transparency, harm reduction, measurable ecological-human welfare. No regime, corporation, or ideological bloc may redefine merit as obedience, censorship, or political loyalty. The right to fork is not theoretical; it is a primary defense against capture.

## **8.5 Legal and ethical accountability**

All scoring systems are subject to formal dispute, audit, and appeal. Individuals and institutions have the right to challenge ratings, correct errors, and contest fraudulent classifications. Truth in reporting is not merely a technical requirement; it is a legal and ethical mandate. The system remains a stabilizing instrument for civilization, not an instrument of control.

# **10\. Transition Model — Three Phases**

UBIMIA does not require a global flag-day. The transition map is three phases, each operationally complete on its own.

## **10.1 Phase 1 — Pilot (Years 1–2)**

* UBIMIA runs in parallel with existing fiat, welfare, and private healthcare/education systems.

* Meritcoin cannot be exchanged for fiat, as specified.

* Participants choose to receive UBI as Gracechain Credits or to continue existing benefits — not both.

* SCALULAR services are provided by existing institutions under contract, reimbursed in fiat from a transition fund.

## **10.2 Phase 2 — Integration (Years 3–5)**

* Existing welfare programs fold into Gracechain UBI as a single transfer.

* Legal-tender amendments allow Meritcoin acceptance for tax payments within the jurisdiction.

* SCALULAR providers must accept Credits; fiat conversion for external inputs is bridged through COI funding.

## **10.3 Phase 3 — Full Settlement (Year 6 onward)**

* No fiat welfare remains.

* Meritcoin and Credits are the only currencies for SCALULAR services.

* External trade — exports, imports — is handled by a separate GAIA-managed fiat reserve, not convertible from Meritcoin.

The phasing matters because most political failure modes of UBI proposals come from demanding too much, too fast. A two-year pilot in a cooperative or municipal setting is sufficient to validate the architecture; the integration and full-settlement phases are sequential consequences of pilot success, not preconditions for it.
