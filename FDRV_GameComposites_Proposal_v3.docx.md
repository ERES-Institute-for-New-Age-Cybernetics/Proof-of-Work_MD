# **Executive Summary**

The ERES Institute invites Game Composites into a milestone-driven design and prototyping partnership to develop the FDRV (Fly & Dive Rotor Vehicle) — a rotor-blade hybrid platform engineered for sustained flight to 25,000 ft, controlled submersion to 200 m depth, and 72-hour sealed life-support endurance for 1–2 occupants.

The FDRV's defining innovation is self-fueling kinetic energy: the Drive-Train Battery captures energy continuously from the vehicle's own moving parts — rotor rotation, deceleration, and mechanical motion — converting back-EMF into conditioned DC power stored in removable, scalable battery modules. No external fuel source. No solar dependency on the vehicle. The FDRV sustains itself through motion.

This is the physical anchor of ERES's Bio-Ecologic Economy (BEE) — a resilience framework where energy is generated by life-cycle activity, stored in modular units, and distributed as a commons. The FDRV prototype demonstrates that principle at the mobility scale. Game Composites builds the vessel that proves it.

## **Drive-Train Battery — Self-Fueling Architecture**

The FDRV generates its own power through the kinetics of operation. Rotor rotation, deceleration phases (autorotation descent, dive braking), and mechanical motion all produce electrical current via electromagnetic induction (back-EMF) from motor parts moving relative to stator windings. This raw output is conditioned by the Transition Stage (regenerative inverter \+ DC-DC conversion \+ control logic) into stable DC power.

Conditioned energy is stored in removable battery modules — physically extractable, independently rechargeable, and redeployable across the BEE network. Scalability is structural: more modules, more range, more capacity. The vehicle's energy economy scales with its operational profile, not with access to external fuel infrastructure.
