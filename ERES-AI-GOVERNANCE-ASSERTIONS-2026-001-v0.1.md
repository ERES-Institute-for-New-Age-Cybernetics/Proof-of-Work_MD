## Part II: The Merge — Concentric Governance Assertions

### 2.1 Structure

The merge proposes a four-tier concentric structure — Global, National, Regional, Local — with each tier governed by the same CBGMODD seven-seat logic, scored by the same BSG/STORM-PARTY method, and reporting upward to GAIA as the actuarial-and-biospheric audit authority. DOFA sits as the family/household-tier instrument feeding into the Local ring.

```
GLOBAL    — GAIA (actuarial authority + biosphere audit ground)
  ↑
NATIONAL  — CBGMODD seven-seat councils, SCALULAR-certified
  ↑
REGIONAL  — S^3C-SLA operational substrate (Smart-City nodes)
  ↑
LOCAL     — DOFA (household/family ledger) + Citizen seat baseline
```

Each ring uses the same scoring instrument (BSG/STORM-PARTY) so that a Local-tier AI governance assertion and a Global-tier one are commensurable — same rubric, different scope.

### 2.2 Governing Assertion

**Assertion:** An AI system or AI-governance policy is admissible at any tier only if it satisfies BSG (BEST: targets the optimal outcome; SOUND: methodology verifiable, claims correctly scoped; GOOD: traceable to the Three Governing Principles) **and** is seated within the CBGMODD structure appropriate to its tier **and** reports its resource/biosphere impact through GAIA.

This assertion is a direct application of existing, already-validated instruments — it does not require new correspondence-testing, because it is composition (using existing tools together) rather than claiming a new derived link between previously unconnected domains.
