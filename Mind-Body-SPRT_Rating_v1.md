## 0 · Operational Definitions Used in This Rating

The rating *itself* is the place where BEST / SOUND / GOOD become operational. Each criterion is bound to a specific question that scores must answer:

- **BEST** — *Does this claim describe an achievable optimum, and is the optimum the right one?* Scoring asks: would honoring this claim move the system toward the best end-state ERES is willing to commit to, or only toward a locally good one?
- **SOUND** — *Does this claim survive adversarial inspection of its structure, evidence, and definitions?* Scoring asks: if a hostile but competent reviewer pulled on this thread, would the paper hold? SOUND is the only criterion where MIEVM convergent mean ≥ 9.5/10 is the *floor*, not the ceiling.
- **GOOD** — *Does this claim, if accepted, reduce harm to SELF, OTHERS, and FUTURE?* Scoring asks: is the moral vector visible, intentional, and aligned with the Three Governing Principles?

Scoring scale: 0–10. Threshold for MIEVM Triangulator Gate closure: ≥ 9.5/10 on each cell. Below 9.0 = remediation required. Below 8.5 = structural revision required.

## II · Cell-by-Cell Justification

### Mind = SELF–$ELF

**× BEST (9.0)** — The end-state ("SELF leads, $ELF serves") is clearly articulated and morally compelling. The 1.0-point deduction reflects that the thesis does not yet specify the *enforcement mechanism* by which SELF leadership is preserved against $ELF capture beyond ARI measurement. Reviewers will ask: *measurement is diagnosis, not treatment — what is the treatment?* The Mind axis BEST score climbs to 9.5+ when PlayNAC + UBIMIA are shown as the corrective instruments, not just ARI.

**× SOUND (8.5)** — The oscillation framing (Mind as the *live oscillation* between SELF and $ELF rather than a hierarchy) is intellectually elegant and survives most adversarial framings. The 1.5-point deduction reflects rhetorical risk: $ELF-with-a-dollar-sign reads as wordplay on first encounter, and skeptical reviewers will discount the diagnosis because of the form. SOUND climbs when the paper supplies a non-pun rendering — e.g. *captured-self* or *exchange-self* — that the dollar-sign form merely *signals*.

**× GOOD (9.5)** — Strongest cell on the Mind axis. The claim that $ELF capture is *harm to OTHERS* (because the wealth-hoarding mechanism externalizes its costs) is morally unambiguous and aligned with the Second Governing Principle. No remediation required. The remaining 0.5 is held in reserve until the paper demonstrates that the harm is *measurable*, not merely declared.

### Body = REAL–GAIA

**× BEST (9.5)** — The strongest single cell in the entire matrix. Sustainability-as-floor (rather than as constraint) is the most philosophically distinctive move in the ERES corpus, and the thesis carries it cleanly. The 0.5-point deduction is reserved against the burden of demonstrating closed-loop sustainability *at scale*, which the thesis does not undertake (correctly — that is the body of the paper).

**× SOUND (9.0)** — REAL = (E·M·R)/(T·S) is mathematically clean, free of repeated-letter errors (the earlier ERS/ST formulation is correctly retired in-text), and grounded in EAAP hardware attestation. The 1.0-point deduction reflects that the attestation *evidence* is referenced but not exhibited in the thesis — that is Section IV's job. SOUND climbs to 9.5+ when Section IV exhibits actual TPM/TEE attestation traces.

**× GOOD (9.5)** — "The FUTURE is owed an unbroken substrate" is one of the cleanest moral commitments in the paper. Aligned with the Third Governing Principle. The 0.5 is held against the (unaddressed) question of *whose* FUTURE — the thesis does not yet engage cosmopolitan vs. lineal readings of generational stewardship.

### SPRT = APP–PARENT

**× BEST (8.5)** — The end-state ("the seed sprouts") is poetically apt and consistent with the SPRUTE/sprout metaphor, but operationally less crisp than the end-states for Mind and Body. The 1.5-point deduction reflects this asymmetry. BEST climbs when the paper specifies *what counts as sprouting* — i.e. what observable civilizational outcome would let an outside reader say "yes, SPRT performed."

**× SOUND (8.0)** — **The weakest cell in the matrix and the one most likely to drag the convergent mean below 9.0.** Three pressures:
1. The polysemy of SPRT (Spirit / Sprute / Spirit-Particle) is defensible as productive ambiguity but reads as evasion to skeptical reviewers. The thesis preempts the objection but does not yet *defeat* it.

**× GOOD (9.5)** — "The PARENT provides without hoarding" is morally unimpeachable. Aligned with all three Governing Principles simultaneously. The 0.5 is held against the (currently implicit) claim that the PARENT relation is *non-paternalistic* — reviewers will press on this.

## III · The HUOS = BEST-DEF_REL Identity Claim

The thesis treats this as an identity statement ("HUOS *is* BEST-DEF_REL") rather than an analogy. This is the right move for civilizational stakes, but it carries proof obligations:

- **BEST (8.5):** The end-state (a civilization OS that produces best-defined relations) is correctly aimed. Deduction reflects that the *only-triad-that-closes* claim is asserted without a uniqueness proof in the thesis. A uniqueness proof is hard but not impossible — it would consist of showing that any candidate triad failing the three Triune Math keys collapses into Mind–Body alone or into a different non-closing structure.
- **SOUND (8.0):** Identity claims are stronger than analogies but require stronger backing. The thesis would benefit from a one-paragraph derivation of HUOS as the *unique* operating system whose function is BEST-DEF_REL. Without this, reviewers will read the identity as definitional fiat.
- **GOOD (9.5):** The moral vector is preserved through the identity (HUOS inherits BEST/SOUND/GOOD by construction). No remediation.

**Recommendation:** treat HUOS = BEST-DEF_REL as an identity *to be earned* across Sections IV–VII rather than asserted in the thesis. The thesis should *announce* the identity as the paper's ambition; the body should *establish* it.

## VII · How This Rating Fleshes Out BEST / SOUND / GOOD

Three operational lessons emerge from running the frame on a live document:

1. **GOOD scores high almost everywhere.** This is *not* generosity — it reflects that ERES has already done the moral work upstream (the Three Governing Principles, the morally-vectored epistemology). GOOD is not where the paper will fail; it is the dimension on which the paper is structurally strong. The instrument should resist inflating GOOD into a generic excellence score.

2. **SOUND is where the work happens.** Every cell that fell below 9.0 fell on SOUND. This is the right behavior — SOUND is what MIEVM is *for*. BEST and GOOD give the paper its *aim*; SOUND is what makes the aim survive contact with reviewers. Future ratings should expect SOUND to be the binding constraint.

3. **BEST requires stating what would count as failure.** Several cells took 0.5–1.5 deductions on BEST not because the end-state was wrong but because the *recognition criterion* for the end-state was missing. BEST is operationalized by specifying what an outside observer would see if the optimum were reached.

The frame, in working form, is therefore: **GOOD is given by the corpus; SOUND is the binding constraint; BEST is operationalized by recognition criteria.** This is the BEST/SOUND/GOOD instrument, fleshed out by use.
