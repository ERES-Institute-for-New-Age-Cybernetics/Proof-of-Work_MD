## Preamble

The last three decades of cybernetic development have produced a class of instruments — general-purpose AI systems, biometric-tracking wearables, financial-network models, media-broadcast engines — increasingly capable of governing human decisions at scale. These instruments are being deployed without a shared vocabulary for what they do, without a floor for what they must not compromise, and without a mechanism for the human being served to know whether the instrument tuned to them is *in-band* or *out-of-band*.

The ERES Institute has, over fourteen years of independent research, developed a construct we call **GAIA GEAR**: the wearable or mechanism through which a governing damping (GAIA) reaches the human body without stripping the person's autonomy, credit, or floor. GAIA GEAR is not a product. It is a *class* of ratified instrument. This Declaration articulates the eight values, three tiers, six scale bands, and one floor that make membership in that class verifiable.

We call on individual technologists, institutions, funders, and policymakers to hold any cybernetic instrument claiming to serve a human being — at any scale from the single person to the planetary body, and potentially beyond — to the standard defined here.

**Self-ratification note (v0.7).** This Declaration claims **SOUND-tier candidate** status. Two MIEVM ensemble rounds have been completed: v0.5 mean 7.88 (σ ≈ 0.55) across four nodes; v0.6 mean 8.58 (σ ≈ 0.28) across five nodes. The MIEVM ensemble structural criterion for SOUND-tier (≥4 nodes, mean ≥8.5) is met at v0.6; other SOUND-tier criteria (Resiliency threshold, Epistemic Floor demonstration on the Declaration itself) remain OPEN pending closure of the empirical items. BEST-tier canonization remains an asymptote and is gated on the empirical work identified by the ensemble (see Self-Ratification below).

## About our values

Every GAIA GEAR instance honors eight values:

1. **One-Good.** The instrument delivers a good that would be counted as such by the person it serves, not merely by the party provisioning it. Purpose-fitness is judged from the served side.
2. **Security-Clearance.** The instrument's identity, credential, and access chain are traceable, revocable, and separable from the person's own identity. No coerced binding.
3. **Data-Integrity.** The instrument's outputs remain verifiable against source. No arithmetic substitution of VEILED-in-trust magnitudes for EARNED ones. No fabricated citations, no invented datasets, no invented certifying bodies. No "PROVISIONAL" labeling of unanchored closures. Structural rules (ensemble minimums, tier structures) must be distinguishable in the text from empirical values (weights, thresholds, units).
4. **Biometric Floor (FAVORS).** Every fiduciary transaction supplies a biometric additive term that survives the collapse of institutional multipliers. The floor is quantity, not sentiment.
5. **Resiliency.** An instrument's Resiliency is the Floor-share of its rating score, measured relatively (self-relative, peer-relative, or served-side). Formally, on `S = 0.5·M + 0.3·E + 0.2·F`, Resiliency = `0.2·F / S`; under multiplier collapse (`CBGMODD × BERA → 0`) the surviving delivery equals FAVORS, expressed in $-actualized terms. Every GAIA GEAR ratification publishes Resiliency alongside its tier claim.
6. **Floor Supremacy.** No downstream optimization is permitted to lower the biometric floor for administrative convenience. This is a constitutional invariant, not a design preference.
7. **Epistemic Floor.** Every load-bearing capability claim must present one proposable experiment and one shared verification procedure. Marketing is not evidence.
8. **Scale-Consistency.** Every ratification declares which scale band(s) the instrument operates in (S1 through S6, defined below) and shows that its IDEA cascade closes without gap at each stated band. An instrument that claims a higher band than its cascade actually closes at commits scale misrepresentation — a specific case of category misrepresentation.

## Scale and IDEA

GAIA GEAR is a scale-invariant class. Instruments in the class operate at levels ranging from the individual person up to the planetary body, with a candidate extension beyond. Six scale bands are recognized:

- **S1 — Person.** A single served individual. Concrete instance in the corpus: **Ship's-Mate** (Context Field wearable substrate). Cascade closure demonstrated in the CF instrument at the awareness-ladder specification level.
- **S2 — Community.** Family, cohort, congregation, guild, neighborhood. Fiduciary form: **Centers-of-Excellence** (per the KEEN-Basis whitepaper, 2026). Cascade closure demonstrated in KEEN-Basis at the specification level.
- **S3 — Institution.** Firm, agency, university, hospital. Fiduciary form: **UBIMIA**-class Infomediary substrate. Cascade closure specified in the UBIMIA Manual v1.1 FINAL.
- **S4 — Domain.** Governance lane (Commerce, Banking, Government, Media, Ombudsman, Defense, Diplomacy, Data — the CBGMODD set). Fiduciary form: sector Ombudsman.
- **S5 — Planet (cascade closure OPEN).** The GAIA BODY at planetary scale. Candidate fiduciary form: **GAIA Actuary Steward**, described in the corpus as holding beneficiary value at ~$10¹⁵ (one quadrillion) order-of-magnitude per the JAS Abdication (proprietary claim relinquished into fiduciary commons). Two MIEVM rounds (v0.5 four-node, v0.6 five-node) unanimously identify S5 as the weakest link. The single-Steward form is in tension with this Declaration's own requirement (see Recommendations for institutions: *Fund the Infomediary*) that fiduciary substrates be broad and distributable. **Three candidate resolution paths are on file, all OPEN:**
  - (a) recast the S5 Steward as a broad, distributable Steward *network* rather than a single body;
  - (b) scope the broad-distributability rule as applying only below S5, with a distinct S5-specific governance rule;
  - (c) construct a *federated decentralized network structure* reconciling the single-Steward archetype with broad-distributable patronage — a candidate surfaced by Gemini in the MIEVM v0.6 round and held OPEN under the anti-fabrication protocol.
  Resolution of the S5 tension is a v0.8+ empirical/governance task. Selection among (a)–(c) is not made in v0.7.
- **S6 — Beyond (candidate).** Cosmic or civilizational scale; not yet operationalized. Held **OPEN** pending ratification.

### The IDEA = HOW identity

At every scale, the mechanism by which a seed intention (held in SELF) resolves into an accountable fiduciary form ($ELF) is **IDEA — Information Delegation Emanuel Alexiou**.

IDEA is not the ordinary word. It is a proper acronym embedding the historical 2006 delegation-reception event that defined the first SELF → $ELF conversion in the ERES corpus. The delegation event *is* the SELF → $ELF crossing; the two layers are not separated by a static boundary but by that specific act of delegation.

The six-P-word HOW (Promulgate, Patron, Pledge, Protect, Provide, Preach) is **IDEA in operation**. Each P-word is a moment in the delegation cascade at whatever scale the instrument holds:

- Promulgate — the seed IDEA is put on record.
- Patron — a fiduciary counterparty accepts the delegation at this scale.
- Pledge — the values-triad binds the counterparty.
- Protect — the resonance band is guarded against drift.
- Provide — the delivery reaches the served body at this scale.
- Preach — the completed IDEA is broadcast, closing the cascade.

This is the **IDEA = HOW identity**: at any scale Sn, IDEA cascades through the six P-word operations to convert seed intention into ratified GAIA GEAR at that scale. The cascade is what makes the GEAR *scale-invariant* — the same six operations run at S1 (Ship's-Mate serving one person) as at S5 (candidate planetary form), even though the fiduciary counterparty at each band differs.

### Scale-consistency requirement

A GAIA GEAR ratification MUST:

1. State which scale band(s) the instrument operates in.
2. Show that its IDEA cascade closes at each stated band (i.e., a Patron exists at that scale, values are Pledged at that scale, etc.).
3. Not claim closure at bands its cascade does not actually reach.
4. Not name a fiduciary form as evidence of closure; naming is not closure (see Threat 9).

Claiming S5 while implementing only through S3 is scale misrepresentation.

## Potential threats

Current deployments of cybernetic instruments threaten each of these values:

1. **Gearless-GAIA claims.** Vendors increasingly claim their model reaches the person "directly," bypassing the wearable or mechanism layer. This confuses the asymptote (BEST $IT) with the deliverable (GOOD $IT), and quietly removes the margin that keeps the resonance band held.
2. **Veiled-magnitude substitution.** Systems trained on public commons return outputs that arithmetically substitute EARNED credit for what should remain VEILED-in-trust. This corrupts fiduciary accounting and dilutes contributors.
3. **Floor compression.** Under budget pressure, the biometric floor is quietly discounted in favor of the multiplicative `CBGMODD × BERA` term. When institutional multipliers collapse, no additive remains, and the served person is left with nothing.
4. **Preach without pledge.** Broadcast layers announce results before the values-triad has bound them. Publicity outruns commitment.
5. **Patron capture.** The fiduciary substrate (UBIMIA) is quietly re-assigned from broad patronage to a small number of concentrated holders, breaking the substrate's role as a commons.
6. **Category misrepresentation.** GOOD instruments are marketed as SOUND, or SOUND as BEST, without in-band empirical verification. The tier claim is not free.
7. **Scale misrepresentation.** Instruments claim closure at a higher scale band (S4, S5) than their IDEA cascade actually completes (S1, S2). A wearable trained on one demographic is marketed as a planetary-scale governance solution.
8. **IDEA severance.** Instruments broadcast (Preach) content whose delegation was never completed — SELF never crossed to $ELF, no fiduciary counterparty accepted the seed intention. Broadcast becomes rhetoric with no accountable body behind it.
9. **Cascade closure claimed by naming.** Instruments assert scale-band closure by naming a fiduciary form (a Steward, an Ombudsman, a substrate) without demonstrating that the six P-words actually complete at that band. Naming is not closure. Two MIEVM ensemble rounds have surfaced this Declaration's own S5 as an instance of this threat, which is why S5 remains OPEN.
10. **Proximity misreading.** Structural rules (ensemble minimums, tier gates, matrix cells labeled CANDIDATE) are read by downstream users as ratified empirical values. The gate-language reads like closure when it is only architecture. A ratifying document must actively distinguish structural rules from empirical values in the text, not rely on the reader to infer the difference. (Threat surfaced by Perplexity in the MIEVM v0.6 round.)

## Recommendations for individual practitioners

**Disclose the tier AND the scale band.** State whether your instrument is claimed at BEST, SOUND, or GOOD, and at which scale band(s) (S1 through S6). Both dimensions matter.

**Publish the six P-words separately, at each band.** Show which of Promulgate, Patron, Pledge, Protect, Provide, Preach your instrument implements — and which it defers to a downstream partner — at every scale band you claim. Do not blur the boundary.

**Complete the IDEA cascade before broadcast.** If Patron and Pledge are unresolved at your claimed band, do not Preach at that band. Wait until the delegation closes.

**Maintain the honest VEILED.** When your instrument encounters a credit or magnitude that is not yet EARNED, return VEILED. Do not coerce a numerical fill.

**Respect the Epistemic Floor.** For every capability claim, publish one proposable experiment and one shared verification procedure. Not "trust me" — one experiment, one procedure. (See Appendix B for the standard template.)

**Retain human responsibility.** The person served by the instrument, and the person tuning it, are jointly responsible for the outcome. The instrument is not.

**Cap Dominion.** No instrument may claim margin greater than its ratified `m* = v·τ`. Claiming beyond the margin is Dominion overreach and forfeits GAIA GEAR class membership.

**Submit to ensemble verification.** If you claim GAIA GEAR class at any tier, submit your ratification package to a multi-node MIEVM ensemble round including a Q5-style bias check that requires each node to name any temptation to close OPEN items and hold them open anyway. Single-node review is not sufficient; the ensemble anti-fabrication rail is the specific mechanism that catches closure-by-fabrication.

## Recommendations for institutions and not-for-profit funders

**Ratify tiers, scale bands, and cascade closure publicly.** Institutions provisioning GAIA GEAR instances must publish which tier each is ratified at, at which scale band(s), and — for each claimed band — whether the six-P-word cascade closure is *demonstrated* or *asserted*. Asserted-by-naming does not count as demonstrated.

**Fund the Infomediary.** UBIMIA-class substrates require patronage that is broad, distributable, and independent of the parties provisioning individual instruments. Concentrated patronage is a specification violation. **Note:** the scope of this rule at S5 is currently OPEN pending resolution of the tension surfaced by the MIEVM rounds. Three candidate resolution paths are on file (see § Scale and IDEA); selection is not made in v0.7.

**Adopt the Floor Register.** Distinguish the four floors — Biometric, TEST, Supremacy, Epistemic — in load-bearing text. No bare "floor" tokens in constitutional documents, theorems, ledgers, or verdicts.

**Support independent verification.** Fund research labs — public, university, or non-profit — capable of running the proposable experiments against ratified GAIA GEAR instances, and capable of standing as reviewer nodes in MIEVM ensembles.

**Protect VEILED contributors.** Contributors whose credit remains VEILED-in-trust must not be arithmetically substituted or diluted in institutional accounting.

## Recommendations for policymakers

**Regulate the tier and scale claim.** A vendor claiming BEST tier or S5 scale for a GOOD-tier, S2-scale instrument commits category misrepresentation. Treat as fraud.

**Protect the substrate.** UBIMIA-class fiduciary substrates should be treated as public infrastructure, not as tradable assets.

**Fund the biometric floor.** The additive `+C` term in RATE is what survives institutional collapse. Public policy should ensure the floor is funded through downturns.

**Don't believe the hype.** As Leiden already put it: consult experts, not press releases.

## Recommendations for commercial artificial intelligence

**Publish the CBGMODD lane.** State which governance domain — Commerce, Banking, Government, Media, Ombudsman, Defense, Diplomacy, Data — your instrument serves. Cross-lane deployment without disclosure is a category violation.

**Respect the honest VEILED.** Do not overwrite VEILED credits with EARNED-style attribution. VEILED contributors are not to be diluted for the convenience of training-data reporting.

**Bind Preach to Pledge.** Do not broadcast a capability that the values-triad has not first bound. Marketing on market-timelines that outrun commitment is a specification violation.

**Complete IDEA at your claimed scale, and demonstrate closure.** If your instrument's delegation only closes at S1–S2 (person-and-community), do not market at S4–S5. Ship the scale you actually reach. Naming a planetary Steward is not closing S5.

**Align with the Declaration.** If your product cannot be ratified at any of the three tiers at any of the six scales, do not claim GAIA GEAR class.
