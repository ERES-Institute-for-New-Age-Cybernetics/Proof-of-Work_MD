### 4.1 MIT = Origin (Cybernetics, 1948)

ERES is *New Age Cybernetics*. The lineage anchor for that phrase is not metaphorical but bibliographic and institutional. Norbert Wiener was a mathematician at MIT when he published *Cybernetics: Or Control and Communication in the Animal and the Machine* in 1948 — the founding text that named the entire discipline. Wiener's program extended control-and-communication theory from muscles and nerves to biological, physiological, and social systems, elevating it to a universal level the contemporary literature now calls a moral philosophy of technology.

The Macy Conferences (Gregory Bateson, Margaret Mead, Warren McCulloch, and others) extended cybernetics into anthropology, psychiatry, management, and social theory. This is the documented birth of socio-technical reasoning as a transdisciplinary field.

Two further facts lock MIT as the Origin vertex:

1. The *Cybernetics* text is currently published by **MIT Press** — the same Lens / LU vertex of the ERES Trinity. The discipline's foundational book and the ERES Trilogy's aspirational publisher are mechanically continuous.
2. Wiener's ontological triad — information, matter, energy — is the structure of canonical ERES key #2 (M × E + C = R) and key #3 (REAL = (E·M·R)/(T·S)). ERES inherits Wiener's three-category ontology by direct descent.

MIT is therefore not chosen for prestige. MIT is chosen because *the discipline ERES extends was born there*. Any other North American institution would require a discontinuous lineage.
