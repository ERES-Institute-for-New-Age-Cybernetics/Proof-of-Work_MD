# ERES Institute Corpus and “God as an Inverted Triangle” Module — SEPLTA Assessment & Implementation Report

## Technical, Legal, and Social Appraisal with Recommendations for Pilot Implementation of a 7‑Level Resonance Training Module within the New Age Cybernetics (NAC) Ecosystem

Keywords  
 ERES Institute, New Age Cybernetics, PlayNAC, GraceChain, Meritcoin, SOMT, MIEVM, SEPLTA, SCALULAR, EarnedPath (EP), BERA, Talonics, PlayNAC‑KERNEL, Proof‑of‑Work\_MD, NAC\_Images, ZIP, CCAL

Abstract  
 This report evaluates the ERES Institute corpus (GitHub organization and primary document set) using a SEPLTA (Social, Economic, Political, Legal, Technical, Administrative) lens and assesses the readiness, risks, and implementation pathway for a training module derived from the corpus: “Knowing God as an Inverted Triangle (7 levels).” The corpus demonstrates strong technical foundations and documentation maturity suitable for pilot implementations; primary risks are political legitimacy, social acceptance, and cross‑jurisdiction legal validation. The training module aligns conceptually with New Age Cybernetics and can be implemented safely within PlayNAC given explicit governance, assessment rubrics, and careful pilot design. The report provides prioritized recommendations, an implementation roadmap, governance safeguards, and references to the ERES GitHub repositories where canonical artifacts should be stored.

Body of Evidence

1. Corpus appraisal (SEPLTA summary)  
* Composite SEPLTA score: 7.3 / 10 (evidence: extensive documentation, multi-repo structure, archival strategy, legal framing).  
* Social (7.5): The project contains extensive outreach and documentation materials; however, terminology (resonance, bioenergetics) is niche and may elicit skepticism. Evidence: README overview, outreach links (ResearchGate, Medium), PlayNAC narrative. Recommendation: publish plain-language executive summaries and replication datasets in Proof-of-Work\_MD.  
* Economic (7.0): Meritcoin/GraceChain and UBIMIA tokenomic concepts are described; however, they require robust simulation and staged issuance. Evidence: Gracechain-Meritcoin repo listing, GERP Vacationomics formula. Recommendation: run agent-based simulations in PlayNAC-KERNEL test suite and publish stress tests.  
* Political (6.5): Political risk acknowledged; SCALULAR and Hague submission concepts present but untested. Evidence: README mentions international proposals and legal strategy. Recommendation: establish policy advisory group and pilot in neutral jurisdictions.  
* Legal (7.5): CCAL licensing and SCALULAR design show advanced legal thinking; cross-border enforcement untested. Evidence: license and citation sections. Recommendation: run a legal tabletop and one test arbitration with consenting partners.  
* Technical (8.0): Strongest domain — PlayNAC-KERNEL, Proof-of-Work\_MD, SOMT ideas, and model descriptions exist. Evidence: multiple repos, Volyanskyi transposition references, MIEVM concept, BERA sensor notes. Recommendation: provide runnable prototypes (PlayNAC demo, GraceChain testnet).  
* Administrative (7.5): Protocol-as-administration concept present; automations documented but need operational runbooks and early ops testing. Evidence: Governance and SOP references in Support-Documentation. Recommendation: produce operations runbook and small ops pilot team.  
2. Key technical artifacts and readiness  
* PlayNAC-KERNEL: core research and PDF archive; primary location for quest engine, assessment design, and kernel specs. Readiness: high for research; needs runnable microservices and test harnesses.  
* Proof-of-Work\_MD: canonical Markdown documentation — the canonical source for formal models, formulas (EarnedPath EP), and metadata design (SOMT). Readiness: high for reference; CI pipelines to ensure dual-format builds recommended.  
* Gracechain-Meritcoin: tokenomics and anchor concepts; readiness: conceptual/proof-of-work stage; requires smart-contract prototypes and permissioned testnet.  
* NAC\_Images & ZIP: visualizations and packaged artifacts useful for transparency and distribution; readiness: available but must be integrated into release pipelines.  
3. Module appraisal: “God as an Inverted Triangle (7 levels)”  
* Conceptual coherence (8.0): The inverted-triangle, 7-level mapping maps well to NAC constructs (resonance, attractors, layered systems). Evidence: Assistant transcript mapping levels to ARI/ERI and PlayNAC concepts; corpus supports layered, gamified learning (PlayNAC).  
* Implementability (7.0): Feasible to implement as a PlayNAC micro-course with EP micro-credentials and SOMT metadata capture. Evidence: EP formula, PlayNAC-KERNEL quest engine references, MIEVM for assessment validation. Gaps: precise mapping of behavioral/sensor signals to levels, assessment rubrics, non-clinical safeguards.  
* Social/ethical risk (6.0): Potential for perceived elitism, religious/cultural offense, and misuse as psychological diagnosis if not carefully framed. Evidence: corpus emphasis on GERP/ethical constraints and CCAL licensing. Mitigation: explicit consent flows, opt-out options, culturally adaptive wording, GERP policy-as-code enforcement.  
4. Governance, legal, and archival considerations  
* Licensing: CARE Commons Attribution License v2.1 (CCAL) — supports open use but restricts exploitative or closed derivatives.  
* Archival: IPFS and OpenTimestamps referenced; GraceChain as ledger anchor proposed. Evidence: README archive section and recommended strategies.  
* SCALULAR: proposed legal instrument for perpetual portability and dispute resolution; requires testing and precedents.  
5. Risks and mitigations  
* Political/regulatory: Use research-first pilots, IRB and legal consultation, and targeted jurisdiction selection.  
* Economic/perverse incentives: Simulations, issuance caps, timelock disbursements, and strict transparency of reward rules.  
* Technical: Model drift controls, versioning (SOMT \+ GraceChain anchors), MIEVM validator audits, redundancy and attestation for sensors.

Conclusions

* The ERES corpus is well-documented and technically mature enough to begin small, controlled pilots (PlayNAC prototype \+ GraceChain anchors \+ SOMT schema). The highest-priority gaps are producing runnable reference implementations, legal tabletop testing of SCALULAR, and community-facing communications to reduce social skepticism.  
* The “God as an Inverted Triangle” module aligns well with NAC theory and can be implemented safely within PlayNAC as a non-clinical, consented training module if strong governance (GERP, policy-as-code), clear assessment rubrics, cultural localization, and MIEVM validation are applied.  
* Recommended immediate actions (priority order):  
  1. Produce a runnable PlayNAC prototype (minimal ingest → model → EP → credentialing) and publish in PlayNAC-KERNEL and Proof-of-Work\_MD with CI artifacts.  
  2. Establish a legal tabletop for SCALULAR; run a test arbitration to create precedent and guidance for pilots.  
  3. Prepare plain-language materials and pilot recruitment packages; run three low-risk pilots to validate the inverted-triangle module and system integration.
