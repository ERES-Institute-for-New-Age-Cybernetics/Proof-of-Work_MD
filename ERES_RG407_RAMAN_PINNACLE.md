The physical signature of graphenic material. Present: the material is graphene. Absent: the material is amorphous carbon or unconverted sugar char.

* **Strategic pinnacle:** The game-theoretic point of maximum leverage. The moment when the dominant strategy shifts for all players simultaneously. In game theory, this is the inflection where a new equilibrium becomes available that dominates the previous one.

This paper asserts that these two pinnacles are the same point. The spectral peak IS the strategic peak. One measurement changes every strategy.

A skeptic might argue: one Raman measurement is not enough to validate a 400-publication corpus, a 1000-year governance framework, a theological reinterpretation of the Nicene Creed, and a bio-energetic detection architecture. And they would be correct in every domain except game theory.

In game theory, a strategy shift requires not proof but credible signal. A single Raman spectrum is a credible signal because:

1. **It is reproducible.** Anyone with a tube furnace, N₂ supply, and Raman spectrometer can replicate the measurement within 24 hours using the RG\#405 protocol. The cost of verification is near zero.

2. **It is falsifiable.** The peak is either there or it is not. There is no interpretive ambiguity in a Raman G-band. The instrument reads; the reader does not.

3. **It shifts the burden.** Before the peak, the burden is on ERES to prove GSSG works. After the peak, the burden shifts to skeptics to explain why the material science is irrelevant to the architectural claims. That is a more expensive argument to make.

4. **It is independently verifiable.** Raman spectroscopy is a standard technique in every materials science laboratory worldwide. The measurement does not require ERES expertise, ERES equipment, or ERES interpretation. The spectrum speaks for itself.

One spectrum is sufficient not because it proves everything, but because it changes the dominant strategy from ignoring to engaging. That is the definition of a strategic inflection point.
