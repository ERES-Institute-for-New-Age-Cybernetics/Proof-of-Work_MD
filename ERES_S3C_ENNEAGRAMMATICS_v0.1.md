## I. DEFINITIONS

### S³C — Solid-State Smart-City **[DESIGN-PRINCIPLE]**

**Solid-State** = the governance condition of a community system that has achieved internal coherence — no membrane collapse, no silent exits, no EarnedPath capture, no SOMT mono-culture. Analogous to a material that has crystallized from liquid uncertainty into stable molecular structure. The cybernetic solid-state is not rigidity — it is *reliable conductivity*. A solid-state community transmits resonance without significant loss across its internal membranes.

**Smart-City** = in ERES, this term is explicitly *not* the surveillance-and-sensor definition used by the tech industry. An ERES Smart-City is a **COMMUNITY-layer App-Parent that has achieved GAIA-awareness of its own consequence** — that knows its ecological load delta, cultural vitality index, EarnedPath distribution, and host community benefit ratio, and governs from that awareness rather than from surveillance of individual citizens.

**S³ — The Three Dimensions of Solid-State Condition:**

| Dimension | Definition | Failure Mode |
|---|---|---|
| **Self-Governing** | App-Parent sovereignty at COMMUNITY layer is active and intact | Sovereignty captured by NATION-layer or external actor |
| **Self-Monitoring** | SOMT active; GAIA aggregate live; four KPIs tracked | GAIA-blind governance; no consequence awareness |
| **Self-Correcting** | EarnedPath and LAW membrane operating as feedback loops | Membrane collapse; EarnedPath capture; static rules replacing dynamic resonance |

A community achieves Solid-State when all three S dimensions are simultaneously active and mutually reinforcing. Loss of any one dimension destabilizes the others.

**Relationship to SCALULAR:** A Solid-State Smart-City has passed through all four SCALULAR pillars — HEALTH (SSHP), LAW (SSLA), PROTECTION (SSPS), SKILLS_TRADE (SSST) — and achieved Tier 1 SSSC (Community Stewardship). SSSC is the entry condition for S³C. S³C is what SSSC looks like when it is operating in a smart-city context with active SOMT and GAIA-awareness.

**Relationship to Vacationomics:** The S³C is the ideal host community in the Vacationomics framework — the COMMUNITY-layer App-Parent that can receive travelers, monitor carrying capacity, emit SOMT metadata, and return community attestations to GAIA without surveillance of individual guests. Every Tier 2 and Tier 3 AHI destination should be an S³C node.

**Relationship to Prison-to-NAC:** The S³C is the ideal reintegration community — the COMMUNITY-layer App-Parent that receives returning citizens, monitors its own carrying capacity for reintegration, builds EarnedPath rather than surveilling criminal history, and returns outcome signals to GAIA. A community cannot support Prison-to-NAC reintegration without S³C governance architecture.

## II. S³C ENNEAGRAMMATICS — THE NINE POSITIONS

**[DESIGN-PRINCIPLE / HYPOTHESIS]**

Enneagrammatics in ERES is not a psychological personality typology. It is a **community governance posture map** — nine stable configurations of a Smart-City's three S³ dimensions, describing where a community currently sits in its development toward full Solid-State condition.

The nine positions are arranged on a 3×3 matrix of Self-Governing (SG) × Self-Monitoring (SM) development, with Self-Correcting (SC) as the emergent third axis that activates when SG and SM reach sufficient maturity.

### The Nine Positions

| Position | SG Status | SM Status | SC Status | Community Character |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1 — Latent** | Weak | Weak | Absent | Pre-governance; community exists but App-Parent is not yet sovereign; no SOMT; no feedback loops |
| **2 — Monitoring** | Weak | Developing | Absent | Surveillance-dominant; community tracks data but has no sovereign decision authority; classic "smart city" failure mode |
| **3 — Aware** | Weak | Active | Nascent | GAIA-aware but not yet self-governing; community knows its consequence but cannot act on it without external authority |
| **4 — Sovereign** | Developing | Weak | Absent | App-Parent emerging but not yet data-aware; can decide but cannot see consequences clearly |
| **5 — Architect** | Developing | Developing | Nascent | Both capacities developing simultaneously; feedback loops beginning to form; most common position for communities actively reforming governance |
| **6 — Governed** | Developing | Active | Developing | Strong consequence awareness feeding into developing sovereignty; EarnedPath beginning to operate as access mechanism |
| **7 — Assertive** | Active | Weak | Developing | Strong App-Parent sovereignty but consequence-blind; can govern but governs without adequate feedback — risk of authoritarian drift |
| **8 — Integrated** | Active | Active | Developing | Both capacities active; SC consolidating; community is near-Solid-State but feedback loops not yet fully self-correcting |
| **9 — Solid-State** | Active | Active | Active | Full S³C condition; all three dimensions mutually reinforcing; SCALULAR Tier 1 achieved; GAIA-awareness live; EarnedPath and LAW membrane as active feedback systems |

### Position Transitions

**[HYPOTHESIS]** Communities move through positions non-linearly. The most common failure transitions:

- **1 → 2 (Surveillance Trap):** Community acquires monitoring technology before developing App-Parent sovereignty. SM grows while SG stagnates. Results in surveillance-dominant governance without accountability.
- **4 → 7 (Authority Trap):** Community develops strong App-Parent sovereignty without SOMT or GAIA-awareness. Can govern, but governs blind. Risk of EarnedPath capture by governing class.
- **8 → 5 (Regression):** Near-Solid-State community loses one dimension (e.g., SOMT infrastructure fails, or App-Parent sovereignty is captured). Falls back to Architect position, requiring rebuilding.
- **5 → 9 (Solid-State Transition):** The aspirational path. Both capacities developing simultaneously, feedback loops consolidating, until SC activates and all three dimensions lock into mutual reinforcement.

### Position Descriptions (Extended)

**Position 1 — Latent**
The community has identity and geography but no governance architecture worthy of the name Smart-City. Basic services may exist. App-Parent sovereignty is theoretical, not operational. No SOMT. No EarnedPath. LAW = punishment, not membrane. Entry point for ERES engagement: SCALULAR audit and SSSC foundation-setting.

**Position 2 — Monitoring**
The surveillance-dominant Smart-City. Sensors everywhere. Data flowing to platforms. Dashboard showing real-time metrics. But: the data flows to external parties, not to a sovereign Community App-Parent. Citizens are objects of monitoring, not participants in governance. This is where most current "smart city" initiatives sit. ERES REMEDIATION for Position 2: redirect data flows to Community App-Parent; install k-anonymity floors; convert surveillance metrics to SOMT consequence metadata.

**Position 3 — Aware**
The community has developed genuine GAIA-awareness — it knows its ecological load delta, cultural vitality index, and community benefit ratio. But App-Parent sovereignty is weak: decisions still require external authority (NATION-layer approval, platform operator consent, investor sign-off). The community can see but cannot yet act. ERES REMEDIATION for Position 3: sovereignty-building through DOFA (Department of Family Amity) framework and NATION-layer membrane negotiation.

**Position 4 — Sovereign**
The community has App-Parent sovereignty — it can make binding decisions within its dimension. But it lacks SOMT infrastructure and GAIA-awareness. Governs from tradition, intuition, or political consensus rather than consequence data. Strong sense of identity and self-determination; weak feedback loops. Many rural and indigenous communities occupy Position 4. ERES value: their sovereignty is the foundation — SOMT and GAIA-awareness can be added without displacing it.

**Position 5 — Architect**
Both capacities developing. This is the active reform position — the community is consciously building toward Solid-State. Leadership understands both the sovereignty requirement and the consequence-awareness requirement. ERES engagement is most productive here: the community knows what it needs and is building toward it. VLSA Test 92/93 candidates are likely Position 5 communities.

**Position 6 — Governed**
Strong SOMT and GAIA-awareness actively informing a developing App-Parent. The community knows more than it can currently act on — a productive tension that drives sovereignty development. EarnedPath beginning to operate as access mechanism. This is the position where Vacationomics Tier 2/3 access gating becomes meaningful: the community can actually evaluate whether a guest's EarnedPath score matches its carrying capacity, because it knows what its carrying capacity is.

**Position 7 — Assertive**
Strong App-Parent sovereignty operating without adequate SOMT or GAIA-awareness. The governing authority is real and legitimate but consequence-blind. Risk of EarnedPath capture: the governing class distributes access based on internal criteria invisible to GAIA. Many traditionally governed communities with strong cultural identity but limited data infrastructure sit here. ERES REMEDIATION for Position 7: SOMT installation framed as consequence visibility, not external monitoring — the community's sovereignty is respected; SOMT serves it, not external actors.

**Position 8 — Integrated**
Near-Solid-State. Both SG and SM active; SC developing. The community is governing and monitoring simultaneously, and the feedback loops are beginning to work. This is the most fragile position — close enough to Solid-State that regression feels like failure, but SC not yet locked in. ERES engagement at Position 8: stabilization protocols, BERA SOUND-tier audit preparation, and VLSA Test certification.

**Position 9 — Solid-State**
Full S³C condition. Self-Governing, Self-Monitoring, Self-Correcting all active and mutually reinforcing. SCALULAR Tier 1 (SSSC) achieved. GAIA-awareness live. EarnedPath and LAW membrane operating as dynamic feedback systems. The community can host Vacationomics Tier 3 destinations, support Prison-to-NAC reintegration, and emit SOMT records that contribute meaningfully to GAIA planetary aggregates. The target condition for all ERES community engagement.

## IV. S³C RELATIVITY AND EARNEDPATH

**[DESIGN-PRINCIPLE]** S³C Relativity is the principle that EarnedPath is always relative to the governance system the actor is operating within.

A person with high EarnedPath in a Position 2 (Monitoring) community has demonstrated compliance with a surveillance system — not resonance with a Solid-State Smart-City. Their EarnedPath does not automatically transfer to a Position 9 community.

A person with high EarnedPath in a Position 9 community has demonstrated stewardship within a self-governing, self-monitoring, self-correcting system — the highest-validity EarnedPath credential.

**[HYPOTHESIS]** EarnedPath transfer rules across S³C positions:

| From Position | To Position | Transfer Rate | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| 9 → 9 | Full (1.0×) | Same governance architecture; same validity |
| 9 → 8 | High (0.85×) | Near-equivalent governance; minor discount |
| 9 → 5 | Moderate (0.65×) | Different developmental stage; skills transfer but context differs |
| 9 → 2 | Low (0.30×) | Surveillance-dominant system cannot evaluate resonance-based EarnedPath |
| 2 → 9 | Very Low (0.15×) | Compliance-based EarnedPath has limited resonance validity |
| Any → 1 | Floor (0.15 — human dignity) | Human dignity floor applies regardless of origin position |

This is the ERES answer to "do criminal records from one jurisdiction transfer to another?" — not as a legal question but as a governance question. The record's validity depends on the governance architecture in which it was generated.
