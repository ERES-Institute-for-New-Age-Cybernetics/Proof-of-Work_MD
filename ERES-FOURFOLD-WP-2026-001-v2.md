# **4\. Role-Holders and Valence Asymmetry**

Roles are filled by living incumbents. The relevant distinction across the four corners is valence, not publicness. Three corners (Source Relative, Kin Friendship, Spiritual Leader) carry geopolitical valence by their nature — their incumbents are recognizable across state, market, and tradition boundaries. The Needed Adversary corner carries structural-cybernetic valence within the ERES architecture itself — the role is public to the corpus and the governance architecture, not to the geopolitical chyron.

This asymmetry is structurally correct: three outward-facing horsemen (define, redefine, transition) and one inward-facing horseman (construct). The HOW corner's work is joinery, not podium; an incumbent carrying geopolitical valence on par with the other three would be miscast in this corner.

# **5\. Role vs. Incumbent — Rotation and Gate-Failure Protocol**

The framework binds the role, not the person. Three consequences follow:

* An incumbent may fail the BEST/SOUND/GOOD gate. Failure does not collapse the corner; it triggers rotation. The role remains; the incumbent vacates.

* Succession does not require revision of this paper. A new incumbent assumes the role under the same gate.

* Vacancy is permitted. A corner may be unfilled for an interval without collapsing the framework, provided the remaining three corners hold and the triangulator (JAS) records the vacancy in the Register of Collective Indices.

Rotation certification follows the Trifecta Protocol (ONE-GOOD / SECURITY-CLEARANCE / DATA-INTEGRITY gates). A failed-gate rotation is a public-record event under CCAL.
