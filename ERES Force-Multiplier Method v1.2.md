## **2\. v1.2: ERES Nomenclature ‚Äî Subject-First Grammar**

ERES has been programmed to understand a base Nomenclature of symbolic shorthand.

The single most important rule in v1.2:

The word preceding `@ # ^ * % ( )` is the SUBJECT.  
Subject first. Symbols after. ERES does the rest.

Without a Subject, symbols float and queries become ambiguous. With a Subject, the entire Nomenclature becomes a predictable, parsable, executable grammar.

### **The Complete Grammar**

`[SUBJECT] @domain #topic ^method *principle %query re: regarding | Key: constraint | Note: caveat | ie. clarification | Commentary: why | e.g. example`

Order is fixed: Subject first. Then modifiers in any order *after* the Subject ‚Äî the symbols tell ERES what each chunk means.

### **The Symbol Set (with Subject)**

| Order | Element | Symbol | Meaning | Example |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| 1 | SUBJECT | *(no symbol ‚Äî it's the word itself)* | The thing being queried | `wire_transfer`, `approval_log`, `sensor`, `system`, `concept` |
| 2 | Domain | `@` | Which ERES subsystem? | `@EAAP`, `@BERA`, `@SCALULAR`, `@UBIMIA`, `@GraceChain` |
| 3 | Topic | `#` | Focus keyword | `#attestation`, `#authorization` |
| 4 | Method | `^` | How to do it | `^crypto`, `^measurement`, `^simulation` |
| 5 | Principle | `*` | Governing priority | `*generations`, `*noharm`, `*self` |
| 6 | Query type | `%` | IS / OF / ARE | `%IS` (definition), `%OF` (composition), `%ARE` (relationship) |
| 7 | Regarding | `re:` | The specific subject matter | `re: wire >50k` |
| 8 | Constraint | `Key:` | What cannot change | `Key: byte_normative` |
| 9 | Caveat | `Note:` | Contextual limit | `Note: no blockchain` |
| 10 | Clarification | `ie.` | Precise meaning | `ie. court_admissible` |
| 11 | Why it matters | `Commentary:` | Business or operational rationale | `Commentary: audit_ready` |
| 12 | Example | `e.g.` | Concrete instance | `e.g. TPM 2.0` |

### **The Commentary Key (Detailed)**

| Marker | Meaning | Example |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| `Note:` | Ancillary observation or context | `Note: No blockchain` |
| `Key:` | Critical constraint or requirement | `Key: Byte-normative output` |
| `Commentary:` | Explanation of why this matters | `Commentary: Court-admissible for 10+ years` |
| `e.g.` | Example follows | `e.g. TPM 2.0 or equivalent` |
| `re:` | Regarding / with respect to | `re: wire transfers > $50k` |
| `ie.` | That is (clarification) | `ie. no raw biometric storage` |

## **3\. Two Complete Examples**

ERES supports both esoteric/heritage queries (for researchers, philosophers, and system architects) and production/audit queries (for enterprise CAIOs, legal, and compliance).

### **Example A: Original Esoteric Query (Preserved & Parsed)**

User input:

\*understand @knowledge hashtag\#ERES ^Instrument *Faith %Query (IS: OF\_ARE), Note: Our Key: Heritage Commentary: Define, e.g. EVOL re: LOVE ie. EFIL*

ERES v1.2 parsing:

| Element | Token | Meaning |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| SUBJECT | *(implicit ‚Äî "understand" is a verb; ERES infers Subject \=* `concept` *or* `system`*)* | The thing being queried is the act of understanding itself |
| `@` | `@knowledge` | Domain \= ERES knowledge architecture |
| `#` | `#ERES` | Topic \= the ERES corpus itself |
| `^` | `^Instrument` | Method \= instrumental reasoning (tool-based inquiry) |
| `*` | `*Faith` | Principle \= faith-based (trust, belief, axiom as starting point) |
| `%` | `%Query (IS: OF_ARE)` | Query type \= definitional (`IS`) or compositional (`OF`) or relational (`ARE`) |
| `Note:` | `Note: Our` | Caveat \= belonging to "us" (collective ERES/community ownership) |
| `Key:` | `Key: Heritage` | Constraint \= heritage-bound (must respect prior ERES foundations) |
| `Commentary:` | `Commentary: Define` | Why \= to define something |
| `e.g.` | `e.g. EVOL` | Example \= EVOL (love spelled backward ‚Äî suggests inversion, reflection, or duality) |
| `re:` | `re: LOVE` | Regarding \= love as a concept |
| `ie.` | `ie. EFIL` | Clarification \= EFIL (life spelled backward ‚Äî suggests inversion of life) |

ERES interpretation (internal):  
*"Define, through instrumental reasoning within ERES knowledge architecture, the relationship between LOVE and LIFE (EVOL/EFIL as inverse pairs), respecting heritage constraints, with faith as the governing principle. Output a definitional or compositional answer."*

Valid for: Philosophy of cybernetics, ERES internal architecture review, symbolic reasoning.

## **4\. The Three-Step Force-Multiplier Method (Two Versions)**

### **Version A: Full Template (For new users or complex governance boards)**

| Component | Question to answer |
| :---- | :---- |
| CONTEXT | Which ERES domain? (EAAP / BERA / SCALULAR / UBIMIA / Governance) |
| PROBLEM | The enterprise pain point, stated as a gap |
| CONSTRAINT | What cannot change? |
| OUTPUT\_REQUIREMENT | What must the answer include? |
| PRIORITY | Which of the Three Governing Principles applies? |

### **Version B: Subject-First Nomenclature Shorthand (v1.2)**

`[SUBJECT] @[DOMAIN] #[TOPIC] ^[METHOD] *[PRINCIPLE] %[IS/OF/ARE] re: [REGARDING] | Key: [CONSTRAINT] | Note: [CAVEAT] | ie. [CLARIFICATION] | Commentary: [WHY] | e.g. [EXAMPLE]`

Rule: The pipe (`|`) separates clauses. You can omit any optional field.  
Minimum viable query: `[SUBJECT] @DOMAIN %QUERYTYPE`

Examples of minimum viable queries:

| Query | Meaning |
| :---- | :---- |
| `approval_log @EAAP %IS` | Define an approval\_log in EAAP |
| `workflow @BERA %OF` | What composes a workflow in BERA? |
| `sensor @GraceChain %ARE re: attestation` | How are sensors related to attestation in GraceChain? |
